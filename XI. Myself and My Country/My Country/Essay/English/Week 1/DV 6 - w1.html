"Me, Myself, and My Beloved Country" is a 24-day challenge to know and love both myself and my country. It is a personal growth journey designed to help us discover our true identity—as God’s masterpiece and as proud Filipinos. This program aims to deepen our understanding of who we are, nurture a strong sense of national identity, and awaken within us a genuine love and care for our homeland. Through daily reflection, we are trained to grow spiritually, emotionally, and patriotically—for the glory of God and the good of our nation.

José Rizal:
Flame of Truth, Model of a Nation’s Rise


His face is etched on our currency. Monuments honoring him stand across the Philippines and around the world. To many, he is the familiar figure in Luneta—the National Hero, the Great Malay. Yet often, in placing him so high upon a pedestal, we forget to ask: Why was he named our national hero? And why does the world recognize the greatness of his legacy?

A Hero Shaped by History

Dr. José Rizal’s place as the national hero was no accident. To some, it was a gift of history; to others, a divine appointment. In a time when the nation cried out for a voice that would awaken its spirit, Rizal stood as the embodiment of the ideal Filipino—intelligent, dignified, God-fearing, and deeply devoted to his country.

Greatness That Echoed Across the Globe

Rizal was not just a hero—he was a genius. A true polymath, he excelled in medicine, science, literature, history, and the arts. He spoke more than 20 languages, studied in Europe’s most prestigious universities, and wrote with piercing intellect and profound insight. But never did he use his brilliance for self-glory. Instead, he wielded it to awaken a slumbering nation.

His novels, Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, were more than stories—they sparked a revolution. His essays and poems stirred the hearts of the oppressed and awakened the conscience of the powerful. He proved that the pen could shake the very foundations of empire.

His Contribution at Home and Abroad

In his homeland, Rizal championed education, justice, and unity. He founded La Liga Filipina to bring Filipinos together for peaceful reform. He never took up arms, yet he armed the minds of the youth with ideas that ignited freedom.

Abroad, he acted as a quiet diplomat—building alliances, earning respect, and reshaping the world’s view of the Filipino. Even in exile, he remained relentless: founding schools, treating the sick, and writing tirelessly for liberty. His life was a life offered in service.

A Character Refined Through Global Journey

Despite admiration in many lands, Rizal remained humble. He walked with purpose—not for applause, but for the nation’s well-being. In Madrid, Paris, Tokyo, and beyond, he carried with him the honor of the Filipino race. He was never swayed by luxury or recognition. His joy was in service, his strength rooted in truth and love for his country.

A Flame of Truth, A Call for Our Time

Why was José Rizal the way he was?

Because his heart was rooted in two great forces: reverence for God and unshakable love for his country. He sought neither power nor prestige, but the freedom of his people from oppression and ignorance.

And now, history calls us.

Admiration is not enough. Remembrance is not enough. Laying flowers at his monument means nothing if we remain silent amidst injustice, poverty, and division.

If Rizal were alive today, he would not be silent. And so, we too must not remain silent.

His greatness is a summons.
His life is a challenge.
His death is a cry for national awakening.

You Are the Next Flame

Will you live in the shadow of a hero, or will you light the flame of change?
Will you remain seated, or will you rise to defend the dignity of the nation?

Be the spark of true awakening—a flame of light in the darkness, a fire of courage in the face of fear, a torch of unity in a time of division.

This is the age of new Rizals—not to replace him, but to continue his mission.

Let us rise. Let us fight. Let us serve—until the Philippines becomes a nation that is just, honorable, and pleasing to God.

His life was the seed.
We are meant to be the harvest.
And you are the fire that must rekindle the passion in the heart of the Filipino nation.
