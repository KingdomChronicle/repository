"Me, Myself, and My Beloved Country" is a 24-day challenge to know and love both myself and my country. It is a personal growth journey designed to help us discover our true identity—as God’s masterpiece and as proud Filipinos. This program aims to deepen our understanding of who we are, nurture a strong sense of national identity, and awaken within us a genuine love and care for our homeland. Through daily reflection, we are trained to grow spiritually, emotionally, and patriotically—for the glory of God and the good of our nation.

José Rizal:
National Hero of the Filipino Nation


We see his face engraved on our peso. There are monuments around the world dedicated to this man. To us, he’s that familiar face in Luneta. The National Hero. The Great Malayan. Perhaps he has been put too much on the pedestal that we shove our deepest questions as to why the world recognizes his greatness.

But it was no accident that Dr. José Rizal was chosen as the national hero of the Philippines. For many, it was a gift of history—and for others, a divine appointment. At a time when the nation desperately needed an awakened voice and a true example of honor and courage, Rizal rose as a mirror of the ideal Filipino: intelligent, dignified, God-fearing, and selfless in his love for country.

His Greatness That Echoed Across the World

Rizal’s brilliance was undeniable. He was a polymath—well-versed in medicine, science, literature, history, and the arts. He mastered over 22 languages, studied in the most respected institutions of Europe, and wrote with profound insight and piercing intellect. Yet, his knowledge was never for self-glory. He used his gifts to awaken the soul of a sleeping nation.

His novels Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo did not simply tell stories—they ignited a movement. His essays and poems stirred the hearts of the oppressed and embarrassed the pride of the colonial elite. He proved that the might of the pen could shake the foundation of empires.

His greatness did not go unnoticed. In Germany, his scientific acumen was admired by the scholarly community. In France and Spain, he engaged with literary circles and political reformists. In Japan and Hong Kong, he represented the dignity of the Filipino people. Wherever he went, Rizal brought honor to his race. He was not just a traveler—he was a bearer of truth and a bridge between nations.

His Contributions at Home and Abroad

In his homeland, Rizal fought for education, justice, and the right to national identity. He founded La Liga Filipina with a dream of uniting Filipinos in peaceful reform. He did not raise a sword, but his words armed the minds of a new generation.

Abroad, he acted as a quiet diplomat—gathering support, building friendships, and shaping how the world saw the Filipino. Even in exile, he remained productive: opening a school, healing the sick, and writing tirelessly for the cause of freedom. His life was an offering laid at the feet of his people.

His Character While Traveling the World

Despite being admired across continents, Rizal remained grounded. He was a man of humility and dignity—always respectful of other cultures, always faithful to his roots. He walked with poise and purpose, not for applause, but for the greater good.

In Madrid, he studied with excellence. In Paris, he debated with intellect. In Tokyo, he found beauty and inspiration. But in all these places, he never lost sight of home. His journals and letters reveal a man who was reflective, disciplined, wise, and wholly committed to truth.

He did not seek luxury or recognition. His joy was in service. His strength came from principle. And his mission was clear: to liberate his people not only from foreign rule but from ignorance, fear, and division.

Conclusion: The Sacred Flame That Lit a Nation

Why was José Rizal the way he was? Because his heart was anchored in two powerful forces: a holy fear of God and an unquenchable love for the Filipino nation.

Rizal’s greatness did not spring from status, wealth, or ambition—but from reverence. He feared God, and so he feared no man. He loved his country, and so he gave his life freely for its freedom and future.

And this same greatness lies within every Filipino. It begins to awaken when we walk humbly before God, and burn with love for our nation. When we rise above selfishness, fear, and indifference—then we take our place in the same sacred legacy Rizal left behind.

Our nation does not lack heroes. It lacks hearts set on fire.

So let us be that flame.

May you, too, become a spark of true awakening—a matchstick that lights the fire of hope, truth, and courage in this generation. May your life, like Rizal’s, ignite the kind of change that no bullet can silence.

Let us rise as he did.
Let us love as he did.
And let us serve—not merely with words, but with bold and righteous action—until the Philippines becomes the nation of justice, dignity, and destiny that Dr. José Rizal saw with the eyes of a prophet.

His life was the seed.
May ours be the harvest.
And may you be the match that starts the true rising.
