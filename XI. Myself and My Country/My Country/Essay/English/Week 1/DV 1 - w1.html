Me, Myself and My Beloved Country

The Daily Devotional and Reflection Program:
"Me, Myself, and My Beloved Country" is a 24-day challenge to know and love both myself and my country. It is a personal growth journey designed to help us discover our true identity—as God’s masterpiece and as proud Filipinos. This program aims to deepen our understanding of who we are, nurture a strong sense of national identity, and awaken within us a genuine love and care for our homeland. Through daily reflection, we are trained to grow spiritually, emotionally, and patriotically—for the glory of God and the good of our nation.

Guidelines for Understanding and Living Out my Reflections:

  • Read with both heart and mind. Take time to read the message slowly and thoughtfully. Feel every word, and seek to understand the author’s intent. What truth is being revealed to you?
  • Identify the central message. What is the main theme of the message? What reality is it trying to awaken us to? Recognize the call to action—and to whom it is being addressed.
  • Reflect on the historical context. Use the historical references in the message as a mirror to examine our nation’s present state. What aspects of our society have remained the same? What lessons can we learn?
  • Recognize the emotions expressed. Feel the sorrow, frustration, hope, and urgency conveyed in each paragraph. This is not just literary content—it is the cry of a patriotic heart.
  • Write your personal reflection. What struck you the most? Was there a part of the message that deeply resonated with you? Write a short reflective paragraph expressing your thoughts and feelings and share it on our community chat.
  • Take concrete actions to show love for the nation. What can you do today in response to the message’s call? Start small—with honesty, respect, discipline, and helping others. True patriotism is measured by action.
  • Keep the spirit of the message alive in daily life. Don’t let the message end with reading. Let it live on in your thoughts, your words, and most importantly, your actions.
  • "Share your reflection in the community chat anytime between 6:00 AM and 6:00 PM."

“The Dream That Refuses to Die”


I am not a politician, not a celebrity, not a figure of influence.

I am but a simple Filipino—with no titles to boast, no riches to show—only a heart that still believes in a dream many have long abandoned: the dream of a nation that will rise again, be healed, and shine—just as it was destined to be.

Once, we were called the Pearl of the Orient Seas—a land of promise, a people with a divine destiny. We were blessed with intelligence, resources, and soul to stand among the greatest nations.

But somewhere along the way, we stumbled.
Betrayal destroyed our fate.
Corruption poisoned our dreams.
Injustice silenced our voices.
And slowly, we began to lose something far more dangerous than progress—we began to lose faith in ourselves.

We stopped believing in the dream.
We stopped believing in us.

I have seen it in the eyes of our people—the silent sorrow, the unspoken disappointment, the ridicule thrown at those who dare to hope again. I've heard it in conversations, felt it in stories, and watched it unfold in the choices we make—when we choose to leave, rather than stay and build. When we say, “There’s no more hope,” and lose the will to love our nation.

But I refuse to give up and believe that all hope is gone.

I write—not out of bitterness, but out of a deep, burning love for my country. A fire within that refuses to be extinguished. A love that aches but still dares to dream. This is not a mere lament—it is a stand. A call from one voice, hoping to awaken many.

Because I believe the Filipino spirit is not dead.
It's only imprisoned, which is why it can't move.
And the time for freedom and awakening has come now.

I remember a story that pierced me deeply. A well-dressed Filipina abroad was asked if she was from the Philippines. She replied sharply, “No, I am not.” Not because she couldn’t speak the truth, but because she was ashamed of it. That single moment spoke volumes. It wasn’t just a personal denial—it was a reflection of a generation slowly losing pride in who we are.

Then I remembered another moment I witnessed myself—young students discussing their future. Most of them dreamed of leaving the country, of working and settling abroad. Saddest of all, they no longer dreamed of ever coming back to the land that raised and nurtured them. But one student said, “I want to stay and serve my country.” He was mocked, dismissed as “naive.” Not because he had no ambition—but because he had the courage to believe in a greater purpose: the future of his nation.

I have also witnessed individuals and institutions who once stood with unwavering courage, fighting for the nation’s rise. But I cannot deny the sorrow I feel today—because many of them have quietly faded away, while others have settled for the small victories they achieved within their own limited circles. It saddens me to see that the noble cause they once ignited—a struggle meant for the entire nation—has slowly been reduced to a mission for a chosen few.

Today, they seem content with the small.
Small—because only a single sector reaped the benefit, and the impact never reached the greater whole of our people. They have forgotten how far the journey still stretches, and how deep the wounds of this nation remain. They have reached a point where they no longer wish to expand the cause—because in their own eyes, they have already won. They are safe. They are satisfied.

But to me, this is not yet the victory our nation longs for.
Because with each passing day, more of our people lose hope.
More are left behind in despair, forgetting the dream, and slowly, the vision of rising again fades from their eyes.

I also firmly believe there are still a few leaders who remain faithful and pure in their sworn duty.

Sadly, they are often overshadowed by those in power who have betrayed the nation—leaders who are quick to fill their own pockets, yet fail to see the empty stomachs that go untouched by even the smallest portion of food.

These are not just isolated moments. They are mirrors of a collective wound. The pain of a people who have lost their way. A nation that once dreamed together… but gradually grew tired, gave up, and forgot the once-burning dream.

But it’s not too late.

Yes, we’ve been disappointed. Yes, our hopes have been betrayed. But this is not the end of our story. Not while there are still hearts that beat for the Philippines. Not while there are still voices that cry out for truth, for justice, for dignity.

Because the greatest revolutions don’t begin in the streets.
They begin within the soul.
In the quiet but firm decision of one person to say:

I will believe again.
I will rise again.
I will not give up on this nation.

And as you read this, let this be your call.

You don’t have to be a hero on the front page.
You just have to be a Filipino who refuses to forget who we are.
A Filipino who believes that this land is worth fighting for.
Worth loving.
Worth rebuilding-even if many have already given up on it.

Because the real enemy is not poverty.
Not politics.
Not even corruption.

The greatest battle is within us:
The battle against indifference.
The war against shame.
The struggle to remember who we truly are—and what we are meant to be as a people.

We are not a cursed race.
We are not a land forgotten by God.
We are a chosen nation—whose destiny may have been delayed, but never forsaken.

So I speak now not with arrogance, but from hope.

To the youth—this is your moment.
To the weary—this is your new strength.
To those abroad—this is your invitation to return—not just to the land, but to the purpose.

Let us rise together—not in anger or blame, but with clarity and conviction.

Let us reclaim our true identity and history—not the one written by colonizers and sustained by traitors, but the one carved by the courage of our ancestors who truly fought.

Let us be proud of our heritage—not because we are perfect, but because we are resilient.

This land is not forsaken.
It is our paradise, our true home.

So run toward it—not with fear, but with fire in our hearts. Not with bitterness, but with a love so bold that even darkness will tremble.

Because when the Filipino begins to believe again—when we stop waiting for heroes and instead become the heroes our nation needs. That will be the day the Philippines, the chosen nation, rises again.

And to those who remain standing—
wounded yet still fighting,
thank you, fellow Filipino.

Your tears were not in vain,
nor the wounds you bore for the sake of the nation.
For a wide-awake and united people is rising.
A new breath of life is coming—
a nation ready to rise together.

Now is the time to offer the flame you’ve long carried
for a purpose greater than yourself, greater than any personal ambition.
Now is the time for every heart’s fire to unite, no longer divided.
This is no longer the time for isolated battles.
No longer the time for scattered cries.

This is the hour to unify our actions.
This is the moment to strengthen our resolve.

Together, let us lift up a nation that will rise again—
not because of one, but because of all.
Because of you.
Because of me.
Because of us all.

The call is clear.
The dream still lives.
And the new chapter of our nation’s rising—
begins now.

It begins with you.
It begins with me.
It begins with a commitment that will never again turn its back on our nation.

And now—
to those who still have strength left,
to those who still hold on to hope,
to the hearts where a fire still burns.

Come.
Let the division end.
Let the isolated struggles cease.
It is time to strengthen our resolve,
to unite our voices, our actions, and our convictions.

Let us walk together—not as scattered voices,
but as one united Filipino nation—
with courage in our hearts and clarity in our purpose.
Come, fellow countrymen—
let us lead the rising.

This is the call.
This is the invitation.
Not for one person.
Not for one movement.
But for the nation.

The time is now.
All of us—
are the beginning of a dream reborn.
All of us—
are the light on the path of our nation’s awakening.
