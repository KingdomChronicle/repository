"Me, Myself, and My Beloved Country" is a 24-day challenge to know and love both myself and my country. It is a personal growth journey designed to help us discover our true identity—as God’s masterpiece and as proud Filipinos. This program aims to deepen our understanding of who we are, nurture a strong sense of national identity, and awaken within us a genuine love and care for our homeland. Through daily reflection, we are trained to grow spiritually, emotionally, and patriotically—for the glory of God and the good of our nation.

“The Greatness and Patriotism of Our Filipino Heroes”


At every crucial turning point in our nation’s history, one enduring truth stands firm: the greatness of the Filipino spirit is most powerfully revealed in the lives of our heroes—men and women who did not seek personal gain, but acted out of a deep and selfless love for the land of their birth. With courage, conviction, and unshakable faith in freedom, they etched their legacy into the very soul of our nation.

Their greatness is not measured solely by the victories they achieved, but by the sacrifices they made. They gave up comfort, safety, and even their own lives to uphold the dignity of the Filipino people. Their patriotism was not spoken through slogans, but written in blood, sweat, and sacrifice.

Dr. José Rizal wielded his pen as his weapon, dispelling the darkness of ignorance and injustice with the light of his intellect. His writings awakened a sleeping nation and proved that truth has the power to spark revolution.

Andres Bonifacio, the fearless Father of the Katipunan, ignited the flame of rebellion through bold action. Born into poverty, he rose with honor to lead—showing that true power lies not in position, but in purpose.

Despite his physical limitations, Apolinario Mabini stood as the "Sublime Paralytic," guiding the young nation with the brilliance of his thought.

But heroism was not limited to men.

Melchora Aquino, the “Mother of the Revolution,” gave her home, strength, and love wholeheartedly to the cause.

Gabriela Silang, after losing her husband, continued the fight against the colonizers—proving that Filipina women possess hearts ablaze with freedom and a strength as unyielding as iron.

They were young and old, rich and poor—bound not by status, but by their unwavering love for the Motherland. They labored to build a nation that would stand on its own, speak its own language, and shape its own destiny. Their stories still echo through our history, reminding us that freedom was never freely given—it was earned, fought for, and paid for by those who dared to dream.

But their story does not end there.

Today, we face a new kind of battlefield—not one of swords and guns, but of apathy, division, and a forgotten identity. The real struggle now is for truth, for our identity, and for the soul of the nation. In an age when heroes are rarely remembered, we are being called to pick up the torch they once carried.

So we must ask ourselves:

What if we lived with the same love for our country that Rizal held when he wrote Noli Me Tangere?

What if we bore the courage of Bonifacio as he tore his cedula and declared revolution?

What if every Filipino today carried a fire that no power on earth could ever extinguish?

Would we not rise again—united, dignified, strong, and unstoppable?

The greatness of our heroes is not confined to the pages of history books—it waits to be awakened within us.

Will you answer the call?

Will you become the hero your country longs for?

Because the time for forgetting is over.

It is time to rise again—and march with purpose toward a new dawn.
