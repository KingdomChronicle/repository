Sacred Writings

"All writings must be evaluated through its content, not on who have written it."

Chapter 1

1 Testament of Solomon, son of David, who was king in Jerusalem, and mastered and controlled all spirits of the air, on the earth, and under the earth.
2 By means of them also he wrought all the transcendent works of he Temple.
3 Telling also of the authorities they wield against men, and by what angels these demons are brought to naught.
4 Of the sage Solomon.
5 Blessed art thou, O Lord God, who didst give Solomon such authority.
6 Glory to thee and might unto the ages. Amen.


Chapter 2

1 And behold, when the Temple of the city of Jerusalem was being builded, and the artificers were working thereat, Ornias the demon came among them toward sunset; and he took away half of the pay of the chief-deviser's little boy, as well as half his food.
2 He also continued to suck the thumb of his right hand every day.
3 And the child grew thin, although he was very much loved by the king.


Chapter 3

1 So King Solomon called the boy one day, and questioned him, saying: "Do I not love thee more than all the artisans who are working in the Temple of God?
2 Do I not give thee double wages and a double supply of food?
3 How is it that day by day and hour by hour thow growest thinner?"


Chapter 4

1 But the child said to the king: "I pray thee, O king.
2 Listen to what has befallen all that thy child hath.
3 After we are all released from our work on the Temple of God, after sunset, when I lie down to rest, one of the evil demons comes and takes away from me one half of my pay and one half of my food.
4 Then he also takes hold of my right hand and sucks my thumb.
5 And lo, my soul is opressed, and so my body waxes thinner every day."


Chapter 5

1 Now when I Solomon heard this, I entered the Temple of God, and prayed with all my soul, night and day, that the demon might be delivered into my hands, and that I might gain authority over him.
2 And it came about through my prayer that grace was given to me from the Lord Sabaoth by Michael his archangel.
3 [He brought me] a little ring, having a seal consisting of an engraved stone, and said to me: "Take, O Solomon, king, son of David, the gift which the Lord God has sent thee, the highest Sabaoth.
4 With it thou shalt lock up all demons of the earth, male and female; and with their help thou shalt build up Jerusalem.
5 [But] thou [must] wear this seal of God.
6 And this engraving of the seal of the ring sent thee is a Pentalpha."


Chapter 6

1 And I Solomon was overjoyed, and praised and glorified the God of heaven and earth.
2 And on the morrow I called the boy, and gave him the ring, and said to him: "take this, and at the hour in which the demon shall come unto thee, throw this ring at the chest of the demon, and say to him: 'In the name of God, King Solomon calls thee hither.'
3 And then do thou come running to me, without having any misgivings or fear in respect of aught thou mayest hear on the part of the demon."


Chapter 7

1 So the child took the ring, and went off; and behold, at the customary hour Ornias, the fierce demon, came like a burning fire to take the pay from the child.
2 But the child according to the instructions received from the king, threw the ring at the chest of the demon, and said: "King Solomon calls thee hither."
3 And then he went off at a run to the king.
4 But the demon cried out aloud, saying: "Child, why hast thou done this to me?
5 Take the ring off me, and I will render to thee the gold of the earth.
6 Only take this off me, and forbear to lead me away to Solomon."


Chapter 8

1 But the child said to the demon: "As the Lord God of Israel liveth, I will not brook thee.
2 So come hither." And the child came at a run, rejoicing, to the king, and said: "I have brought the demon, O king, as thou didst command me, O my master.
3 And behold, he stands before the gates of the court of thy palace, crying out, and supplicating with a loud voice; offering me the silver and gold of the earth if I will only bring him unto thee."


Chapter 9

1 And when Solomon heard this, he rose up from his throne, and went outside into the vestibule of the court of his palace; and there he saw the demon, shuddering and trembling.
2 And he said to him: "Who art thou?"
3 And the demon answered: "I am called Ornias."

Chapter 10

1 And Solomon said to him: "Tell me, O demon, to what zodiacal sign thou art subject." And he answered: "To thw Water-pourer.
2 And those who are consumed with desire for the noble virgins upon earth . . . . . [there appears to be a lacuna here], these I strangle.
3 But in case there is no disposition to sleep, I am changed into three forms.
4 Whenever men come to be enamoured of women, I metamorphose myself into a comely female; and I take hold of the men in their sleep, and play with them.
5 And after a while I again take to my wings, and hie me to the heavenly regions.
6 I also appear as a lion, and I am commanded by all the demons.
7 I am offspring of the archangel Uriel, the power of God."


Chapter 11

1 I Solomon, having heard the name of the archangel, prayed and glorified God, the Lord of heaven and earth.
2 And I sealed the demon and set him to work at stone-cutting, so that he might cut the stones in the Temple, which, lying along the shore, had been brought by the Sea of Arabia.
3 But he, fearful of the iron, continued and said to me: "I pray thee, King Solomon, let me go free; and I will bring you all the demons."
4 And as he was not willing to be subject to me, I prayed the archangel Uriel to come and succour me; and I forthwith beheld the archangel Uriel coming down to me from the heavens.


Chapter 12

1 And the angel bade the whales of the sea come out of the abyss.
2 And he cast his destiny upon the ground, and that [destiny] made subject [to him] the great demon.
3 And he commanded the great demon and bold Ornias, to cut stones at the Temple.
4 And accordingly I Solomon glorified the God of heaven and Maker of the earth.
5 And he bade Ornias come with his destiny, and gave him the seal, saying: "Away with thee, and bring me hither the prince of all the demons."


Chapter 13

1 So Ornias took the finger-ring, and went off to Beelzeboul, who has kingship over the demons.
2 He said to him: "Hither! Solomon calls thee." But Beelzeboul, having heard, said to him: "Tell me, who is this Solomon of whom thou speakest to me?"
3 Then Ornias threw the ring at the chest of Beelzeboul, saying: "Solomon the king calls thee."
4 But Beelzeboul cried aloud with a mighty voice, and shot out a great burning flame of fire; and he arose, and followed Ornias, and came to Solomon.


Chapter 14

1 And when I saw the prince of demons, I glorified the Lord God, Maker of heaven and earth, and I said:
2 "Blessed art thou, Lord God Almighty, who hast given to Solomon thy servant wisdom, the assessor of the wise, and hast subjected unto me all the power of he devil."


Chapter 15

1 And I questioned him, and said: "Who art thou?" The demon replied: "I am Beelzebub, the exarch of the demons.
2 And all the demons have their chief seats close to me. And I it is who make manifest the apparition of each demon."
3 And he promised to bring to me in bonds all the unclean spirits.
4 And I again glorified the God of heaven and earth, as I do always give thanks to him.


Chapter 16

1 I then asked of the demon if there were females among them.
2 And when he told me that there were, I said that I desired to see them.
3 So Beelzeboul went off at high speed, and brought unto me Onoskelis, that had a very pretty shape, and the skin of a fair-hued woman; and she tossed her head.


Chapter 17

1 And when she was come, I said to her: "Tell me who art thou?''
2 But she said to me: "I am called Onoskelis, a spirit wrought ...[?shabtai/Saturn?], lurking upon the earth.
3 There is a golden cave where I lie. But I have a place that ever shifts.
4 At one time I strangle men with a noose; at another, I creep up from the nature to the arms [in marg: "worms"].
5 But my most frequent dwelling-places are the precipices, caves, ravines.
6 Oftentimes, however, do I consort with men in the semblance of a woman, and above all with those of a dark skin.
7 For they share my star with me; since they it is who privily or openly worship my star, without knowing that they harm themselves, and but whet my appetite for further mischief.
8 For they wish to provide money by means of memory (commemoration?), but I supply a little to those who worship me fairly."


Chapter 18

1 And I Solomon questioned her about her birth, and she replied: "I was born of a voice untimely, the so-called echo of a man's ordure dropped in a wood."


Chapter 19

1 And I said to her: "Under what star dost thou pass?" And she answered me:
2 "Under the star of the full moon, for the reason that the moon travels over most things."
3 Then I said to her: "And [20] what angel is it that frustrates thee?"
4 And she said to me: "He that in thee [or "through thee" is reigning."
5 And I thought that she mocked me, and bade a soldier strike her.
6 But she cried aloud, and said: "I am [subjected] to thee, O king, by the wisdom of God given to thee, and by the angel Joel."


Chapter 20

1 So I commanded her to spin the hemp for the ropes used in the building of the house of God;
2 and accordingly, when I had sealed and bound her, she was so overcome and brought to naught as to stand night and day spinning the hemp.


Chapter 21

1 And I at once bade another demon to be led unto me; and instantly there approached me the demon Asmodeus, bound, and I asked him: "Who art thou ?'
2 But he shot on me a glance of anger and rage, and said: "And who art thou?'
3 And I said to him: "Thus punished as thou art, answerest thou me?" But he, with rage, said to me: "But how shall I answer thee, for thou art a son of man;
4 whereas I was born an angel's seed by a daughter of man, so that no word of our heavenly kind addressed to the earth-born can be overweening.
5 Wherefore also my star is bright in heaven, and men call it, some the Wain, and some the dragon's child.
6 I keep near unto this star.
7 So ask me not many things; for thy kingdom also after a little time is to be disrupted, and thy glory is but for a season.
8 And short will be thy tyranny over us; and then we shall again have free range over mankind, so as that they shall revere us as if we were gods, not knowing, men that they are, the names of the angels set over us."


Chapter 22

1 And I Solomon, on hearing this, bound him more carefully, and ordered him to be flogged with thongs of ox-hide, and to tell me humbly what was his name and what his business. And he answered me thus:
2 "I am called Asmodeus among mortals, and my business is to plot against the newly wedded, so that they may not know one another.
3 And I sever them utterly by many calamities, and I waste away the beauty of virgin women, and estrange their hearts."


Chapter 23

1 And I said to him: "Is this thy only business?" And he answered me: "I transport men into fits of madness and desire, when they have wives of their own, so that they leave them, and go off by night and day to others that belong to other men;
2 with the result that they commit sin, and fall into murderous deeds."


Chapter 24

1 And I adjured him by the name of the Lord Sabaôth, saying: "Fear God, Asmodeus, and tell me by what angel thou art frustrated."
2 But he said: "By Raphael, the archangel that stands before the throne of God.
3 But the liver and gall of a fish put me to flight, when smoked over ashes of the tamarisk."
4 I again asked him, and said: "Hide not aught from me.
5 For I am Solomon, son of David, King of Israel.
6 Tell me the name of the fish which thou reverest."
7 And he answered: "It is the Glanos by name, and is found in the rivers of Assyria; wherefore it is that I roam about in those parts."


Chapter 25

1 And I said to him: "Hast thou nothing else about thee, Asmodeus?" And he answered:
2 "The power of God knoweth, which hath bound me with the indissoluble bonds of yonder one's seal, that whatever I have told thee is true.
3 I pray thee, King Solomon, condemn me not to [go into] water."
4 But I smiled, and said to him: "As the Lord God of my fathers liveth, I will lay iron on thee to wear.
5 But thou shalt also make the clay for the entire construction of the Temple, treading it down with thy feet."
6 And I ordered them to give him ten water-jars to carry water in.
7 And the demon groaned terribly, and did the work I ordered him to do.
8 And this I did, because that fierce demon Asmodeus knew even the future. And I Solomon glorified God, who gave wisdom to me Solomon his servant.
9 And the liver of the fish and its gall I hung on the spike of a reed, and burned it over Asmodeus because of his being so strong, and his unbearable malice was thus frustrated.


Chapter 26

1 And I summoned again to stand before me Beelzeboul, the prince of demons, and I sat him down on a raised seat of honour, and said to him:
2 "Why art thou alone, prince of the demons?" And he said to me:
3 "Because I alone am left of the angels of heaven that came down.
4 For I was first angel in the first heaven being entitled Beelzeboul.
5 And now I control all those who are bound in Tartarus.
6 But I too have a child, and he haunts the Red Sea.
7 And on any suitable occasion he comes up to me again, being subject to me;
8 and reveals to me what he has done, and I support him.


Chapter 27

1 I Solomon said unto him: "Beelzeboul, what is thy employment?" And he answered me: "I destroy kings.
2 I ally myself with foreign tyrants.
3 And my own demons I set on to men, in order that the latter may believe in them and be lost.
4 And the chosen servants of God, priests and faithful men, I excite unto desires for wicked sins, and evil heresies, and lawless deeds; and they obey me, and I bear them on to destruction.
5 And I inspire men with envy, and [desire for] murder, and for wars and sodomy, and other evil things.
6 And I will destroy the world."


Chapter 28

1 So I said to him: "Bring to me thy child, who is, as thou sayest, in the Red Sea."
2 But he said to me: "I will not bring him to thee.
3 But there shall come to me another demon called Ephippas.
4 Him will I bind, and he will bring him up from the deep unto me."
5 And I said to him: "How comes thy son to be in the depth of the sea, and what is his name?
6 "And he answered me: "Ask me not, for thou canst not learn from me.
7 However, he will come to thee by any command, and will tell thee openly."


Chapter 29

1 I said to him: "Tell me by what angel thou art frustrated."
2 And he answered: "By the holy and precious name of the Almighty God, called by the Hebrews by a row of numbers, of which the sum is 644, and among the Greeks it is Emmanuel.
3 And if one of the Romans adjure me by the great name of the power Eleéth, I disappear at once."


Chapter 30

1 I Solomon was astounded when I heard this; and I ordered him to saw up Theban marbles.
2 And when he began to saw the marbles, the other demons cried out with a loud voice, howling because of their king Beelzeboul.


Chapter 31

1 But I Solomon questioned him, saying: "If thou wouldst gain a respite, discourse to me about the things in heaven."
2 And Beelzeboul said: "Hear, O king, if thou burn gum, and incense, and bulb of the sea, with nard and saffron, and light seven lamps in an earthquake, thou wilt firmly fix thy house.
3 And if, being pure, thou light them at dawn in the sun alight, then wilt thou see the heavenly dragons, how they wind themselves along and drag the chariot of the sun."


Chapter 32

1 And I Solomon, having heard this, rebuked him, and said: "Silence for this present, and continue to saw the marbles as I commanded thee."
2 And I Solomon praised God, and commanded another demon to present himself to me.
3 And one came before me who carried his face high up in the air, but the rest of the spirit curled away like a snail.
4 And it broke through the few soldiers, and raised also a terrible dust on the ground, and carried it upwards; and then again hurled it back to frighten us, and asked what questions I could ask as a rule.
5 And I stood up, and spat on the ground in that spot, and sealed with the ring of God.
6 And forthwith the dust-wind stopped.
7 Then I asked him, saying: "Who art thou, O wind?" Then he once more shook up a dust, and answered me:
8 "What wouldst thou have, King Solomon?"
9 I answered him: "Tell me what thou art called, and I would fain ask thee a question.
10 But so far I give thanks to God who has made me wise to answer their evil plots."


Chapter 33

1 But [the demon] answered me: "I am the spirit of the ashes (Tephras)."
2 And I said to him: "What is thy pursuit?"
3 And he said: "I bring darkness on men, and set fire to fields; and I bring homesteads to naught.
4 But most busy am I in summer.
5 However, when I get an opportunity, I creep into corners of the wall, by night and day. For I am offspring of the great one, and nothing less."
6 Accordingly I said to him: "Under what star dost thou lie?" And he answered:
7 "In the very tip of the moon's horn, when it is found in the south.
8 There is my star.
9 For I have been bidden to restrain the convulsions of the hemitertian fever; and this is why many men pray to the hemitertian fever, using these three names:
10 Bultala, Thallal, Melchal.
11 And I heal them."
12 And I said to him: "I am Solomon; when therefore thou wouldst do harm, by whose aid dost thou do it?" But he said to me:
13 "By the angel's, by whom also the third day's fever is lulled to rest."
14 So I questioned him, and said: "And by what name?" And he answered: "That of the archangel Azael."
15 And I summoned the archangel Azael, and set a seal on the demon, and commanded him to seize great stones, and toss them up to the workmen on the higher parts of the Temple.
16 And, being compelled, the demon began to do what he was bidden to do.


Chapter 34

1 And I glorified God afresh who gave me this authority, and ordered another demon to come before me.
2 And there came seven spirits, females, bound and woven together, fair in appearance and comely.
3 And I Solomon, seeing them, questioned them and said:
4 "Who are ye?" But they, with one accord, said with one voice:
5 "We are of the thirty-three elements of the cosmic ruler of the darkness."
6 And the first said: "I am Deception."
7 The second said: "I am Strife." The third: "I am Klothod, which is battle."
8 The fourth: "I am Jealousy."
9 The fifth: "I am Power."
10 The sixth: "I am Error."
11 The seventh: "I am the worst of all, and our stars are in heaven.
12 Seven stars humble in sheen, and all together.
13 And we are called as it were goddesses.
14 We change our place all and together, and together we live, sometimes in Lydia, sometimes in Olympus, sometimes in a great mountain."


Chapter 35

1 So I Solomon questioned them one by one, beginning with the first, and going down to the seventh.
2 The first said: "I am Deception, I deceive and weave snares here and there.
3 I whet and excite heresies.
4 But I have an angel who frustrates me, Lamechalal."


Chapter 36

1 Likewise also the second said: "I am Strife, strife of strifes.
2 I bring timbers, stones, hangers, my weapons on the spot.
3 But I have an angel who frustrates me, Baruchiachel."


Chapter 37

1 Likewise also the third said: "I am called Klothod [1], which is Battle, and I cause the well-behaved to scatter and fall foul one of the other.
2 And why do I say so much? I have an angel that frustrates me: "Marmarath."


Chapter 38

1 Likewise also the fourth said: "I cause men to forget their sobriety and moderation.
2 I part them and split them into parties; for Strife follows me hand in hand.
3 I rend the husband from the sharer of his bed, and children from parents, and brothers from sisters.
4 But why tell so much to my despite? I have an angel that frustrates me, the great Balthial."


Chapter 39

1 Likewise also the fifth said: "I am Power. By power I raise up tyrants and tear down kings.
2 To all rebels I furnish power. I have an angel that frustrates me, Asteraôth."


Chapter 40

1 Likewise also the sixth said: "I am Error, O King Solomon.
2 And I will make thee to err, as I have before made thee to err, when I caused thee to slay thy own brother.
3 I will lead you into error, so as to pry into graves; and teach them that dig, and I lead errant souls away from all piety, and many other evil traits are mine.
4 But I have an angel that frustrates me, Uriel."


Chapter 41

1 Likewise also the seventh said: "I am the worst, and I make thee worse off than thou wast; because I will impose the bonds of Artemis.
2 But the locust will set me free, for by means thereof is it fated that thou shalt achieve . . . . . . . . . . .. . . . .
3 For if one were wise, he would not turn his steps toward me."


Chapter 42

1 So I Solomon, having heard and wondered, sealed them with my ring; and since they were so considerable, I bade them dig the foundations of the Temple of God.
2 For the length of it was 250 cubits.
3 And I bade them be industrious, and with one murmur of joint protest they began to perform the tasks enjoined.


Chapter 43

1 But I Solomon glorified the Lord, and bade another demon come before me.
2 And there was brought to me a demon having all the limbs of a man, but without a head.
3 And I, seeing him, said to him: "Tell me, who art thou?''
4 And he answered: "I am a demon."
5 So I said to him: "Which?" And he answered me: "I am called Envy.
6 For I delight to devour heads, being desirous to secure for myself a head; but I do not eat enough, but am anxious to have such a head as thou hast."


Chapter 44

1 I Solomon, on hearing this, sealed him, stretching out my hand against his chest.
2 Whereon the demon leapt up, and threw himself down, and gave a groan, saying: "Woe is me! where am I come to?
3 O traitor Ornias, I cannot see!" So I said to him: "I am Solomon.
4 Tell me then how thou dost manage to see." And he answered me: "By means of my feelings."
5 I then, Solomon, having heard his voice come up to me, asked him how he managed to speak.
6 And he answered me: "I, O King Solomon, am wholly voice, for I have inherited the voices of many men.
7 For in the case of all men who are called dumb, I it is who smashed their heads, when they were children and had reached their eighth day.
8 Then when a child is crying in the night, I become a spirit, and glide by means of his voice. . . .
9 In the crossways also I have many services to render, and my encounter is fraught with harm.
10 For I grasp in all instant a man's head, and with my hands, as with a sword, I cut it off, and put it on to myself.
11 And in this way, by means of the fire which is in me, through my neck it is swallowed up.
12 I it is that sends grave mutilations and incurable on men's feet, and inflict sores."


Chapter 45

1 And I Solomon, on hearing this, said to him: "Tell me how thou dost discharge forth the fire?
2 Out of what sources dost thou emit it?" And the spirit said to me: "From the Day-star.
3 For here hath not yet been found that Elburion, to whom men offer prayers and kindle lights.
4 And his name is invoked by the seven demons before me. And he cherishes them."


Chapter 46

1 But I said to him: "Tell me his name." But he answered: "I cannot tell thee.
2 For if I tell his name, I render myself incurable.
3 But he will come in response to his name."
4 And on hearing this, I Solomon said to him: "Tell me then, by what angel thou art frustrated?"
5 And he answered: "By the fiery flash of lightning."
6 And I bowed myself before the Lord God of Israel, and bade him remain in the keeping of Beelzeboul until Iax should come.


Chapter 47

1 Then I ordered another demon to come before me, and there came into my presence a hound, having a very large shape, and it spoke with a loud voice, and said, "Hail, Lord, King Solomon!"
2 And I Solomon was astounded.
3 I said to it: Who art thou, O hound?"
4 And it answered: "I do indeed seem to thee to be a hound, but before thou wast, O King Solomon, I was a man that wrought many unholy deeds on earth.
5 I was surpassingly learned in letters, and was so mighty that I could hold the stars of heaven back.
6 And many divine works did I prepare.
7 For I do harm to men who follow after our star, and turn them to.....
8 And I seize the frenzied men by the larynx, and so destroy them."


Chapter 48

1 And I Solomon said to him: "What is thy name?" And he answered: ''Staff"(Rabdos).
2 And I said to him: "What is thine employment?
3 And what results canst thou achieve?" And he replied:
4 'Give me thy man, and I will lead him away into a mountainous spot, and will show him a green stone tossed to and fro, with which thou mayest adorn the temple of the Lord God."


Chapter 49

1 And I Solomon, on hearing this, ordered my servant to set off with him, and to take the finger-ring bearing the seal of God with him.
2 And I said to him: "Whoever shall show thee the green stone, seal him with this finger-ring.
3 And mark the spot with care, and bring me the demon hither.
4 And the demon showed him the green stone, and he sealed it, and brought the demon to me.
5 And I Solomon decided to confine with my seal on my right hand the two, the headless demon, likewise the hound, that was so huge; he should be bound as well.
6 And I bade the hound keep safe the fiery spirit so that lamps as it were might by day and night cast their light through its maw on the artisans at work.


Chapter 50

1 And I Solomon took from the mine of that stone 200 shekels for the supports of the table of incense, which was similar in appearance.
2 And I Solomon glorified the Lord God, and then closed round the treasure of that stone.
3 And I ordered afresh the demons to cut marble for the construction of the house of God.
4 And I Solomon prayed to the Lord, and asked the hound, saying: "By what angel art thou frustrated?" And the demon replied: "By the great Brieus."


Chapter 51

1 And I praised the Lord God of heaven and earth, and bade another demon come forward to me; and there came before me one in the form of a lion roaring.
2 And he stood and answered me saying: "O king, in the form which I have, I am a spirit quite incapable of being perceived.
3 Upon all men who lie prostrate with sickness I leap, coming stealthily along; and I render the man weak, so that his habit of body is enfeebled. But I have also another glory, O king.
4 I cast out demons, and I have legions under my control.
5 And I am capable of being received in my dwelling-places, along with all the demons belonging to the legions under me."
6 But I Solomon, on hearing this, asked him: "What is thy name?"
7 But he answered: "Lion-bearer, Rath in kind."
8 And I said to him: "How art thou to be frustrated along with thy legions?
9 What angel is it that frustrates thee?" And he answered: "If I tell thee my name, I bind not myself alone, but also the legions of demons under me."


Chapter 52

1 So I said to him: "I adjure thee in the name of the God Sabaoth, to tell me by what name thou art frustrated along with thy host.
2 "And the spirit answered me: "The 'great among men,' who is to suffer many things at the hands of men, whose name is the figure 644, which is Emmanuel; he it is who has bound us, and who will then come and plunge us from the steep under water.
3 He is noised abroad in the three letters which bring him down."


Chapter 53

1 And I Solomon, on hearing this, glorified God, and condemned his legion to carry wood from the thicket.
2 And I condemned the lion-shaped one himself to saw up the wood small with his teeth, for burning in the unquenchable furnace for the Temple of God.


Chapter 54

1 And I worshipped the Lord God of Israel, and bade another demon come forward.
2 And there came before me a dragon, three-headed, of fearful hue.
3 And I questioned him: "Who art thou?"
4 And he answered me: "I am a caltrop-like spirit, whose activity in three lines.
5 But I blind children in women's wombs, and twirl their ears round.
6 And I make them deaf and mute.
7 And I have again in my third head means of slipping in.
8 And I smite men in the limbless part of the body, and cause them to fall down, and foam, and grind their teeth.
9 But I have my own way of being frustrated, Jerusalem being signified in writing, unto the place called 'of the head."
10 For there is fore-appointed the angel of the great counsel, and now he will openly dwell on the cross.
11 He doth frustrate me, and to him am I subject."


Chapter 55

1 "But in the place where thou sittest, O King Solomon, standeth a column in the air, of purple...
2 The demon called Ephippas hath brought [it] up from the Red Sea, from inner Arabia. He it is that shall be shut up in a skin-bottle and brought before thee.
3 But at the entrance of the Temple, which thou hast begun to build, O King Solomon, lies stored much gold, which dig thou up and carry off."
4 And I Solomon sent my servant, and found it to be as the demon told me.
5 And I sealed him with my ring, and praised the Lord God."


Chapter 56

1 So I said to him: "What art thou called?" And the demon said: "I am the crest of dragons."
2 And I bade him make bricks in the Temple.
3 He had human hands.


Chapter 57

1 And I adored the Lord God of Israel, and bade another demon present himself.
2 And there came before me a spirit in woman's form, that had a head without any limbs, and her hair was dishevelled.
3 And I said to her: "Who art thou?" But she answered: "Nay, who art thou?
4 And why dost thou want to hear concerning me?
5 But, as thou wouldst learn, here I stand bound before thy face.
6 Go then into thy royal storehouses and wash thy hands.
7 Then sit down afresh before thy tribunal, and ask me questions; and thou shalt learn, O king, who I am."


Chapter 58

1 And I Solomon did as she enjoined me, and restrained myself because of the wisdom dwelling in me; in order that I might hear of her deeds, and reprehend them, and manifest them to men.
2 And I sat down, and said to the demon: "What art thou?"
3 And she said: "I am called among men Obizuth; and by night I sleep not, but go my rounds over all the world, and visit women in childbirth.
4 And divining the hour I take my stand; and if I am lucky, I strangle the child.
5 But if not, I retire to another place.
6 For I cannot for a single night retire unsuccessful.
7 For I am a fierce spirit, of myriad names and many shapes.
8 And now hither, now thither I roam. And to westering parts I go my rounds.
9 But as it now is, though thou hast sealed me round with the ring of God, thou hast done nothing.
10 I am not standing before thee, and thou wilt not be able to command me.
11 For I have no work other than the destruction of children, and the making their ears to be deaf, and the working of evil to their eyes, and the binding their mouths with a bond, and the ruin of their minds, and paining of their bodies."


Chapter 59

1 When I Solomon heard this, I marvelled at her appearance, for I beheld all her body to be in darkness.
2 But her glance was altogether bright and greeny, and her hair was tossed wildly like a dragon's; and the whole of her limbs were invisible. And her voice was very clear as it came to me.
3 And I cunningly said: "Tell me by what angel thou art frustrated, O evil spirit?"
4 By she answered me: "By the angel of God called Afarôt, which is interpreted Raphael, by whom I am frustrated now and for all time.
5 His name, if any man know it, and write the same on a woman in childbirth, then I shall not be able to enter her.
6 Of this name the number is 640."
7 And I Solomon having heard this, and having glorified the Lord, ordered her hair to be bound, and that she should be hung up in front of the Temple of God; that all the children of Israel, as they passed, might see it, and glorify the Lord God of Israel, who had given me this authority, with wisdom and power from God, by means of this signet.


Chapter 60

1 And I again ordered another demon to come before me.

3 And all its limbs, except the feet, were those of a dragon; and it had wings on its back.
4 And when I beheld it, I was astonied, and said: "Who art thou, demon, and what art thou called?
5 And whence hast thou come? Tell me."


Chapter 61

1 And the spirit answered and said: "This is the first time I have stood before the, O King Solomon.
2 I am a spirit made into a god among men, but now brought to naught by the ring and wisdom vouchsafed to thee by God.
3 Now I am the so-called winged dragon, and I chamber not with many women, but only with a few that are of fair shape, which possess the name of xuli, of this star.
4 And I pair with them in the guise of a spirit winged in form, coitum habens per nates.
5 And she on whom I have leapt goes heavy with child, and that which is born of her becomes eros.
6 But since such offspring cannot be carried by men, the woman in question breaks wind.
7 Such is my role.
8 Supposed then only that I am satisfied, and all the other demons molested and disturbed by thee will speak the whole truth.
9 But those composed of fire will cause to be burned up by fire the material of the logs which is to be collected by them for the building in the Temple."


Chapter 62

Chapter 64

1 And there came before my face another spirit, as it were a woman in the form she had.
2 But on her shoulders she had two other heads with hands.
3 And I asked her, and said: "Tell me, who art thou?" And she said to me: "I am Enêpsigos, who also have a myriad names."
4 And I said her: "By what angel art thou frustrated?" But she said to me: "What seekest, what askest thou? I undergo changes, like the goddess I am called.
5 And I change again, and pass into possession of another shape.
6 And be not desirous therefore to know all that concerns me.
7 But since thou art before me for this much, hearken.
8 I have my abode in the moon, and for that reason I possess three forms.
9 At times I am magically invoked by the wise as Kronos.
10 At other times, in connection with those who bring me down, I come down and appear in another shape.
11 The measure of the element is inexplicable and indefinable, and not to be frustrated.
12 I then, changing into these three forms, come down and become such as thou seest me; but I am frustrated by the angel Rathanael, who sits in the third heaven.
13 This then is why I speak to thee. Yonder temple cannot contain me."


Chapter 65

2 And I sealed her with a triple chain, and (placed) beneath her the fastening of the chain.
3 I used the seal of God, and the spirit prophesied to me, saying: "This is what thou, King Solomon, doest to us.
4 But after a time thy kingdom shall be broken, and again in season this Temple shall be riven asunder; and all Jerusalem shall be undone by the King of the Persians and Medes and Chaldaeans.
5 And the vessels of this Temple, which thou makest, shall be put to servile uses of the gods; and along with them all the jars, in which thou dost shut us up, shall be broken by the hands of men.
6 And then we shall go forth in great power hither and thither, and be disseminated all over the world.
7 And we shall lead astray the inhabited world for a long season, until the Son of God is stretched upon the cross.
8 For never before doth arise a king like unto him, one frustrating us all, whose mother shall not have contact with man.
9 Who else can receive such authority over spirits, except he, whom the first devil will seek to tempt, but will not prevail over?
10 The number of his name is 644, which is Emmanuel.
11 Wherefore, O King Solomon, thy time is evil, and thy years short and evil, and to thy servant shall thy kingdom be given."


Chapter 66

4 And I glorified the Lord God of Israel, and commanded the spirits to be bound with bonds indissoluble.


Chapter 67

1 And having praised God, I commanded another spirit to come before me; and there came before my face another demon, having in front the shape of a horse, but behind of a fish.

3 I am such a spirit as rounds itself and comes over the expanses of the water of the sea, and I trip up the men who sail thereon.
4 For I round myself into a wave, and transform myself, and then throw myself on ships and come right in on them.
5 And that is my business, and my way of getting hold of money and men.
6 For I take the men, and whirl them round with myself, and hurl the men out of the sea.
7 For I am not covetous of men's bodies, but cast them up out of the sea so far.
8 But since Beelzeboul, ruler of the spirits of air and of those under the earth, and lord of earthly ones, hath a joint kingship with us in respect of the deeds of each one of us, therefore I went up from the sea, to get a certain outlook in his company.


Chapter 68

Chapter 70

Chapter 71

1 And he said: "My dwelling is in fruitful places, but my procedure is this.
2 I seat myself beside the men who pass along among the tombs, and in untimely season I assume the form of the dead; and if I catch any one, I at once destroy him with my sword. But if I cannot destroy him, I cause him to be possessed with a demon, and to devour his own flesh, and the hair to fall off his chin."
3 But I said to him: "Do thou then be in fear of the God of heaven and of earth, and tell me by angel thou art frustrated."
4 And he answered: "He destroys me who is to become Saviour, a man whose number, if any one shall write it on his forehead, he will defeat me, and in fear I shall quickly retreat.
5 And, indeed, if any one write this sign on him, I shall be in fear."
6 And I Solomon, on hearing this, and having glorified the Lord God, shut up this demon like the rest.

Chapter 110

Chapter 111

1 And I Solomon, on hearing this, felt compunction as I looked at his old age; and I bade the child be brought to me.

Chapter 112

1 But I Solomon, having heard this, said to the demon: "Is that true that thou speakest?"

Chapter 113

1 And when they were gone I ordered Ornias to be brought forward, and said to him: "Tell me how you know this;" and he answered: "We demons ascend into the firmament of heaven, and fly about among the stars.

3 and if a man does not die by some untimely disaster or by violence, then we demons transform ourselves in such a way as to appear to men and be worshipped in our human nature."


Chapter 114

1 I therefore, having heard this, glorified the Lord God, and again I questioned the demon, saying: "Tell me how ye can ascend into heaven, being demons, and amidst the stars and holy angels intermingle."
2 And he answered: "Just as things are fulfilled in heaven, so also on earth (are fulfilled) the types of all of them.
3 For there are principalities, authorities, world-rulers, and we demons fly about in the air; and we hear the voices of the heavenly beings, and survey all the powers.
4 And as having no ground (basis) on which to alight and rest, we lose strength and fall off like leaves from trees.
5 And men seeing us imagine that the stars are falling from heaven.
6 But it is not really so, O king; but we fall because of our weakness, and because we have nowhere anything to lay hold of; and so we fall down like lightnings in the depth of night and suddenly.
7 And we set cities in flames and fire the fields.
8 For the stars have firm foundations in the heavens like the sun and the moon."

Chapter 117

8 And behold, King Solomon, I and my people and all my land will serve thee unto death.
9 And all Arabia shall be at peace with thee, if thou wilt perform this act of righteousness for us.
10 Wherefore we pray thee, contemn not our humble prayer, and suffer not to be utterly brought to naught the eparchy subordinated to thy authority.
11 Because we are suppliants, both I and my people and all my land.
12 Farewell to my Lord. All health!"


Chapter 118

1 And I Solomon read this epistle; and I folded it up and gave it to my people, and said to them: "After seven days shalt thou remind me of this epistle.
2 And Jerusalem was built, and the Temple was being completed.
3 And there was a stone, the end stone of the corner lying there, great, chosen out, one which I desired lay in the head of the corner of the completion of the Temple.
4 And all the workmen, and all the demons helping them came to the same place to bring up the stone and lay it on the pinnacle of the holy Temple, and were not strong enough to stir it, and lay it upon the corner allotted to it.
5 For that stone was exceedingly great and useful for the corner of the Temple."


Chapter 119

1 And after seven days, being reminded of the epistle of Adares, King of Arabia, I called my servant and said to him: "Order thy camel and take for thyself a leather flask, and take also this seal.
2 And go away into Arabia to the place in which the evil spirit blows; and there take the flask, and the signet-ring in front of the mouth of the flask, and (hold them) towards the blast of the spirit.
3 And when the flask is blown out, thou wilt understand that the demon is (in it).
4 Then hastily tie up the mouth of to flask, and seal it securely with the seal-ring, and lay it carefully on the camel and bring it me hither.
5 And if on the way it offer thee gold or silver or treasure in return for letting it go, see that thou be not persuaded.
6 But arrange without using oath to release it. And then if it point out to the places where are gold or silver, mark the places and seal them with this seal.
7 And bring the demon to me. And now depart, and fare thee well."


Chapter 120

1 Then the youth did as was bidden him. And he ordered his camel, and laid on it a flask, and set off into Arabia.
2 And the men of that region would not believe that he would be able to catch the evil spirit.
3 And when it was dawn, the servant stood before the spirit's blast, and laid the flask on the ground, and the finger-ring on the mouth of the flask.
4 And the demon blew through the middle of the finger-ring into the mouth of the flask, and going in blew out the flask.
5 But the man promptly stood up to it and drew tight with his hand the mouth of the flask, in the name of the Lord God of Sabaôth.
6 And the demon remained within the flask.
7 And after that the youth remained in that land three days to make trial.
8 And the spirit no longer blew against that city.
9 And all the Arabs knew that he had safely shut in the spirit.


Chapter 121

1 Then the youth fastened the flask on the camel, and the Arabs sent him forth on his way with much honour and precious gifts, praising and magnifying the God of Israel.
2 But the youth brought in the bag and laid it in the middle of the Temple.
3 And on the next day, I King Solomon, went into the Temple of God and sat in deep distress about the stone of the end of the corner.
4 And when I entered the Temple, the flask stood up and walked around some seven steps and then fell on its mouth and did homage to me.
5 And I marvelled that even along with the bottle the demon still had power and could walk about; and I commanded it to stand up.
6 And the flask stood up, and stood on its feet all blown out.
7 And I questioned him, saying: "Tell me, who art thou?" And the spirit within said: "I am the demon called Ephippas, that is in Arabia."
8 And I said to him: "Is this thy name?" And he answered: "Yes; wheresoever I will, I alight and set fire and do to death."


Chapter 122

1 And I said to him: "By what angel art thou frustrated?" And he answered: "By the only-ruling God, that hath authority over me even to be heard.
2 He that is to be born of a virgin and crucified by the Jews on a cross.
3 Whom the angels and archangels worship.
4 He doth frustrate me, and enfeeble me of my great strength, which has been given me by my father the devil."
5 And I said to him: "What canst thou do?'' And he answered: ''I am able to remove mountains, to overthrow the oaths of kings.
6 I wither trees and make their leaves to fall off."
7 And I said to him: "Canst thou raise this stone, and lay it for the beginning of this corner which exists in the fair plan of the Temple?''
8 And he said: "Not only raise this, O king; but also, with the help of the demon who presides over the Red Sea, I will bring up the pillar of air, and will stand it where thou wilt in Jerusalem.''


Chapter 123

1 Saying this, I laid stress on him, and the flask became as if depleted of air.
2 And I placed it under the stone, and (the spirit) girded himself up, and lifted it up top of the flask.
3 And the flask went up the steps, carrying the stone, and laid it down at the end of the entrance of the Temple.
4 And I Solomon, beholding the stone raised aloft and placed on a foundation, said: "Truly the Scripture is fulfilled, which says: 'The stone which the builders rejected on trial, that same is become the head of the corner.'
5 For this it is not mine to grant, but God's, that the demon should be strong enough to lift up so great a stone and deposit it in the place I wished."


Chapter 124

1 And Ephippas led the demon of the Red Sea with the column.
2 And they both took the column and raised it aloft from the earth.
3 And I outwitted these two spirits, so that they could not shake the entire earth in a moment of time.
4 And then I sealed round with my ring on this side and that, and said: "Watch."
5 And the spirits have remained upholding it until this day, for proof of the wisdom vouchsafed to me.
6 And there the pillar was hanging of enormous size, in mid air, supported by the winds.
7 And thus the spirits appeared underneath, like air, supporting it.
8 And if one looks fixedly, the pillar is a little oblique, being supported by the spirits; and it is so to day.


Chapter 125

1 And I Solomon questioned the other spirit which came up with the pillar from the depth of the Red Sea.
2 And I said to him: "Who art thou, and what calls thee?
3 And what is thy business?
4 For I hear many things about thee.''
5 And the demon answered: "I, O King Solomon, am called Abezithibod.
6 I am a descendant of the archangel.
7 Once as I sat in the first heaven, of which the name is Ameleouth -- I then am a fierce spirit and winged, and with a single wing, plotting against every spirit under heaven.
8 I was present when Moses went in before Pharaoh, king of Egypt, and I hardened his heart.
9 I am he whom Iannes and Iambres invoked homing with Moses in Egypt.
10 I am he who fought against Moses with wonders with signs."


Chapter 126

1 I said therefore to him: "How wast thou found in the Red Sea?"
2 And he answered: "In the exodus of the sons of Israel I hardened the heart of Pharaoh.
3 And I excited his heart and that of his ministers. And I caused them to pursue after the children of Israel.
4 And Pharaoh followed with (me) and all the Egyptians.
5 Then I was present there, and we followed together.
6 And we all came up upon the Red Sea.
7 And it came to pass when the children of Israel had crossed over, the water returned and hid all the host of the Egyptians and all their might.
8 And I remained in the sea, being kept under this pillar.
9 But when Ephippas came, being sent by thee, shut up in the vessel of a flask, he fetched me up to thee."


Chapter 127

1 I, therefore, Solomon, having heard this, glorified God and adjured the demons not to disobey me, but to remain supporting the pillar.
2 And they both sware, saying: "The Lord thy God liveth, we will not let go this pillar until the world's end.
3 But on whatever day this stone fall, then shall be the end of the world."


Chapter 128

1 And I Solomon glorified God, and adorned the Temple of the Lord with all fair-seeming.
2 And I was glad in spirit in my kingdom, and there was peace in my days.
3 And I took wives of my own from every land, who were numberless.
4 And I marched against the Jebusaeans, and there I saw Jebusaean, daughter of a man: and fell violently in love with her, and desired to take her to wife along with my other wives.
5 And I said to their priests: "Give me the Sonmanites (i.e. Shunammite) to wife."
6 But the priests of Moloch said to me: "If thou lovest this maiden, go in and worship our gods, the great god Raphan and the god called Moloch."
7 I therefore was in fear of the glory of God, and did not follow to worship.
8 And I said to them: "I will not worship a strange god.
9 What is this proposal, that ye compel me to do so much?" But they said: ". . . . . by our fathers."


Chapter 129

1 And when I answered that I would on no account worship strange gods, they told the maiden not to sleep with me until I complied and sacrificed to the gods.
2 I then was moved, but crafty Eros brought and laid by her for me five grasshoppers, saying: "Take these grasshoppers, and crush them together in the name of the god Moloch; and then will I sleep with you."
3 And this I actually did.
4 And at once the Spirit of God departed from me, and I became weak as well as foolish in my words.
5 And after that I was obliged by her to build a temple of idols to Baal, and to Rapha, and to Moloch, and to the other idols.


Chapter 130

1 I then, wretch that I am, followed her advice, and the glory of God quite departed from me; and my spirit was darkened, and I became the sport of idols and demons.
2 Wherefore I wrote out this Testament, that ye who get possession of it may pity, and attend to the last things, and not to the first.
3 So that ye may find grace for ever and ever. Amen.
