I. The Two Types of People

Since the dawn of humanity, there have always been two kinds of people walking this Earth—two distinct groups sharing the same soil but moving to very different rhythms.

First, The Ordinary:

These are the ones who live for the temporary. Their hearts are chained to material things, their minds shaped by man-made systems, and their vision limited to what the eye can see. They follow without question, conform without resistance, and accept the world’s standards as absolute truth. Rarely do they challenge the noise around them or seek the deeper meaning of life, settling instead for survival, comfort, and fleeting pleasures.

Second, The Extraordinary:

Then there are those who feel different—those who sense a deeper calling, a restless fire within that refuses to be tamed. If you’ve ever felt like you don’t belong, like your soul speaks a language the world doesn’t understand, this is who you truly are. You think deeply, dream boldly, and carry a sacred spark placed in you by the Divine. Your heart longs for something eternal, and your spirit does not originate from Earth’s noise but from Heaven’s silence and majesty. You were never meant to blend in—you were chosen to stand apart.

Jesus said it best:
“They are not of the world, just as I am not of the world” (John 17:16).

You belong to this extraordinary group—agents of transformation, called to awaken, disrupt, and lead. Your journey mirrors the giants of faith: Abraham, Moses, David, and others who walked as strangers and pilgrims, moving in step with Heaven’s agenda. You are here not by accident but as part of a divine strategy. Though your path may be lonely, misunderstood, or resisted, it is filled with power, purpose, and promise.

You were chosen not for comfort but for impact. Not for applause but for obedience. Not to echo the world but to carry the sound of Heaven.
