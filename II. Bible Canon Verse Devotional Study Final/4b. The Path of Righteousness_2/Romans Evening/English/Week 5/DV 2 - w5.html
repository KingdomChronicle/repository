Romans 3:27-31

NIV (New International Version)

RIGHTEOUSNESS THROUGH FAITH

27 Where, then, is boasting? It is excluded. Because of what law? The law that requires works? No, because of the law that requires faith. 28 For we maintain that a person is justified by faith apart from the works of the law. 29 Or is God the God of Jews only? Is he not the God of Gentiles too? Yes, of Gentiles too, 30 since there is only one God, who will justify the circumcised by faith and the uncircumcised through that same faith. 31 Do we, then, nullify the law by this faith? Not at all! Rather, we uphold the law.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After Paul asked whether humanity has anything to boast about, his answer is clear and direct:

“No! Rather, it is because of our faith in Christ.”

This shows that a person’s relationship with God is not built on personal ability, but on trusting in what Christ has done.

Human thinking naturally tends to believe that acceptance before God must be earned. When we do more good things, we may think we are more deserving. When our spiritual lives seem stronger, we can begin to feel closer to God than others.

But Paul challenges this way of thinking.

Salvation is not a reward for the best or strongest in faith. Neither is it a prize for the person who has done the most right things.

In fact, no one can stand before God and say:

“You accepted me because what I did was enough.”

The reason for our acceptance is Christ alone.

Through His life, death, and resurrection, He accomplished what humanity could never do.

And our part is not to pay for it or add our own ability to it—our part is to believe.

Faith is not merely believing that Jesus is real.

It is trusting with all our hearts that what He has done is enough.

  • His sacrifice is enough.
  • His grace is enough.
  • His love is enough to bring us near to God.

CONCLUSION:

Faith in Christ removes every reason for pride and brings us into a place of humility and gratitude.

Salvation does not rest on the strength of our works, but on God’s faithfulness and on what Jesus has done for us.

In the end, the question is not how much we have done for God, but this:

is our trust truly centered on Christ, or are we still depending on our own abilities?
