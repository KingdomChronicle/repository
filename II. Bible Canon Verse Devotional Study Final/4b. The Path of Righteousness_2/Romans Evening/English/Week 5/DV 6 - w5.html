Romans 3:27-31

NIV (New International Version)

RIGHTEOUSNESS THROUGH FAITH

27 Where, then, is boasting? It is excluded. Because of what law? The law that requires works? No, because of the law that requires faith. 28 For we maintain that a person is justified by faith apart from the works of the law. 29 Or is God the God of Jews only? Is he not the God of Gentiles too? Yes, of Gentiles too, 30 since there is only one God, who will justify the circumcised by faith and the uncircumcised through that same faith. 31 Do we, then, nullify the law by this faith? Not at all! Rather, we uphold the law.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

As Paul concludes Romans 3, he addresses another question that may naturally arise in the minds of his listeners:

If people are saved through faith and not through the Law, does that mean the Law no longer has value?

And Paul immediately gives his answer:

“Certainly not!”

Some may think that because salvation comes through faith, God’s commands are no longer important. It can sound as if faith has replaced the Law and completely removed it.

But that is not what Paul is saying.

Christ did not come to abolish the purpose of the Law. Instead, He revealed its true purpose.

  • The Law was not given to save people.
  • It was given to show people their condition.

The Law revealed how high God’s standard is and how deeply we need a Savior.

In simple terms, the Law itself was never the problem.

The problem is humanity’s inability to obey it perfectly.

And this is where Christ’s work comes in.

Through Jesus, the Law does not lose its meaning—instead, its value becomes even clearer.

Because it shows us why we need grace.

When we truly place our faith in Christ, we are not set free to ignore God’s will.

Instead, we receive a new heart that desires to obey Him.

We do not obey in order to be saved.

We obey because we have already been saved.

CONCLUSION:

Faith is not a reason to minimize or disregard God’s commands.

Rather, genuine faith leads us into a deeper understanding of His will.

The Law reveals our need, and Christ provides the answer.

In the end, the question is not whether the Law is still necessary, but this:

is our obedience to God driven only by fear and obligation, or by a heart transformed by His grace?
