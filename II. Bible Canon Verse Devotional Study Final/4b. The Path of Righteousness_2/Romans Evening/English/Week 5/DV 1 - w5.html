Romans 3:27-31

NIV (New International Version)

RIGHTEOUSNESS THROUGH FAITH

27 Where, then, is boasting? It is excluded. Because of what law? The law that requires works? No, because of the law that requires faith. 28 For we maintain that a person is justified by faith apart from the works of the law. 29 Or is God the God of Jews only? Is he not the God of Gentiles too? Yes, of Gentiles too, 30 since there is only one God, who will justify the circumcised by faith and the uncircumcised through that same faith. 31 Do we, then, nullify the law by this faith? Not at all! Rather, we uphold the law.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After Paul revealed God’s great plan of salvation through Jesus Christ, he raises an important question:

“What then can we boast about?”

And he immediately gives the answer:

“Nothing!”

This represents a major shift in the way people naturally think. Human beings often look for something they can take pride in. We may boast in our goodness, obedience to rules, knowledge of God, or spiritual activities.

But Paul says that when it comes to salvation, none of these things can become a basis for pride.

Why?

Because salvation is not obtained through our own obedience to the Law.

If salvation were the result of personal effort, then people would have something to boast about. They could say:

  • “I am better than others.”
  • “I am more obedient.”
  • “I am more deserving.”

But that is not God’s way.

Salvation is given through faith in Christ and through what He accomplished on the cross.

This means that all people stand on equal ground before God—no one can come with pride because everyone depends entirely on grace.

This is what Paul calls the “law of faith.”

It is not a system of earning points through good works, but a life of trusting in what God has done for us.

So at the foot of the cross, every reason for boasting disappears.

Because the truth is clear:

  • God made the way,
  • God gave the grace,
  • and God brought salvation.

CONCLUSION:

When we truly understand the grace of God, pride begins to disappear and is replaced with gratitude.

We have nothing to boast about regarding our salvation, because we did not earn it.

Everything we have is the result of God’s goodness and mercy.

In the end, the question is not what we can boast about, but this:

is our confidence placed in our own abilities, or in the grace of God given through Jesus Christ?
