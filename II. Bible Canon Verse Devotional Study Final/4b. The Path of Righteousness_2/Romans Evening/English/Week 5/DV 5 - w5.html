Romans 3:27-31

NIV (New International Version)

RIGHTEOUSNESS THROUGH FAITH

27 Where, then, is boasting? It is excluded. Because of what law? The law that requires works? No, because of the law that requires faith. 28 For we maintain that a person is justified by faith apart from the works of the law. 29 Or is God the God of Jews only? Is he not the God of Gentiles too? Yes, of Gentiles too, 30 since there is only one God, who will justify the circumcised by faith and the uncircumcised through that same faith. 31 Do we, then, nullify the law by this faith? Not at all! Rather, we uphold the law.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

As Paul continues his explanation, he clearly says:

“He is also the God of the Gentiles, since there is only one God.”

During Paul’s time, many Jews believed that God belonged only to their nation. Because they had received God’s Law and His promises, they could easily think that they had a special claim on Him.

But Paul reminds them of an important truth:

there is only one God.

And because there is only one God, there is also one standard for all people.

There is not one God for the Jews and another God for the Gentiles.

There is not one way of salvation for one group and a different way for another.

Everyone will stand before the same God, and everyone is offered the same way to be declared righteous—through faith.

This is important because it shows that no one has a special advantage when it comes to salvation.

Being part of a certain family, religion, or tradition is not enough.

Neither is the number of years spent in church nor the amount of good works a person has done.

The true basis for God’s acceptance is not who we are, but whom we trust.

And God’s invitation is open to everyone.

  • Whether Jew or Gentile.
  • Rich or poor.
  • High or low in social status.

Everyone is invited to come through faith in Jesus Christ.

CONCLUSION:

There is only one God, and there is also only one way of salvation.

We are not saved by our background, status, or personal ability.

We are brought near to God through faith in Christ.

At the foot of the cross, all human distinctions disappear, and one truth remains:

everyone needs grace, and that grace is available to all.

In the end, the question is not who we are or where we belong, but this:

is our faith truly centered on Jesus Christ?
