Romans 4:9-12

NIV (New International Version)

Faith First before Circumcision

9 Is this blessedness only for the circumcised, or also for the uncircumcised? We have been saying that Abraham’s faith was credited to him as righteousness. 10 Under what circumstances was it credited? Was it after he was circumcised, or before? It was not after, but before! 11 And he received circumcision as a sign, a seal of the righteousness that he had by faith while he was still uncircumcised. So then, he is the father of all who believe but have not been circumcised, in order that righteousness might be credited to them. 12 And he is then also the father of the circumcised who not only are circumcised but who also follow in the footsteps of the faith that our father Abraham had before he was circumcised.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Outward signs are not the source of salvation—they are simply the result of genuine faith.

In the previous verses, Paul showed that Abraham was declared righteous by God before he was circumcised. Now he explains the true purpose of circumcision.

  • It was not the reason for his righteousness.
  • It was not what saved him.
  • It was not what gave him a right standing before God.

Instead, circumcision became a sign of something he already possessed—the righteousness that came through faith.

In other words, Abraham was not declared righteous because he was circumcised.

He was circumcised because he had already been declared righteous by God.

This distinction is extremely important.

People often think that religious activities are the reason God accepts them. But Abraham’s example shows that God’s acceptance came before any outward sign of obedience.

  • Faith came before the sign.
  • Relationship came before the ritual.
  • Grace came before works.

This is also the principle of the gospel.

We are not saved by baptism, ministry service, church attendance, or any good works.

These things are important, but they are not the basis of our salvation.

They are the fruit of a heart that has placed its faith in Christ.

When faith is genuine, it will be visible in a person’s life.

  • There will be obedience.
  • There will be transformation.
  • There will be outward evidence of a heart that has been changed by God.

But these signs are not the root of salvation—they are only its fruit.

This passage also reveals the greatness of God's plan.

Because Abraham was declared righteous before he was circumcised, he became an example not only for the Jews but also for all people who would believe in God.

This means that the door of salvation is not based on race, tradition, or religious identity.

It is open to everyone who comes to God through faith.

In our time, it is important that we examine ourselves.

Are we trusting in what we do for God?

Or are we trusting in what Christ has done for us?

True faith is not centered on human effort but on the grace of God.

And when we understand this, our obedience becomes a response of gratitude rather than an attempt to earn salvation.

CONCLUSION

Abraham’s circumcision was not the cause of his righteousness.

It was simply a sign of the righteousness he had already received through faith.

The same truth applies to us today.

We are not saved because of our works, rituals, or religious efforts.

We are saved through faith in God and in what Jesus Christ has done for us.

And when that faith is genuine, it will be seen in a life that is willing to obey.

In the end, the question is not how much we do for God.

The question is this:

Are the things we do an attempt to earn salvation, or are they the fruit of a faith that has already received the grace of God?
