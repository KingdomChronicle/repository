Romans 4:9-12

NIV (New International Version)

9 Is this blessedness only for the circumcised, or also for the uncircumcised? We have been saying that Abraham’s faith was credited to him as righteousness. 10 Under what circumstances was it credited? Was it after he was circumcised, or before? It was not after, but before! 11 And he received circumcision as a sign, a seal of the righteousness that he had by faith while he was still uncircumcised. So then, he is the father of all who believe but have not been circumcised, in order that righteousness might be credited to them. 12 And he is then also the father of the circumcised who not only are circumcised but who also follow in the footsteps of the faith that our father Abraham had before he was circumcised.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After showing that a person is declared righteous through faith and not through works, Paul now raises another important question:

Who is this blessing for?

The blessing he is referring to is the forgiveness of sins and being counted righteous before God.

In Paul's day, many Jews believed that God's promises and blessings belonged exclusively to them as God's chosen people. Because of circumcision and their identity as part of Israel, some thought they held a special standing before God that others did not.

But Paul's answer is clear:

"No! It is also for the uncircumcised."

In other words, God's grace is not limited to one race, culture, or group of people.

Forgiveness is not only for the Jews.

Salvation is not only for those with a particular religious identity.

God's grace is available to all who come to Him in faith.

This is one of the most beautiful truths of the gospel.

God does not measure people by their background, social status, education, or ethnicity.

He looks at the heart that trusts in Him.

To prove this point, Paul returns to the example of Abraham.

He shows that Abraham was declared righteous by God before he was circumcised.

This means that faith came first, not the outward sign of circumcision.

Because of this, it becomes clear that God's blessing is based not on rituals or traditions, but on faith.

This lesson is important for us today as well.

Sometimes we fall into the mistaken belief that God loves someone more because they have been a Christian longer, know more about the Bible, or are more active in ministry.

But salvation is not based on external things.

We all come to God the same way—through faith in Jesus Christ.

There is no special group that has exclusive access to His grace.

CONCLUSION:

The gospel is good news for everyone.

It is not limited to one race, nation, or type of person.

God's grace is open to all who place their faith in Him.

Just as God accepted Abraham because of his faith, He accepts every person who comes to Him today in faith.

In the end, the question is not whether we belong to the right group or religious tradition, but this:

Is our faith truly centered on Jesus Christ, who is the way of blessing and salvation for all people?
