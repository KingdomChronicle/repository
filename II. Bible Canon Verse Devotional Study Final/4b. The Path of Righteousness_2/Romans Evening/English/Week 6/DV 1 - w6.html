Romans 4:1-8

NIV (New International Version)

Abraham Justified by Faith

1What then shall we say that Abraham, our forefather according to the flesh, discovered in this matter? 2 If, in fact, Abraham was justified by works, he had something to boast about—but not before God. 3 What does Scripture say? “Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness.”

4 Now to the one who works, wages are not credited as a gift but as an obligation. 5 However, to the one who does not work but trusts God who justifies the ungodly, their faith is credited as righteousness. 6 David says the same thing when he speaks of the blessedness of the one to whom God credits righteousness apart from works:

7 “Blessed are those whose transgressions are forgiven, whose sins are covered.8 Blessed is the one whose sin the Lord will never count against them.”

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After explaining that a person is justified by faith and not by observing the Law, Paul now addresses an important question:

What about Abraham?

As the father of the nation of Israel, Abraham was one of the most revered figures in Jewish history. If anyone could be considered an example of righteousness before God, surely it would be Abraham.

That is why Paul asks:

“What did Abraham discover concerning this matter?”

In other words,

  • how did Abraham become righteous in God's sight?
  • Was he declared righteous because of what he had done?
  • Because of his obedience?
  • Or was there a deeper reason?

As we enter Romans 4, Paul begins to show that God's way of salvation is not something new. It did not begin only with Jesus or only in the New Testament.

Even Abraham, who is known as the father of faith, was accepted by God not because of his own goodness but because of his faith.

This is important to understand because we often assume that the great men and women of the Bible were accepted by God because they had very few flaws.

But the truth is that they, too, needed God's grace.

Abraham was not great because he was perfect.

He became an example because he learned to trust God.

This is exactly what Paul wants his readers to understand:

salvation has always been based on faith, not on human ability or achievement.

CONCLUSION

When we look at the heroes of faith, it is easy to think that they were accepted by God because of what they accomplished.

Yet Abraham's life shows us that the foundation of a relationship with God is not personal righteousness but faith.

God is not looking for perfect people.

He is looking for hearts that are willing to trust Him.

In the end, the question is not how much we can do for God, but this:

Like Abraham, are we willing to trust God even when we cannot yet see the full picture of His plan?
