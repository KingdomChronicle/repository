Romans 4:1-8

NIV (New International Version)

Abraham Justified by Faith

1What then shall we say that Abraham, our forefather according to the flesh, discovered in this matter? 2 If, in fact, Abraham was justified by works, he had something to boast about—but not before God. 3 What does Scripture say? “Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness.”

4 Now to the one who works, wages are not credited as a gift but as an obligation. 5 However, to the one who does not work but trusts God who justifies the ungodly, their faith is credited as righteousness. 6 David says the same thing when he speaks of the blessedness of the one to whom God credits righteousness apart from works:

7 “Blessed are those whose transgressions are forgiven, whose sins are covered.8 Blessed is the one whose sin the Lord will never count against them.”

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After using Abraham as an example of faith that God counts as righteousness, Paul now turns to David—another great figure in Israel’s history.

Through David’s words, Paul demonstrates that this truth has been taught in Scripture all along:

a person is not made righteous because of his works, but because of the grace of God.

David says that blessed is the person whose transgressions have been forgiven and whose sins have been covered.

Notice what he does not say:

“Blessed is the person who has never sinned.”

Neither does he say:

“Blessed is the person who lives a perfect life.”

Instead, he says:

“Blessed is the person who has been forgiven.”

This distinction is extremely important.

All people have sinned.

All have fallen short of the glory of God.

Yet there are those who have experienced a great blessing—the forgiveness of God.

Forgiveness is not a reward for the good.

It is a gift of grace given to those who place their faith in God.

In fact, David himself experienced this reality.

He knew what it felt like to be burdened by sin and to realize that no amount of personal effort could remove it.

He also knew the joy of being forgiven by God.

So when David says, “Blessed,” it is not merely a theological statement.

It is the testimony of a man who personally experienced the mercy and grace of God.

David goes on to say:

“Blessed is the one whose sin the Lord will never count against them.”

This does not mean that God ignored sin.

Nor does it mean that He simply forgot about it.

Rather, the penalty for sin was placed upon another.

We now know that Jesus Christ bore that penalty on the cross.

Because of this, those who trust in Him will no longer be condemned for their sins.

Their guilt has been forgiven, and their debt has been paid in full.

This is why the gospel is truly good news.

It is not about how we can erase our past.

It is about how God has removed our guilt through His grace.

CONCLUSION

The greatest blessing in life is not wealth, success, or fame.

It is the assurance that we have been forgiven by God.

The forgiven person is free from the burden of guilt and has peace in his relationship with God.

This is the grace that Abraham and David received, and it is the same grace that God offers to all who place their faith in Jesus Christ.

In the end, the question is not whether our good works are sufficient to erase our sins, but this:

Have we received by faith the forgiveness that God freely offers through Jesus Christ?
