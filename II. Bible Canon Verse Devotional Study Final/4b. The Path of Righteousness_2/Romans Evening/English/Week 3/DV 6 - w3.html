Romans 3:20

NIV (New International Version)

No One Is Righteous

9 What shall we conclude then? Do we have any advantage? Not at all! For we have already made the charge that Jews and Gentiles alike are all under the power of sin. 10 As it is written:

“There is no one righteous, not even one;11 there is no one who understands; there is no one who seeks God.12 All have turned away, they have together become worthless;there is no one who does good, not even one.”13 “Their throats are open graves; their tongues practice deceit.”“The poison of vipers is on their lips.”14 “Their mouths are full of cursing and bitterness.”15 “Their feet are swift to shed blood;16 ruin and misery mark their ways,17 and the way of peace they do not know.”18 “There is no fear of God before their eyes.”

19 Now we know that whatever the law says, it says to those who are under the law, so that every mouth may be silenced and the whole world held accountable to God. 20 Therefore no one will be declared righteous in God’s sight by the works of the law; rather, through the law we become conscious of our sin.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In the final part of his argument, Paul presents a truth that is difficult for human pride to accept:

no one is declared righteous before God by observing the Law.

This means that no matter how strict a person tries to be, no matter how many good works are done, it is still not enough to make a person righteous before God’s perfect standard.

This is not because the Law is flawed, but because a sinful human being cannot perfectly fulfill it.

Instead of becoming a means to achieve self-righteousness, the Law has a different purpose:

to make people aware of sin.

It functions like a mirror—it does not change the person’s appearance, but it reveals what is truly there.

When a person looks into God’s Law, what they see is not perfection, but their own shortcomings.

And the result is not pride, but the recognition of need for grace.

Here Paul brings his argument about the Law to its conclusion:

it is not a savior, but a revealer of sin.

But it is important to remember that God’s message does not end here.

CONCLUSION:

The Law was not given to provide human righteousness, but to reveal human need for God.

When our shortcomings become clear, it also becomes clear that we cannot save ourselves.

And that is where true hope begins—not in human effort, but in God’s action.

In the end, the question is not how well we can meet the standard, but this:

are we willing to accept that righteousness is not something we can achieve, but something we must receive from God?
