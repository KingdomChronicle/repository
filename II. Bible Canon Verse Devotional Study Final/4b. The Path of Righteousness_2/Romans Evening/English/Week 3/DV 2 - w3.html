Romans 3:10-12

NIV (New International Version)

No One Is Righteous

9 What shall we conclude then? Do we have any advantage? Not at all! For we have already made the charge that Jews and Gentiles alike are all under the power of sin. 10 As it is written:

“There is no one righteous, not even one;11 there is no one who understands; there is no one who seeks God.12 All have turned away, they have together become worthless; there is no one who does good, not even one.”13 “Their throats are open graves; their tongues practice deceit.”“The poison of vipers is on their lips.”14 “Their mouths are full of cursing and bitterness.”15 “Their feet are swift to shed blood;16 ruin and misery mark their ways,17 and the way of peace they do not know.”18 “There is no fear of God before their eyes.”

19 Now we know that whatever the law says, it says to those who are under the law, so that every mouth may be silenced and the whole world held accountable to God. 20 Therefore no one will be declared righteous in God’s sight by the works of the law; rather, through the law we become conscious of our sin.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

At this point, Paul is no longer simply building an argument—he is confirming it directly from Scripture.

This is not a new idea. It is not merely Paul’s opinion. It is a long-established truth revealed in God’s Word, as seen in passages like Psalm 14.

The weight of the statement is unavoidable:

“There is no one righteous, not even one.”

This shows that the problem of sin is not only about actions, but about the condition of all humanity.

And the description goes deeper:

“There is no one who understands; there is no one who seeks God.”

Here we see the root of humanity’s spiritual condition. The issue is not only that people make wrong choices—it is that the human heart is naturally turned away from God.

Humanity, in its natural state, does not actively seek God. This is not because God is hidden, but because the human heart is not oriented toward Him.

Sin, therefore, is not merely external behavior—it is an internal direction of the heart and mind.

So even religious knowledge or awareness of God does not automatically mean a person is truly coming near to Him.

This reveals an important truth in the gospel:

coming to God does not begin with man—it begins with God Himself working in the heart of man.

If no one naturally seeks God, then every genuine movement toward Him is the result of His grace and initiative.

CONCLUSION:

God’s Word is clear: no one is righteous, and no one naturally seeks Him.

This is not meant to degrade humanity, but to reveal the truth that prepares us for grace.

Because if no one can seek God on their own, then salvation cannot begin with human effort—it must begin with God’s mercy.

In the end, the question is not how well we search for God, but this:

are we asking God to turn our hearts back to Him?
