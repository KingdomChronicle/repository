Romans 3:15-18

NIV (New International Version)

No One Is Righteous

9 What shall we conclude then? Do we have any advantage? Not at all! For we have already made the charge that Jews and Gentiles alike are all under the power of sin. 10 As it is written:

“There is no one righteous, not even one;11 there is no one who understands; there is no one who seeks God.12 All have turned away, they have together become worthless;there is no one who does good, not even one.”13 “Their throats are open graves; their tongues practice deceit.”“The poison of vipers is on their lips.”14 “Their mouths are full of cursing and bitterness.”15 “Their feet are swift to shed blood;16 ruin and misery mark their ways,17 and the way of peace they do not know.”18 “There is no fear of God before their eyes.”

19 Now we know that whatever the law says, it says to those who are under the law, so that every mouth may be silenced and the whole world held accountable to God. 20 Therefore no one will be declared righteous in God’s sight by the works of the law; rather, through the law we become conscious of our sin.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this section, Paul continues exposing the condition of humanity using the imagery of a “journey” of life—from speech, to actions, and now to the direction of one’s entire way of living.

In Scripture, “feet” often symbolize our actions and the paths we choose to walk.

And according to Paul, the natural path of humanity is not toward peace, but toward destruction.

“Their feet are swift to shed blood.”

This shows that when a person is separated from God, it becomes easier to disregard the value of human life. Sin is no longer just in thoughts or words—it becomes visible in actions that harm and destroy others.

“Ruin and misery mark their ways.”

The image is heavy: wherever sin leads, it leaves behind damage, pain, and brokenness. This is not a neutral path—it is a way that affects everyone it touches.

“And the way of peace they do not know.”

Here we see a deeper problem: humanity does not merely reject peace—it does not naturally understand or recognize it.

Peace is not the natural outcome of a sinful heart. It disappears when God is removed from the center of life.

And at the root of all this is the final statement:

“There is no fear of God before their eyes.”

The absence of true reverence for God opens the door to all distorted directions in life. When fear of God is gone, moral boundaries weaken, and human life becomes disordered.

CONCLUSION:

The picture Paul presents is not only about “evil people,” but about the true condition of all humanity apart from God. These verses reveal that sin is not merely a mistake people occasionally commit—it is deeply embedded in human nature.

When a person is disconnected from God, it becomes very easy to be carried away by sin. The mind becomes deceived, the heart drifts away, and one’s steps eventually lead toward destruction. That is why the Lord said:

“...for apart from Me you can do nothing.” — John 15:5

In our own strength, we are incapable of living in true righteousness. The human heart is easily deceived and easily pulled away from God. As Proverbs also says:

“There is a way that seems right to a person, but its end is the way to death.” — Proverbs 14:12

Because of this, we come to understand even more how desperately we need God’s mercy, grace, and constant guidance every single day. Human discipline and good intentions alone are not enough, because our sinful nature naturally pulls us back toward sin whenever we drift away from Him.

Therefore, the prayer of every believer should not only be,

“Lord, forgive me,”

but more on:

“Lord, keep me. Do not allow me to be carried away by evil. Guide every step I take in every day, hour, minute, and second of my life.”

And in His goodness, God did not leave us without help. He gave us His Word, His wisdom, and His presence to guide us in the right path and to enable us to live victoriously in a world filled with sin.

In the end, true peace and righteousness are not found in our own strength, but in continually remaining connected to God—the true source of life, wisdom, and holiness.
