Romans 3:19

NIV (New International Version)

No One Is Righteous

9 What shall we conclude then? Do we have any advantage? Not at all! For we have already made the charge that Jews and Gentiles alike are all under the power of sin. 10 As it is written:

“There is no one righteous, not even one;11 there is no one who understands; there is no one who seeks God.12 All have turned away, they have together become worthless;there is no one who does good, not even one.”13 “Their throats are open graves; their tongues practice deceit.”“The poison of vipers is on their lips.”14 “Their mouths are full of cursing and bitterness.”15 “Their feet are swift to shed blood;16 ruin and misery mark their ways,17 and the way of peace they do not know.”18 “There is no fear of God before their eyes.”

19 Now we know that whatever the law says, it says to those who are under the law, so that every mouth may be silenced and the whole world held accountable to God. 20 Therefore no one will be declared righteous in God’s sight by the works of the law; rather, through the law we become conscious of our sin.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this verse, Paul concludes his broad argument about the condition of humanity before God.

The “Law” he refers to is the law given by God to Moses—a holy standard that reveals what is right and wrong. But its purpose is not only to give knowledge; it is to reveal the truth about humanity.

And that truth is heavy:

no one has an excuse before God.

Paul says that the Law speaks to those under it, and its message is clear—it cannot make a person righteous. Instead, it exposes sin.

Rather than being a tool for self-defense, the Law becomes a mirror that shows our true condition.

That is why Paul says it is “so that every mouth may be silenced.”

This means that humanity can no longer rely on self-righteousness, personal effort, or comparison with others as a basis for being right before God.

Instead, the Law leads to one clear conclusion:

the whole world is accountable to God.

Whether Jew or Gentile, religious or not, everyone stands before the same God and the same standard of His holiness.

But the purpose of this truth is not to destroy hope—it is to remove false foundations of human confidence in themselves.

CONCLUSION:

God’s Law was not given to make people righteous by their own effort, but to show that people need God.

When all excuses are removed, one truth remains:

we are all accountable to God.

At that point, no one can hold on to personal goodness—only to the need for grace.

In the end, the question is not how many reasons we have to defend ourselves, but this:

are we willing to stand before God not based on our own righteousness, but on His mercy and grace?
