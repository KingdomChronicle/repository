Romans 3:21-26

NIV (New International Version)

Righteousness Through Faith

21 But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been made known, to which the Law and the Prophets testify. 22 This righteousness is given through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe. There is no difference between Jew and Gentile, 23 for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, 24 and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus. 25 God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his forbearance he had left the sins committed beforehand unpunished— 26 he did it to demonstrate his righteousness at the present time, so as to be just and the one who justifies those who have faith in Jesus.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this passage, Paul explains more clearly how God made a way to forgive humanity’s sin without setting aside His justice.

He says that Christ was

“presented by God as a sacrifice.”

This shows that the cross was not an accident or merely a tragedy—it was part of God’s plan to deal with the problem of sin.

Sin carries a penalty, and that penalty is death. A holy and righteous God cannot simply ignore sin as though it has no consequence.

So instead of leaving humanity under judgment, God gave His own Son as the sacrifice for sin.

“And through the shedding of His blood…”

The blood of Christ represents His life given in place of ours. On the cross, God poured out the full judgment against sin upon Jesus so that those who believe in Him could receive forgiveness.

It is important to see that forgiveness did not come because God merely overlooked sin without cost.

God did not say that sin was “acceptable.”

Instead, He judged sin—but Christ received the judgment.

That is why Paul says God did this to demonstrate His righteousness.

God is both loving and just. He does not ignore sin, yet He also does not leave humanity without hope.

Even the sins committed in earlier times were not forgotten by God. In His patience and perfect timing, the full payment for sin was ultimately placed upon Christ at the cross.

Here we see the depth of the gospel:

God’s mercy and God’s justice are not opposed to one another—they meet together in Jesus.

CONCLUSION:

The cross of Christ proves that God is serious about sin, but also serious about His love for humanity.

God did not ignore our sin—He paid for it through the blood of His Son.

That is why forgiveness is not cheap grace. It is grace purchased at an immeasurable cost.

And anyone who places faith in Christ can come before God no longer carrying condemnation, but forgiveness and new life.

In the end, the question is not whether our own payment for sin is enough, but this:

are we receiving Christ’s sacrifice as the complete payment for our sins?
