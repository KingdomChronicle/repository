Romans 3:21-26

NIV (New International Version)

Righteousness Through Faith

21 But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been made known, to which the Law and the Prophets testify. 22 This righteousness is given through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe. There is no difference between Jew and Gentile, 23 for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, 24 and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus. 25 God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his forbearance he had left the sins committed beforehand unpunished— 26 he did it to demonstrate his righteousness at the present time, so as to be just and the one who justifies those who have faith in Jesus.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After Paul declared that all people have sinned and fallen short of the glory of God, he now reveals humanity’s great hope:

God freely gives righteousness through His grace.

It is not a reward for good people. It is not wages earned through human effort. And it is not something people can achieve through hard work.

Paul says it comes from God’s

“freely given grace.”

That is the meaning of grace—to receive something we do not deserve.

In reality, what humanity deserves because of sin is judgment and separation from God. But instead of giving that, God made a way for people to be declared righteous.

And that way is Christ Jesus.

Paul says that Jesus brings “redemption.” This word carries the deep meaning of paying a price in order to set a captive free.

It reveals that humanity is enslaved to sin and unable to save itself. The penalty of sin is death, and no human being can pay that debt through personal strength.

But Jesus paid the price.

On the cross, He received the punishment that should have been ours so that we could be freed from the power and penalty of sin.

Salvation, therefore, is not simply “forgiveness”—it is redemption purchased at a cost, and Christ Himself paid that cost.

CONCLUSION:

The grace of God is not free because it has no cost—it is free to us because Christ Himself paid its full price and weight.

We are declared righteous not because we became perfect, but because Jesus became our substitute and payment.

And through Him, slaves of sin are offered freedom and new life.

In the end, the question is not whether we can pay for our sins, but this:

will we receive the freedom Christ offers—the freedom He purchased with His own life?
