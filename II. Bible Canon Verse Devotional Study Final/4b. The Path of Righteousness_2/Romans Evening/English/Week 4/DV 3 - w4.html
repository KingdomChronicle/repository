Romans 3:21-26

NIV (New International Version)

Righteousness Through Faith

21 But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been made known, to which the Law and the Prophets testify. 22 This righteousness is given through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe. There is no difference between Jew and Gentile, 23 for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, 24 and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus. 25 God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his forbearance he had left the sins committed beforehand unpunished— 26 he did it to demonstrate his righteousness at the present time, so as to be just and the one who justifies those who have faith in Jesus.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this passage, Paul clearly declares a truth that destroys every form of human pride:

“There is no difference among people.”

Before God, no person is higher, holier, or more deserving of salvation than another. People may differ in status, knowledge, culture, or lifestyle—but when it comes to sin, everyone stands in the same condition.

“For all have sinned.”

Paul did not say that “some” have sinned, but “all.” This means that no one has reached God’s perfect standard.

And the result is clear:

“All fall short of the glory of God.”

The glory of God refers to His holiness, righteousness, and perfect character. And by our own ability, no person can attain it.

Sometimes people measure themselves by comparing their lives with others. If they appear morally better than someone else, they assume that is enough.

But humanity is not God’s standard.

God’s own holiness is the standard.

And when that becomes the measure, it becomes clear that all of us fall short.

This is also why no one can be saved simply through personal goodness. Humanity’s problem is not merely a lack of effort, but the reality that sin is part of the human condition.

Yet in the middle of this heavy truth, the beauty of God’s grace also begins to shine more clearly.

Because if all humanity shares the same condition of sin, then God also offers the same salvation through Christ to all who believe.

CONCLUSION:

Romans 3:23 reminds us that no one can boast before God.

All have fallen short. All are in need of grace.

But this is also the beauty of the gospel—God is not looking for perfect people to save, but for hearts willing to admit their need for His salvation.

In the end, the question is not whether we are better than others, but this:

are we willing to acknowledge our shortcomings and come to Christ, who alone can bring us into the glory of God?
