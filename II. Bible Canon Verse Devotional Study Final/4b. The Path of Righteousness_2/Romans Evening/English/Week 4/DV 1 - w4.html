Romans 3:21-26

NIV (New International Version)

Righteousness Through Faith

21 But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been made known, to which the Law and the Prophets testify. 22 This righteousness is given through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe. There is no difference between Jew and Gentile, 23 for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God, 24 and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus. 25 God presented Christ as a sacrifice of atonement, through the shedding of his blood—to be received by faith. He did this to demonstrate his righteousness, because in his forbearance he had left the sins committed beforehand unpunished— 26 he did it to demonstrate his righteousness at the present time, so as to be just and the one who justifies those who have faith in Jesus.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After Paul laid out the heavy reality of humanity’s sinfulness, there is now a sudden turning point in his message.

Two simple words introduce hope:

“But now…”

In the previous verses, Paul clearly showed that no one can become righteous through personal obedience to the Law. No amount of good works, goodness, or human effort is enough to meet God’s perfect standard.

It seemed as though every path for humanity was closed.

But here, the good news begins.

“But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been made known.”

This reveals that the righteousness humanity desperately needs is no longer something people must achieve through their own strength—it is something God Himself provides.

And Paul makes it clear: “apart from the law.”

This means salvation is not based on humanity’s ability to obey perfectly, but on the way God Himself prepared.

Yet this does not mean that the message contradicts the Law or the Prophets. In fact, Paul says that all Scripture had long been pointing toward this truth.

From the very beginning, it was God’s plan to give righteousness to humanity not through works, but through His own saving action.

Here the heart of the gospel becomes clear: humanity is not saved because it is sufficient, but because God Himself made a way.

CONCLUSION:

The “But now” of Romans 3:21 reminds us that the human story does not end in sin and condemnation.

There is hope because God has done what humanity could never do.

The righteousness we could never attain through our own efforts is the very righteousness God has revealed and provided for us.

That is why salvation is never a reason for human pride, but always a reason for gratitude for the grace of God.

In the end, the question is not whether our works are enough to make us righteous, but this:

are we willing to receive the righteousness that comes from God and not from ourselves?
