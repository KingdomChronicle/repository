Romans 4:18-25

NIV (New International Version)

Abraham's Unwaivering Faith

18 Against all hope, Abraham in hope believed and so became the father of many nations, just as it had been said to him, “So shall your offspring be.” 19 Without weakening in his faith, he faced the fact that his body was as good as dead—since he was about a hundred years old—and that Sarah’s womb was also dead. 20 Yet he did not waver through unbelief regarding the promise of God, but was strengthened in his faith and gave glory to God, 21 being fully persuaded that God had power to do what he had promised. 22 This is why “it was credited to him as righteousness.” 23 The words “it was credited to him” were written not for him alone, 24 but also for us, to whom God will credit righteousness—for us who believe in him who raised Jesus our Lord from the dead. 25 He was delivered over to death for our sins and was raised to life for our justification.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this passage, Paul highlights one of the strongest characteristics of true faith—remaining steadfast in trusting God through a long season of waiting.

“Yet he did not waver through unbelief regarding the promise of God.”

This does not mean that Abraham’s waiting was easy. In fact, more than twenty-five years passed between the giving of the promise and its fulfillment. As time went on, the situation appeared even more impossible from a human perspective.

Yet instead of growing weaker, his faith became stronger.

This is the opposite of the natural human response. When waiting becomes prolonged, doubt often grows deeper. When nothing seems to change, trust gradually weakens. It is not always the problem itself that damages faith, but the length of time a promise remains unfulfilled.

But Abraham did not surrender to this way of thinking. Instead of drifting away from God, he drew nearer to Him in faith.

“And gave glory to God.”

Here we see the depth of his trust. He did not merely wait patiently—he continued to worship even before he saw the fulfillment of the promise.

Many people are able to praise God after receiving an answer. But Abraham’s faith praised God even before the answer came.

This shows that his trust was not based on results, but on the character of God. He believed that God is faithful, good, and unchanging regardless of his circumstances.

This is also the challenge for us today.

When God seems silent, does our trust remain?

When the answer has not yet come, does our praise continue?

True faith does not remain only in seasons of joy; it also remains steadfast in seasons of waiting.

CONCLUSION:

God chose righteousness and the fulfillment of His promises to be received through faith so that it would be clearly seen that salvation does not depend on human speed, the strength of one's endurance, or the ability to control circumstances, but on the faithfulness of God who fulfills His Word.

Abraham was not considered righteous because the promise was fulfilled quickly, but because he continued trusting God even as many years passed and his situation became increasingly impossible from a human perspective. His faith was not broken by waiting; rather, it was strengthened through it.

No one can claim to be considered righteous through personal effort.

No one can boast that they received God’s promises because of their own ability or perseverance.

Everything depends on the God who never changes and never fails in His Word.

And the same faith that connected Abraham to the righteousness of God is the faith appointed for us today—the faith in God who raised Jesus Christ our Lord from the dead, the One who was delivered over to death for our sins and raised to life for our justification.

Therefore, righteousness, forgiveness, and new life are not rewards for those who have waited the longest, but gifts given to those who believe in the God who is faithful to His promise.

In the end, the question is not how long we have been waiting or how great our need may be.

The question is:

Do we continue to trust in the unchanging God—the God who fulfills His promises in His perfect time through Jesus Christ our Lord?
