Romans 4:13-17

NIV (New International Version)

The Promise Comes Through Faith

13 It was not through the law that Abraham and his offspring received the promise that he would be heir of the world, but through the righteousness that comes by faith. 14 For if those who depend on the law are heirs, faith means nothing and the promise is worthless, 15 because the law brings wrath. And where there is no law there is no transgression.

16 Therefore, the promise comes by faith, so that it may be by grace and may be guaranteed to all Abraham’s offspring—not only to those who are of the law but also to those who have the faith of Abraham. He is the father of us all. 17 As it is written: “I have made you a father of many nations.” He is our father in the sight of God, in whom he believed—the God who gives life to the dead and calls into being things that were not.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

God's promises are not based on man's ability to obey perfectly, but on His grace, which is received through faith.

In this passage, Paul continues his explanation of God's promise to Abraham. He makes it clear that if receiving God's promises depended solely on perfect obedience to the Law, then faith would be meaningless and the promise itself would lose its value.

Why?

Because no human being is capable of obeying the Law perfectly.

From the earlier chapters of Romans, Paul has already demonstrated that all have sinned and have fallen short of God's standard. If salvation and God's promises were available only to those who perfectly obeyed the Law, not a single person would qualify to receive them.

In such a situation, faith would become useless because acceptance would be based on human performance rather than trust in God.

But this is not God's plan.

Beginning with Abraham, God already showed that His promises are received through faith. Abraham believed God before he saw the fulfillment of what had been promised to him.

His relationship with God did not begin with obedience to commandments but with trust in God's Word.

This is the same truth we need to understand today.

Many people think they must first become good enough before God will accept them. They believe they need to fix all their mistakes before they can come to Him.

But the gospel teaches the opposite.

  • God does not accept us because we have become perfect.
  • God accepts us because we trust in Christ, who fulfilled what we could never fulfill.

This does not mean that obedience to God is unimportant. Rather, obedience is the result of faith and not the means of obtaining salvation.

  • Faith is the root.
  • Obedience is the fruit.

When we reverse this order, we lose sight of the beauty of God's grace and replace it with confidence in ourselves.

CONCLUSION

If God's promises were based solely on perfect obedience to the Law, none of us would have any hope.

But thanks be to God, His promises rest on His faithfulness and are received through faith.

  • We are not saved by our works.
  • We are not accepted by God because we deserve it.
  • We are accepted because of Jesus Christ, and through faith in Him we become partakers of God's promises.

In the end, the question is not whether we have done enough for God.

The question is:

Is our trust in the God who fulfills His promises enough?
