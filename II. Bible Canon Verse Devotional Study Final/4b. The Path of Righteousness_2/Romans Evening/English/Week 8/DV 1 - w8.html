Romans 4:13-17

NIV (New International Version)

The Promise Comes Through Faith

13 It was not through the law that Abraham and his offspring received the promise that he would be heir of the world, but through the righteousness that comes by faith. 14 For if those who depend on the law are heirs, faith means nothing and the promise is worthless, 15 because the law brings wrath. And where there is no law there is no transgression.

16 Therefore, the promise comes by faith, so that it may be by grace and may be guaranteed to all Abraham’s offspring—not only to those who are of the law but also to those who have the faith of Abraham. He is the father of us all. 17 As it is written: “I have made you a father of many nations.” He is our father in the sight of God, in whom he believed—the God who gives life to the dead and calls into being things that were not.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

God's promises do not depend on man's ability to obey perfectly, but on God's faithfulness and on the faith that responds to His Word.

In the previous verses, Paul showed that Abraham was declared righteous because of his faith and not because of any work or ritual. Now he turns the discussion to the very promises that God gave to Abraham.

When God called Abraham, the Law had not yet been given to Moses. The nation of Israel had not yet received commandments to obey. Yet God had already promised to bless Abraham and his descendants.

This shows that the foundation of God's relationship with humanity did not begin with the Law but with faith.

When God told Abraham to leave his homeland and follow His guidance, he had not yet seen the fulfillment of the promises.

  • He had no son.
  • He had no nation of his own.
  • There was no visible evidence.

Yet he believed God.

And because he believed, he walked forward with confidence, trusting in promises he could not yet see fulfilled.

Here we find an important truth:

Faith is not merely believing that God exists.

It is trusting His Word even when its fulfillment is not yet visible.

Often, we want to see the results before we obey. We want to see the answer before we trust. But Abraham's example teaches the opposite.

First, he believed.

Then, he saw the fulfillment.

This is also God's way in our lives today.

Salvation is not a reward for successful obedience to the Law. It is not a prize for those who are good enough. It is God's gift to those who trust in Him.

  • God's promises are received through faith.
  • Forgiveness is received through faith.
  • And even eternal life is received through faith in Jesus Christ.

When we look at ourselves, it is easy to become discouraged because we see our shortcomings.

But when we look at God, we see His faithfulness.

Our hope does not depend on our ability to be perfect.

It rests on the God who always keeps His promises.

God's promise to Abraham was not given because of his obedience to the Law, but because of his faith.

The same is true for us today.

God does not save us because our goodness is sufficient, but because we trust in His grace through Jesus Christ.

Faith is the means by which we experience the promises of God.

Therefore, instead of relying on our own abilities, let us learn to depend on the God who never fails to keep His Word.

But God's promise to Abraham was not merely about one family or one nation. Paul says that God promised Abraham and his descendants that they would inherit the whole world. This reveals that God's plan is far greater than many people realize. From the very beginning, God's purpose was for His reign and blessing to reach all nations through Abraham's offspring, a promise ultimately fulfilled in Christ and in His Kingdom.

Not only will God's personal promises surely be fulfilled, but His purposes and plans for peoples, nations, and the entire world will also be fully accomplished because He is faithful to His Word.

Like the faith demonstrated by Abraham, we too are called to trust God even when we have not yet seen the complete fulfillment of all that He has promised.

As citizens of God's Kingdom, we live with faith and hope that His reign continues to be revealed in the world. This is why we pray:

“Your kingdom come, Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.”

By faith, we hold firmly to the assurance that the day will come when this prayer will be completely fulfilled. Yet even now, God is already at work through His people. His reign begins in hearts that submit to Christ, expands into families, churches, communities, towns, provinces, and nations, and reaches even the nations of the world.

As people place their faith in God, obey His Word, and submit to His will, His Kingdom continues to be revealed on earth. From a few it becomes many. From one town it reaches other towns. From one nation it spreads to other nations. All of this unfolds according to God's purpose and promise.

CONCLUSION

We believe this not because of human ability, but because of God's faithfulness. The God who fulfilled His promise to Abraham is the same God who will fulfill all His words to the very end.

The day is coming when the Kingdom of God will be fully revealed, His will shall be done throughout the earth as it is in heaven, and everything He has promised will be accomplished. For the reign of God is not merely a hope or a possibility—it is a certainty. The God who made the promise is faithful, and His Kingdom will surely triumph.

In the end, the question is not how strong we are or how good we are.

The question is:

Are we willing to trust God, live by faith, and take part in His purpose as His Kingdom is progressively revealed until His will is fully accomplished on earth as it is in heaven?
