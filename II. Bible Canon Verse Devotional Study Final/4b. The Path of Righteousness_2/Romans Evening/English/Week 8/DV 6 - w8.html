Romans 4:13-17

NIV (New International Version)

The Promise Comes Through Faith

13 It was not through the law that Abraham and his offspring received the promise that he would be heir of the world, but through the righteousness that comes by faith. 14 For if those who depend on the law are heirs, faith means nothing and the promise is worthless, 15 because the law brings wrath. And where there is no law there is no transgression.

16 Therefore, the promise comes by faith, so that it may be by grace and may be guaranteed to all Abraham’s offspring—not only to those who are of the law but also to those who have the faith of Abraham. He is the father of us all. 17 As it is written: “I have made you a father of many nations.” He is our father in the sight of God, in whom he believed—the God who gives life to the dead and calls into being things that were not.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Faith Is Focused on the Almighty God

Paul not only presents Abraham as the father of all who believe; he also reveals the God in whom Abraham placed his faith. The God Abraham trusted is not limited by circumstances, time, or human ability. He is the God who gives life to the dead and calls into existence things that do not yet exist.

When Abraham received God's promise, there was no visible evidence that it would be fulfilled. He and Sarah were already advanced in age, and it seemed impossible for them to have a child. Yet Abraham chose to trust God rather than what he could see. He placed his faith in the power of God to accomplish the impossible.

God Is Faithful to His Promises

When God told Abraham that he would become the father of many nations, none of those nations yet existed. Nevertheless, God spoke of His promise as though it had already been accomplished. This demonstrates that God's Word is certain and never fails. His promises do not depend on human ability but on His faithfulness and power.

The same is true for us as believers today. There are many times when God's promises may seem distant or impossible in our lives. Yet the God whom Abraham trusted is the same God we serve today—the God who has the power to do what human beings cannot do.

True Faith Trusts Even Before It Sees

Abraham's example teaches us that faith is not merely believing when the outcome is already visible. True faith is trusting God even when the fulfillment has not yet been seen. It is confidence in God's character—that He is powerful, faithful, and incapable of lying.

When our faith is centered on God rather than on our circumstances, we can experience peace and hope even in the midst of situations that seem impossible.

CONCLUSION

Abraham became the father of many nations not because he immediately saw the fulfillment of God's promise, but because he trusted the God who fulfills His Word. The God in whom he believed is the same God we serve today—the God who gives life to seemingly dead hopes and calls into existence things that do not yet exist. Therefore, whatever you may be facing, learn to trust God with all your heart. His power is greater than any impossibility, and His promises will always be fulfilled in His perfect time.

We can also see that God's promise to Abraham—that he would become the father of many nations—is already being fulfilled. Through faith in Jesus Christ, millions of believers from different races, languages, and nations have become part of Abraham's spiritual family. Yet the fulfillment of this promise does not end there. As the gospel continues to spread and the Kingdom of God continues to advance in the hearts of people, throughout families, communities, towns, and nations, the fullness of God's promise becomes increasingly evident.

The day will come when all of God's purposes will be completely fulfilled. His Kingdom will be fully revealed, and His will shall be accomplished throughout all creation. Therefore, as followers of Christ, we are called to live by faith and participate in God's work as we await the complete fulfillment of the prayer our Lord taught us:

“Your kingdom come, Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.” (Matthew 6:10)

May we be like Abraham—steadfast in faith, trusting in God's faithfulness, and living with hope fixed not only on the present but also on the glorious fulfillment of all His promises.
