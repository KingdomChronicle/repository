Romans 4:13-17

NIV (New International Version)

The Promise Comes Through Faith

13 It was not through the law that Abraham and his offspring received the promise that he would be heir of the world, but through the righteousness that comes by faith. 14 For if those who depend on the law are heirs, faith means nothing and the promise is worthless, 15 because the law brings wrath. And where there is no law there is no transgression.

16 Therefore, the promise comes by faith, so that it may be by grace and may be guaranteed to all Abraham’s offspring—not only to those who are of the law but also to those who have the faith of Abraham. He is the father of us all. 17 As it is written: “I have made you a father of many nations.” He is our father in the sight of God, in whom he believed—the God who gives life to the dead and calls into being things that were not.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Law was not given to save humanity, but to reveal our true condition before God.

In the previous verses, Paul explained that God's promises to Abraham were not based on obedience to the Law. If receiving God's promises depended on perfect obedience to the Law, then faith would be meaningless and it would be impossible for anyone to receive those promises.

Now Paul explains why.

The Law does not bring salvation to sinful people. Instead, it reveals their transgressions and their accountability before God.

The more we come to understand God's commands, the more clearly we see how far we fall short of His holy standard.

  • The problem is not the Law.
  • The problem is the sinful nature of humanity.

The Law is holy, righteous, and good. But when it confronts the human heart, it exposes the sin that is already there.

The Law is like a mirror.

It does not cleanse the dirt from a person's face, but it shows where the dirt is.

In the same way, the Law reveals our shortcomings, disobedience, and sin before God.

As a result, the outcome is not justification but judgment.

Not because God desires to destroy humanity, but because His holiness cannot compromise with sin.

This is why the Law cannot be the basis of salvation.

If our hope rests on our ability to obey perfectly, then we have no hope.

We

  • all fall short.
  • We all transgress.
  • We all need grace.

This is where the importance of the gospel becomes even more evident. Before we can fully appreciate the greatness of God's grace, we must first understand the depth of our need for Him.

  • The Law reveals our sin.
  • Christ provides the remedy.

CONCLUSION

The Law was not given as a ladder to salvation.

It was given to show us that we cannot save ourselves.

While the Law exposes our sin, it also points us to our need for Jesus Christ.

When we see our true condition before God, we begin to understand more deeply the magnitude of the grace He has given through His Son.

In the end, the question is not how well we are able to obey every command of God.

The question is:

Will we acknowledge our need for the Savior who fulfilled what we could never fulfill?
