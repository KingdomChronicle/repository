Romans 4:13-17

NIV (New International Version)

The Promise Comes Through Faith

13 It was not through the law that Abraham and his offspring received the promise that he would be heir of the world, but through the righteousness that comes by faith. 14 For if those who depend on the law are heirs, faith means nothing and the promise is worthless, 15 because the law brings wrath. And where there is no law there is no transgression.

16 Therefore, the promise comes by faith, so that it may be by grace and may be guaranteed to all Abraham’s offspring—not only to those who are of the law but also to those who have the faith of Abraham. He is the father of us all. 17 As it is written: “I have made you a father of many nations.” He is our father in the sight of God, in whom he believed—the God who gives life to the dead and calls into being things that were not.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

God's promises are based on faith so that they may be given by grace and not as a reward for human effort.

After showing that the Law cannot be the means by which God's promises are received, Paul now explains why God chose faith as the way of receiving them.

The reason is simple:

so that salvation and God's promises may be gifts of grace.

If receiving God's promises depended on obedience to the Law, no one could have any assurance.

  • Everyone falls short.
  • Everyone sins.
  • Everyone fails.

But because the promise is based on faith, it rests on God's faithfulness rather than on human ability.

This is what gives every believer a firm and secure hope.

  • Our salvation does not fluctuate according to our performance.
  • It does not depend on the number of our good works.
  • It is not founded upon our own righteousness.
  • It rests on the grace of God that we have received through faith in Jesus Christ.

This passage also shows that the family of God is far broader than a single race or nation.

Not only the Jews are counted among Abraham's children.

Included as well are all those who share the same faith that Abraham demonstrated.

Even before the Law was given, Abraham believed God. And because of that faith, he was counted as righteous.

In the same way, every person who comes to God through faith in Christ becomes part of Abraham's spiritual family.

What a wonderful truth this is for us.

God does not bring us near to Himself because of our ancestry, social status, or personal goodness.

He accepts us through faith.

The door of grace stands open to all who believe in Him.

CONCLUSION

God chose faith as the means of receiving His promises so that it would be clear that salvation is a gift of grace.

No one can claim that he deserves it.

No one can boast that he obtained it through his own effort.

Everything is received as a gift from God.

Because of this, the promise is not limited to one group of people but is available to all who believe as Abraham believed.

In the end, the question is not how much we have done for God.

The question is:

Are we trusting in the grace of God that has been freely given through Jesus Christ?
