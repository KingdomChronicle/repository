Proverbs 9:16a

NIV (New International Version)

16“Let all who are simple come to my house!”

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Counterfeit Invitation of Folly

Similar Invitations, Different Destinations

In the first part of Proverbs 9, we saw that Wisdom calls those who lack understanding so that they may learn, grow, and find life.

But in this passage, another voice is calling.

The voice of Folly.

She also says:

👉 "Come here, all you who lack understanding!"

It is striking that her invitation sounds almost identical to the invitation of Wisdom.

  • Both call out.
  • Both extend an invitation.
  • Both seek people who need direction.

Yet their purposes are completely different.

  • Wisdom invites in order to give life.
  • Folly invites in order to bring destruction.

👉 Not every invitation that appears good leads to what is good.

The Enemy Often Imitates the Truth

One of Satan's methods of deception is to imitate the things that come from God.

He does not always appear as obvious evil.

Often, he presents falsehood in a form that looks like truth.

  • He presents what is wrong as right.
  • He presents sin as attractive.
  • He presents danger as safe.

Throughout Scripture, we see that the enemy often uses counterfeit versions of things that genuinely come from God.

The True Lion vs. The Counterfeit Lion

Christ is called the "Lion of Judah."

"Do not weep! See, the Lion of the tribe of Judah, the Root of David, has triumphed." (Revelation 5:5)

The lion here is a picture of Christ's power, victory, and reign.

But Satan is described as "like a roaring lion."

"Your enemy the devil prowls around like a roaring lion looking for someone to devour." (1 Peter 5:8)

Notice that he is not the Lion; he is merely like a lion. Christ's lionhood speaks of authority and victory, while Satan's imitation speaks of intimidation and destruction.

The True Morning Star vs. The Fallen Star

Jesus said:

"I am the Root and the Offspring of David, and the bright Morning Star." (Revelation 22:16)

Christ is the true source of light and hope.

On the other hand, we read:

"How you have fallen from heaven, morning star..." (Isaiah 14:12)

Although the primary context refers to the king of Babylon, it is often used as a picture of Satan's pride and fall.

Christ is the true Morning Star; Satan is a fallen star brought down because of his rebellion against God.

The True Light vs. The Pretended Light

Jesus said:

"I am the light of the world." (John 8:12)

But Paul also wrote:

"Satan himself masquerades as an angel of light." (2 Corinthians 11:14)

He does not come appearing obviously evil. Often he wraps falsehood in a form that seems good and beneficial.

The True Christ vs. False Christs

Jesus said:

"I am the way and the truth and the life." (John 14:6)

But He also warned:

"Many false messiahs and false prophets will appear." (Matthew 24:24)

The enemy even imitates the person of Christ in order to deceive people through false saviors and false gospels.

The Lamb of God vs. The Beast That Looks Like a Lamb

Jesus was called:

"Look, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!" (John 1:29)

Yet Revelation describes a beast:

"It had two horns like a lamb, but it spoke like a dragon." (Revelation 13:11)

It looks like a lamb, but its voice is not from God. It is a picture of deception that has an appearance of godliness while being opposed to the truth.

The Spirit of Truth vs. The Father of Lies

The Holy Spirit is called:

"The Spirit of truth." (John 16:13)

Meanwhile, Jesus said of Satan:

"He is a liar and the father of lies." (John 8:44)

God reveals truth in order to save people. Satan uses lies in order to deceive and destroy people.

We can see that the enemy rarely creates something entirely new. More often, he imitates, distorts, or mixes falsehood with things that genuinely come from God in order to deceive people.

👉 The most dangerous deception is that which appears to be truth but actually leads us away from God.

Many people are led astray not because they sought evil, but because they believed a counterfeit version of the truth.

Folly Entices the Spiritually Unstable

Once again, we see that Folly's targets are the "simple" and the "senseless."

This does not mean they lack intelligence.

Rather, it refers to people who lack spiritual discernment and are easily carried away by whatever they hear.

People who:

  • have no solid foundation in the Word of God
  • are easily influenced
  • follow feelings rather than truth
  • and do not examine what they hear

👉 A person who lacks a firm foundation in the truth can easily be deceived by lies.

This is why continual study of God's Word is so important.

  • Being religious is not enough.
  • Being familiar with Bible verses is not enough.
  • We must grow in a genuine knowledge of God.

Many Voices Compete with the Voice of God

In our day, countless voices call out to us every day.

There are voices that say:

  • "Just follow your heart."
  • "Do whatever makes you happy."
  • "There's nothing wrong with it if no one gets hurt."
  • "Truth is no longer important."

Often, these messages are attractive.

Easy to believe.

Easy to follow.

But not everything popular is right.

Not everything pleasing is beneficial.

👉 Truth is not measured by how many people believe it, but by whether it aligns with the Word of God.

As believers, we must learn to discern which voice comes from God and which comes from the world.

Jesus' Warning About False Teachers

Jesus warned about false prophets.

He said they would come dressed like sheep but inwardly be ferocious wolves.

Outwardly, they may appear good.

They may be gifted speakers.

Their message may be appealing.

But their goal is to lead people away from the truth.

👉 Not everyone who teaches about God is truly teaching the truth of God.

That is why it is essential to examine everything we hear in the light of Scripture.

The Only Safe Guide Is the Word of God

In the midst of so many voices in the world, there is one voice that never changes.

It is the voice of God through His Word.

  • Human opinion changes.
  • Culture changes.
  • Trends change.

But God's truth remains forever.

👉 When our lives are rooted in the Word of God, we will not be easily swept away by the world's counterfeit invitations.

This is why it is important to be diligent in reading, studying, and applying Scripture.

Living It Out: Which Voice Are You Listening To?

Examine yourself:

  • Am I quick to believe everything I hear?
  • Do I measure teachings according to the Word of God?
  • Are there influences in my life that are gradually drawing me away from the Lord?
  • Do I set aside time to grow in the knowledge of God?
  • Which voice do I follow more often—the voice of God or the voice of the world?

Spiritual safety is not only about avoiding sin but also about recognizing the truth.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:16 reminds us that Wisdom is not the only voice calling.

There is also the voice of Folly continually inviting people.

  • Both make offers.
  • Both make promises.

But their destinations are completely different.

  • Wisdom leads to life.
  • Folly leads to destruction.

Therefore, as believers, we must be discerning and remain firmly rooted in the Word of God.

For the enemy rarely comes as obvious evil. More often, he comes disguised as truth in order to deceive those who are not watchful.

The question:

When voices compete with God's truth in your life, are you able to discern which is the voice of Wisdom and which is the voice of Folly?
