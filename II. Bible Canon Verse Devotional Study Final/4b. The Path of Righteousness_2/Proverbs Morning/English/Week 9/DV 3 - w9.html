Proverbs 9:15

NIV (New International Version)

calling out to those who pass by, who go straight on their way,

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Temptation Actively Seeks Its Victims

Folly Does Not Merely Wait

In the previous verses, we saw that Folly sits in the open places of the city, waiting for people to notice her.

But in this verse, we see her purpose more clearly:

👉 "She calls out to every passerby she sees, persistently inviting them to come."

Folly does not merely wait.

She actively seeks.

  • She continually calls out.
  • She continually entices.
  • She continually looks for people she can lead astray.

This is also how the enemy works in our spiritual lives.

  • He never stops.
  • He never rests.
  • He continually looks for opportunities to draw us away from God.

"Be alert and of sober mind. Your enemy the devil prowls around like a roaring lion looking for someone to devour." — 1 Peter 5:8

👉 Temptation does not arrive by accident; it often deliberately seeks our attention.

Even the Righteous Are Not Immune to Its Temptations

It is noteworthy that those being called by Folly are people who are "passing by" and continuing on their way.

This may describe people who are already walking in the right direction.

It reminds us that temptation is not reserved only for the weak.

Even those who follow God are targets of the enemy.

  • Peter was tempted.
  • David was tempted.
  • Even Jesus was tempted by Satan in the wilderness.

Being Tempted Is Not Sin, but It Becomes Dangerous When We Begin to Lean Toward It

👉 The enemy does not choose only certain people to test; even those walking on the right path are among his targets.

Drifting Away Often Begins Small and Usually Goes Unnoticed at First

One clear example of this is Lot.

At first, he did not live in Sodom.

He merely chose to settle near it.

Over time, he eventually found himself living inside the city.

And when God's judgment came, he nearly lost everything.

This is also what happens to many believers.

Typically, drifting away from God does not begin with a major sin.

It begins with small compromises.

  • A little neglect of prayer.
  • A little neglect of God's Word.
  • A little playing around with temptation.
  • A little involvement with things we know do not please the Lord.

Until one day we realize that we have drifted far from the warmth and closeness we once had in our relationship with God.

👉 Many spiritual falls begin with compromises that seem small.

The Enemy Is Looking for Someone to Devour

Peter compared the devil to a roaring lion seeking someone to devour.

This shows that his actions are intentional.

He does not tempt people merely to cause trouble.

  • His goal is to destroy our faith.
  • To weaken our obedience to God.
  • And to draw people away from the will of the Lord.

Satan's goal was more than simply bringing suffering to Job; he wanted to destroy Job's trust in God and lead him into sin. This is also the danger of temptation in our lives. It does not immediately drag us into deep sin but first invites us into small compromises. Yet when these are left unchecked, they can pull our hearts away from God and weaken our faith.

That is why Scripture repeatedly reminds us to remain watchful.

👉 The spiritual life is not a time for sleeping but a time for vigilance.

Fix Your Eyes on Christ

If Folly continues to call out, how can we remain steadfast?

The answer is not merely avoiding what is wrong but continually drawing near to God and His Word.

Like a runner focused on the finish line, we must fix our eyes on the Lord.

When Jesus is the center of our lives:

  • we can more easily recognize deception
  • we can more quickly detect temptation
  • and we can remain steadfast on the right path

👉 The best defense against temptation is a close relationship with Christ.

Living It Out: Are There Voices Pulling You Away?

Examine yourself:

  • Are there things in my life that continually pull me away from God?
  • Are there compromises I allow to remain because I think they are only small matters?
  • Are there influences I should avoid in order to guard my faith?
  • How can I deepen my relationship with Christ each day?
  • Am I watchful, or have I become complacent in my spiritual condition?

The person who guards his heart is better prepared to recognize and reject the invitations of Folly.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:15 reminds us that Folly is not quietly waiting.

It actively calls, entices, and seeks people who can be led astray.

Even those who are walking on the right path are not immune to the enemy's temptations and deceptions.

Therefore, we must not become complacent in our spiritual lives.

Prayer, reading God's Word, fellowship with other believers, and continual obedience to Christ are essential for remaining steadfast.

For drifting away from God rarely happens in an instant.

It begins by listening to the wrong voice, accepting a small compromise, and making room for things that gradually pull us away from the Lord.

But the person who remains focused on Christ will gain the wisdom to recognize the enemy's traps and the strength to remain faithful until the end.

The Question:

When the invitations of the world and of Folly call for your attention, do you remain focused on Christ, or are you gradually being drawn away from the path He has set before you?
