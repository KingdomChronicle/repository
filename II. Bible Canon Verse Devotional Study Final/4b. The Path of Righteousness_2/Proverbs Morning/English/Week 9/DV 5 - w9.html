Proverbs 9:16b-17

NIV (New International Version)

16bTo those who have no sense she says, 17“Stolen water is sweet; food eaten in secret is delicious!”

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Deception of Sin

Sin Often Appears Attractive

As Solomon continues his comparison between Wisdom and Folly, he now describes one of the greatest lies that sin uses to entice people:

👉 "Stolen water is sweet; food eaten in secret is delicious."

This does not mean that stolen things are actually better.

Rather, it reveals how sin deceives the human mind.

There is a peculiar attraction to forbidden things.

There is something within our sinful nature that is drawn to the idea of doing what we know is wrong.

As a result, what is forbidden seems sweeter, more exciting, and more satisfying.

👉 One of sin's most effective weapons is making what is wrong appear better than what is right.

The First Deception in the Garden of Eden

This is exactly what the serpent did to Adam and Eve in the Garden of Eden.

God had given them an abundance of blessings.

They were free to eat from every tree in the garden except one.

Yet that was the very thing the enemy focused their attention on.

He convinced them that God was withholding something good from them.

As though their lives would be better if they disobeyed God's command.

In the end, they chose to take what was forbidden.

And the result was sin, separation from God, and death.

👉 The enemy often tempts us to believe that something better exists outside the will of God.

Sin Promises Pleasure

When temptation comes, it rarely reveals its true outcome immediately.

  • It does not show the pain.
  • It does not show the regret.
  • It does not show the destruction.

Instead, it promises:

  • pleasure
  • freedom
  • power
  • success
  • or temporary happiness

But these promises are only temporary.

Sin is like bait on a hook.

All the fish sees is the food.

It does not see the hook hidden behind it.

👉 Sin always promises more than it can deliver and always leaves a greater cost than we expect.

Hidden Sin Is Not Hidden from God

Solomon also mentions "food eaten in secret."

This carries the idea of something done secretly.

This is another lie of sin.

It convinces us that:

  • "No one will know."
  • "No one is watching."
  • "It's just a secret."

But nothing is hidden from God.

  • He sees everything.
  • He knows everything.
  • He even understands the thoughts of our hearts.

👉 We may hide our sin from people, but we can never hide it from God.

And because God loves us, He will not allow us to remain on a path that leads to destruction.

What God Offers Is Better

It is noteworthy that Folly offers stolen water and stolen bread.

Meanwhile, Wisdom offers a rich and abundant banquet.

  • Food has been prepared.
  • Drink has been prepared.
  • There is enough for everyone.

This is an important picture of the difference between God's will and sin.

❌ Sin steals.

✔️ God provides.

❌ Sin takes.

✔️ God gives.

❌ Sin makes promises but fails to fulfill them.

✔️ God is faithful to all His promises.

👉 What God gives is always far better than anything sin promises.

Christ Is the True Fulfillment of God's Banquet

On a deeper level, the banquet offered by Wisdom also points to Christ.

While Folly offers stolen bread and stolen water, Jesus offers Himself.

At the Last Supper, He took the bread and said:

"This is my body given for you."

He also took the cup and said:

"This is my blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many."

❌ Sin offers temporary pleasure that leads to death.

✔️ But Christ offers His own life so that we may have eternal life.

He is the True Bread of Life.

  • Whoever comes to Him will never hunger.
  • Whoever believes in Him will never thirst.

👉 While the world offers stolen and temporary things, Christ offers grace that truly gives life to the soul.

The Real Problem Is Not Temptation but the Heart

When we are tempted, we often blame the situation.

But this verse shows that the real battle takes place in the heart.

Why does stolen water seem sweet?

Because there is something within our sinful nature that is attracted to it.

Therefore, the solution is not merely avoiding temptation.

We must also allow God to transform our hearts.

The more we love God, the less power temptation has over us.

The more we taste the goodness of the Lord, the less valuable the world's counterfeit offers become.

👉 A heart that is satisfied in God is not easily deceived by the temporary pleasures of sin.

Living It Out: What Seems "Sweet" to You Right Now?

Examine yourself:

  • Are there temptations that I believe will bring me satisfaction?
  • Are there things I do in secret that I know are not pleasing to God?
  • Do I believe that something better exists outside the will of God?
  • Where do I seek the true satisfaction of my soul?
  • Do I value Christ's offers more than the offers of the world?

True freedom is not found in disobeying God but in living according to His will.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:17 exposes one of the enemy's oldest lies:

the belief that what is forbidden is better than what God provides.

Yet from the Garden of Eden until today, sin has repeatedly proven that its sweet promises ultimately end in bitterness and destruction.

Meanwhile, God does not withhold good things from His children. His commands are not meant to take away our joy but to lead us into true life.

Above all, He gave Christ—the Bread of Life and His blood poured out for our salvation—so that we might find in Him the fullness that sin can never provide.

The question:

Are there any "stolen waters" in your life that seem sweet right now but are drawing you away from God? Or have you learned to find in Christ the true satisfaction that the world can never give?
