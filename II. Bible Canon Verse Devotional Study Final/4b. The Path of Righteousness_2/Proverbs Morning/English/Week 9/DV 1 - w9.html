Proverbs 9:13

NIV (New International Version)

Folly is an unruly woman; she is simple and knows nothing.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Folly Has an Attractive Appearance but a Dangerous End

Two Voices Calling

In the first part of Proverbs 9, we saw Wisdom as a noble woman who prepared a house and a banquet to invite people into life and truth.

But in this passage, Solomon introduces the opposite of Wisdom.

The verse says:

👉 "Folly is like a foolish woman—unruly and shameless."

  • If Wisdom invites people to life, Folly invites them to destruction.
  • If Wisdom teaches truth, Folly uses deception.
  • If Wisdom builds up, Folly tears down.

👉 Every day of our lives, there are two voices calling to us—the voice of Wisdom and the voice of Folly.

Folly Is Loud

Solomon described the foolish woman as "unruly."

This means she is loud, impulsive, and lacks self-control.

She does not need truth in order to attract people.

She uses noise, emotions, and outward appearances to draw others in.

This is also how the world operates.

The voice of sin is often louder than the voice of wisdom.

It is

  • quicker to attract attention.
  • easier to notice.
  • more appealing at first glance.

But not everything that glitters is gold.

👉 Truth is often quiet, but deception is often loud.

Folly Has No True Wisdom

Furthermore, the verse says that she is "shameless."

  • This refers not only to a lack of shame but also to a lack of proper understanding.
  • She does not understand the consequences of her actions.
  • She does not value the truth.
  • She does not acknowledge God.

This is a picture of a person who lives according to personal desires rather than according to the will of the Lord.

He may appear happy at first.

He may even seem successful in the eyes of others.

But because he is separated from God's wisdom, his path leads to destruction.

👉 The world's intelligence is not always true wisdom in God's sight.

The Danger of Outward Attraction

Throughout the book of Proverbs, Solomon repeatedly shows that many people fall into temptation because they focus only on what they see outwardly.

This happened to Samson.

A man who was given extraordinary strength by God.

Yet he fell because of his fascination with Delilah.

This also happened to King David.

When he saw Bathsheba, he allowed desire to prevail over obedience to God.

The issue is not merely physical beauty.

The principle extends much further than that.

Many things in the world look beautiful on the outside but are dangerous within.

These may include:

  • wealth obtained through dishonest means
  • relationships that go against God's will
  • popularity that leads people away from the Lord
  • or sins that seem pleasurable at the beginning

👉 Many temptations are pleasing to the eye but bitter in their outcome.

True Beauty Is Found in the Fear of God

Rather than praising outward appearance alone, the Bible teaches:

"Charm is deceptive, and beauty is fleeting; but a woman who fears the Lord is to be praised."

This principle is not only for women but for everyone.

A person's true worth is not measured by appearance, achievements, or popularity.

It is measured by his relationship with God.

👉 True beauty is the fruit of a heart that reveres and obeys the Lord.

Examine Your Own Life

We should not only examine other people.

It is also important to ask:

  • Which voice do I listen to most often?
  • The voice of Wisdom or the voice of the world?
  • Are there things I follow simply because they are attractive?
  • Do I value outward appearances more than God's truth?
  • Do I allow my emotions and desires to lead me rather than the will of the Lord?

👉 Every wrong choice begins with listening to the wrong voice.

Living It Out: Whose Invitation Are You Following?

Examine yourself:

  • Are there temptations in my life that seem appealing but that I know are not God's will?
  • Am I more easily attracted to outward appearances than to the truth?
  • How can I deepen my listening to God's voice?
  • Are there areas of my life that I need to surrender in order to walk in wisdom?

God continues to call us to choose the path of wisdom rather than the path of folly.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:13 reminds us that not every invitation leads to life.

Some voices are attractive to hear but lead to destruction.

Some offers look appealing but conceal ruin.

Folly is often loud, attractive, and easy to follow.

But the Wisdom of God is what truly gives direction, peace, and life.

Therefore, in every decision, every temptation, and every invitation we hear, we must ask:

Does this voice draw me closer to God, or is it only leading me farther away from Him?

For the path of Wisdom may not always be the easiest, but it is the only path that leads to true life.

The question:

When two voices are calling to your heart—the voice of Wisdom and the voice of Folly—which one do you most often listen to and follow?
