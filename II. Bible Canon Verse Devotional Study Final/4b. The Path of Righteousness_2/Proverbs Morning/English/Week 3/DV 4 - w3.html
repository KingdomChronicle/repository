Proverbs 8:14

NIV (New International Version)

Counsel and sound judgment are mine; I have insight, I have power.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom That Gives Strength and Ability

The True Source of Strength

Many people look for influence in:

  • money
  • position
  • connections
  • talent
  • or personal ability

The world often assumes that powerful people are those with wealth and influence.

But this verse shows a different truth:

👉 True strength and ability come from God’s wisdom.

Wisdom does not only give knowledge—it also provides:

  • understanding
  • right direction
  • stability
  • and the ability to live rightly

Reliable and Steady Wisdom

Unlike proud or deceitful people, God’s wisdom is:

  • true
  • reliable
  • and unshakable

Material wealth and human strength can disappear, but God’s wisdom remains.

👉 There are things money simply cannot buy.

Money can buy:

  • comfort
  • possessions
  • temporary security

But it cannot buy:

  • peace of heart
  • true hope
  • right direction
  • or salvation of the soul

That is why Gospel of Mark says:

“What good is it for someone to gain the whole world, yet forfeit their soul?”

Christ: The True Wisdom

Jesus Christ is the perfect expression of God’s wisdom.

He is called in Book of Isaiah:

  • “Wonderful Counselor”
  • “Mighty God”

👉 Jesus knows what is best for us.

When we follow His wisdom:

  • He guides us in the right path
  • He strengthens us in weakness
  • and He uses even difficult situations for our good

As written in Epistle to the Romans:

“In all things God works for the good of those who love Him.”

Strength in Weakness

Having wisdom does not mean life becomes easy.

But a person who has God’s wisdom receives strength to endure trials and difficulties.

Paul the Apostle said:

“I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me.” (Philippians 4:13)

Paul experienced:

  • lack
  • suffering
  • persecution
  • and trials

Yet because of the wisdom and strength from Christ, he remained steadfast.

👉 Strength from God is greater than human ability.

Personal Application: Where Do You Depend?

Examine yourself:

  • Where do I place my security and strength?
  • Do I trust my own ability more than God?
  • Do I ask for God’s wisdom in my decisions?
  • When trials come, where do I hold on to?

A person who relies on themselves becomes easily weak. But a person who relies on God’s wisdom becomes truly steady.

Conclusion

God’s wisdom is not just knowledge—it gives:

  • strength
  • understanding
  • ability
  • and power to live rightly

👉 When we are with God’s wisdom, we have guidance and strength that the world cannot give.

In weakness, chaos, and trials, God continues to give strength to those who trust Him.

The question is:

Are you relying on your own ability—or on the strength and wisdom that comes from God?
