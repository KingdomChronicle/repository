Proverbs 8:15

NIV (New International Version)

By me, kings reign and rulers issue decrees that are just;

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom That Brings Righteous Leadership

God as the Source of True Leadership

This verse shows that wisdom enables kings and leaders to govern properly.

👉 There is no truly good leadership without God’s wisdom.

A person may have authority, but without wisdom:

  • they become self-centered
  • their decisions become impulsive
  • and they may bring harm to others

Position alone is not enough. Wisdom is needed to use authority correctly.

Wisdom Produces Justice

The verse says that because of wisdom, rulers are able to issue “just laws.”

A true leader is not only skilled in governing—they are also:

  • just
  • careful
  • humble
  • and obedient to God’s will

👉 God’s wisdom teaches how to use power for good, not for personal gain.

When leadership lacks wisdom:

  • corruption spreads
  • the weak are oppressed
  • and society is damaged

But when a leader fears God and follows His wisdom, order and justice flourish.

The Example of Solomon

Solomon understood this when he became king of Israel.

He did not ask God for:

  • wealth
  • long life
  • or victory over enemies

Instead, he asked for:

“an understanding heart to govern” (1 Kings 3:9)

Because of this, God granted him extraordinary wisdom.

👉 A good leader depends on God, not just personal ability.

All Authority Comes from God

Epistle to the Romans 13:1 says:

“There is no authority except that which God has established.”

Not all leaders acknowledge this, but all authority ultimately remains under God’s sovereignty.

Throughout history:

  • some leaders obeyed God
  • while others became proud and rebellious

Leaders who reject God’s wisdom often end in failure.

👉 Power without godliness is dangerous.

Not Only for Kings

Although this verse speaks about kings and rulers, its application includes everyone.

In any position:

  • parents
  • leaders
  • teachers
  • employees
  • or managers

We all need God’s wisdom to make right decisions.

Anyone with influence over others needs:

  • right thinking
  • sound judgment
  • and a heart that follows God

Personal Application: How Do You Use Your Influence?

Examine yourself:

  • Do I seek God’s wisdom in my decisions?
  • Do I use my influence for good?
  • Am I just and humble in dealing with others?
  • Do I rely more on my own intelligence than on God’s guidance?

The way you use your authority reveals who truly guides your heart.

Conclusion

True wisdom is not only for personal success—it is also guidance for righteous leadership and justice.

👉 Good leadership begins with a heart submitted to God.

When a person has God’s wisdom, they use authority and influence responsibly and righteously.

The question is:

When God gives you influence or responsibility, do you use it according to His wisdom—or according to your own desires?
