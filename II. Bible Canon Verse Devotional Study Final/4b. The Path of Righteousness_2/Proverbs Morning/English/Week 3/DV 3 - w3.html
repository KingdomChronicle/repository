Proverbs 8:13

NIV (New International Version)

To fear the Lord is to hate evil; I hate pride and arrogance, evil behavior and perverse speech.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

True Reverence for God

What Does It Mean to “Fear” God?

When we hear the word “fear,” we often think of terror or anxiety.

But the “fear of the Lord” mentioned in this verse is not merely being afraid of punishment.

It means:

  • deep reverence
  • recognizing the holiness of God
  • submitting to His will
  • and desiring to live in a way that pleases Him

👉 True reverence for God is shown by turning away from evil.

A person cannot claim to love God while continually embracing sin.

Wisdom Hates Evil

Wisdom clearly says:

“I hate evil…”

Sin is not merely a “mistake” or a “weakness.” It is rebellion against a holy God.

Therefore, a person with true wisdom:

  • does not tolerate wickedness
  • does not delight in sin
  • and does not play with temptation

👉 The deeper our relationship with God becomes, the more we learn to hate sin.

When we remember that Jesus Christ suffered and died because of our sins, we begin to understand how serious sin is in the eyes of God.

The Danger of Pride

Wisdom also hates:

  • pride
  • arrogance
  • and corrupt speech

Pride is one of the most dangerous sins because it draws people away from God.

A proud person:

  • trusts in themselves more than in God
  • refuses correction
  • and thinks they do not need God

👉 Pride was what led Lucifer to rebel against God.

He desired to exalt himself and become like the Most High. (Isaiah 14:12–14)

His heart became filled with pride and deceit.

That is why Jesus Christ said that Satan is “a liar and the father of lies.” (John 8:44)

The Power of Words

The verse also mentions “corrupt speech.”

A wise person is careful with their words.

They are not:

  • destructive
  • deceitful
  • hypocritical
  • or fond of lying

👉 The mouth reflects what fills the heart.

If the heart is filled with wisdom, it will be seen in the way a person speaks.

Personal Application: What Fills Your Heart?

Examine yourself:

  • Are there sins that I continue to tolerate in my life?
  • Am I easily led into pride and self-reliance?
  • Do my words bring life or cause harm?
  • Am I truly living with reverence and obedience to God?

True fear of God is not merely words—it is revealed in the way we live every day.

Conclusion

God’s wisdom does not compromise with evil.

👉 A person who truly reveres God learns to hate sin and love righteousness.

As we draw closer to God, we see more clearly the seriousness of sin and the importance of holy living.

The question is:

Does your life reflect genuine reverence for God—or is it only an outward appearance without real change in the heart and lifestyle?
