Proverbs 8:33

NIV (New International Version)

Listen to my instruction and be wise; do not disregard it.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom Must Be Heard and Held On To

Wisdom Requires a Response

In this verse, wisdom once again calls out:

👉 “Listen to my instruction.”

It is not enough to know what is right. It is not enough to have access to God's Word.

What matters is how we respond to the truth we hear.

Many people have knowledge about God, yet they do not put it into practice.

True wisdom begins when what we hear becomes part of our daily lives.

Do Not Neglect What You Have Learned

The verse also contains an important warning:

“Do not disregard it.”

People do not usually drift away from God overnight.

It happens gradually.

It often begins with:

  • neglecting prayer
  • drifting away from God's Word
  • losing the desire for worship
  • and becoming preoccupied with the things of this world

👉 Neglecting wisdom is often the first step toward spiritual wandering.

That is why we must not only listen to the truth; we must hold firmly to it every day.

The Example of Those Who Listened to Jesus

When Jesus taught, many people gladly listened to Him.

The Bible says that the people were:

“hanging on His words.”

They knew that His words contained:

  • truth
  • power
  • and life

👉 A heart that truly seeks God hungers for His Word.

When we love the Lord, we do not grow weary of listening to and studying His teachings.

God's Word Brings Stability

The person who continually listens to and obeys God becomes spiritually strong and stable.

Psalm 1 describes such a person as a tree planted by streams of water.

Even through:

  • seasons of drought
  • trials
  • and hardships

it remains firm and fruitful.

👉 The wisdom of God provides direction and stability in life.

The Danger of Drifting Away

There are people who began well in their faith but later drifted away.

One example is Demas.

He was once a fellow worker with Paul in the ministry of the Lord.

Yet later he abandoned the work because he loved the things of this world more.

👉 When we neglect God's Word, it becomes easy to be carried away by temporary things.

A heart that is not focused on God can easily be led astray.

Our drifting away from God's Word often becomes the cause of spiritual coldness in our relationship with Him.

This does not happen suddenly. It begins gradually through neglecting Bible reading, prayer, and listening to God's voice.

When our connection to His Word weakens, our sensitivity to His guidance also begins to fade.

Sadly, many people do not immediately realize that they are drifting away from God. They assume everything is still fine, but over time they are slowly being carried away by the influences of the world and the darkness around them.

👉 Often, we do not recognize how far we have drifted until our hearts have grown cold toward God and our faith has become weak.

That is why Scripture repeatedly warns us to remain in the truth and not depart from the wisdom of God.

The enemy is cunning and often presents small compromises that seem harmless.

Yet small departures from the truth, when ignored, can eventually lead to serious spiritual wandering.

This is why wisdom says:

“Do not disregard it.”

👉 Remaining in God's Word is not only a way to grow in wisdom; it is also our protection against the enemy's deception and the gradual drifting of our hearts away from the Lord.

Every day that we choose to listen to, read, and obey God's Word, we become stronger in our relationship with Him and better prepared against the temptations and deceptions of the world.

Application: What Are You Doing With What You Have Learned?

Examine yourself:

  • Do I merely listen to God's Word, or do I put it into practice?
  • Are there areas of my spiritual life that I have neglected?
  • Am I continuing to grow in my knowledge of God?
  • Are there things in this world that are slowly pulling me away from Him?

Wisdom is not only meant to be heard—it must be held onto and obeyed.

CONCLUSION

Wisdom gives a clear reminder:

👉 Listen to God's instruction and do not neglect it.

The person who continues to walk in the truth grows in wisdom, faith, and stability.

But the person who neglects God's Word gradually drifts away from the right path.

The effects of drifting away from God are not immediately visible. It often begins with small acts of neglect until, over time, the heart grows cold and the person no longer realizes that they have lost their direction.

Therefore, let us guard our hearts. Let us remain firmly anchored in God's Word, for it is there that we find the light, truth, and wisdom we need for each day.

The Question:

Are you still holding firmly to the truths God has taught you, or are you gradually neglecting them?
