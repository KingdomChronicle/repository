Proverbss 9:9

NIV (New International Version)

Instruct the wise and they will be wiser still; teach the righteous and they will add to their learning.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

True Wisdom Continues to Grow

The Wise Never Stop Learning

After comparing the mocker and the wise person in the previous verses, Solomon now reveals another characteristic of true wisdom.

He says:

👉 “Instruct the wise and they will be wiser still; teach the righteous and they will add to their learning.”

At first glance, this statement may seem unusual.

Why teach someone who is already wise?

Isn't what he already knows enough?

Yet here we discover one of the most important marks of wisdom.

A truly wise person never assumes that he knows everything.

The more he learns, the more he realizes how much there is still to learn.

👉 True wisdom produces humility, not pride.

The Wise Welcome Correction

In the previous verse, we saw that a mocker becomes angry when corrected.

The wise person responds differently.

When he is taught, he learns more.

When he is corrected, he grows.

When he receives counsel, he becomes wiser.

He does not view correction as a personal attack.

Instead, he sees it as an opportunity to become a better person.

👉 A teachable heart is a heart that is willing to be transformed by God.

This is why the wise continue to grow while the mocker remains stuck in the same condition.

Life in God's Kingdom Is a Journey of Growth

Many people assume that after years of serving God, there is little left for them to learn.

But that is not the picture Scripture presents.

Life in God's Kingdom is not a destination we arrive at but a journey we continue to walk.

As we walk with the Lord, He continues to teach us.

He continues to shape us.

He continues to transform us.

👉 No believer ever reaches the point where he no longer needs God's wisdom.

Every season of life brings new lessons that the Lord desires to teach us.

Growth Is Part of God's Design

Peter encouraged believers to add to their faith:

  • goodness
  • knowledge
  • self-control
  • perseverance
  • godliness
  • brotherly affection
  • and love

God does not intend for us to remain in the same spiritual condition year after year.

He desires growth.

He desires for us to become more like Christ.

👉 A faith that does not grow is not consistent with God's purpose for His children.

Just as a tree is expected to bear fruit as it grows, God expects to see spiritual growth in our lives.

Even Great Servants of God Continued to Learn

One of the clearest examples of this is Paul.

He was one of the most influential servants God used in the history of the church.

He possessed extensive knowledge of Scripture.

He planted many churches.

God used him to write a large portion of the New Testament.

Yet he never said,

"I have already arrived."

Instead, he declared that he was continually pressing forward toward the goal God had set before him.

"Not that I have already obtained it or have already become perfect, but I press on..." (Philippians 3:12–14)

He was not satisfied with his accomplishments.

He continued growing in faith and in his knowledge of the Lord.

👉 Those who are truly close to God never say they know enough; they continually desire to know Him more.

Knowing God Has No End

The more we know the Lord, the more we understand His greatness.

And the more we understand His greatness, the more we realize how much there is still to learn.

This is why genuinely wise people are often humble.

They know that the riches of God's wisdom can never be exhausted.

👉 The deeper our knowledge of God becomes, the greater our hunger grows to know Him even more.

The Greatest Obstacle to Growth

The greatest enemy of learning is not a lack of intelligence.

It is pride.

When we believe we have nothing left to learn, our growth stops.

When we think we already know everything, we close ourselves to God's continuing instruction.

But a humble heart is always willing to learn.

👉 God has much to teach those who are willing to listen.

Application: Are You Still Growing?

Examine yourself:

  • Do I still have a teachable heart?
  • Am I willing to receive God's correction and instruction?
  • Am I continuing to grow in my knowledge of Christ?
  • Do I make time to study God's Word?
  • Is spiritual growth evident in my life through the things I am learning?

True wisdom is not measured by how much we know but by our continual growth in God.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:9 reminds us that a wise person never stops learning.

The more he is taught, the wiser he becomes.

The more he is guided, the more he grows.

This is because he understands that God's wisdom is deep and inexhaustible.

As believers, we should never become satisfied with our present spiritual condition.

Instead, we should continually grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

For the truly wise person is not merely someone who possesses knowledge.

He is someone who allows God to shape him day by day.

Growth in wisdom is not a sign of weakness.

It is evidence of humility.

It demonstrates a heart that recognizes its ongoing need for God's guidance.

As long as we walk with the Lord, there will always be new lessons to learn, new truths to understand, and new ways to become more like Christ.

👉 The wisest believers are not those who claim to know everything, but those who never stop learning from God.

The Question:

Are you continuing to grow in your knowledge of God, or have you become satisfied with your current spiritual condition?
