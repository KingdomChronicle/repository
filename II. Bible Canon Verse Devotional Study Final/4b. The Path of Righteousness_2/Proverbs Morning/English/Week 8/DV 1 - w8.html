Proverbss 9:7

NIV (New International Version)

Whoever corrects a mocker invites insults; whoever rebukes the wicked incurs abuse.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

A Heart That Refuses to Accept the Truth

Not Everyone Is Open to Wisdom

After Wisdom extends her invitation to all who desire to learn, Solomon now presents an important reality:

👉 Not everyone is willing to accept the truth.

The verse says:

“Whoever corrects a mocker invites insults; whoever rebukes the wicked incurs abuse.”

Some people are grateful when they are corrected.

Others become angry when they are confronted.

Instead of listening to the truth, they attack the one who speaks it.

This reminds us that the problem is not always the message.

Often, the condition of the listener’s heart is the real issue.

👉 When a heart is closed to the truth, even good advice can be perceived as an attack.

Who Is a Mocker?

A mocker is not merely someone who likes to ridicule others.

In the book of Proverbs, a mocker is someone who refuses instruction.

He does not want anyone telling him what is right.

He resists correction and rejects guidance.

Even when the truth is clear, he chooses to cling to his own opinions.

When confronted, he often responds with:

  • pride
  • ridicule
  • anger
  • or hostility

👉 A mocker is not usually lacking knowledge; more often, he is lacking humility.

Pride Is the Enemy of Wisdom

One of the greatest barriers to wisdom is pride.

When a person believes he already knows everything, he stops learning.

When he is convinced he is always right, he stops listening.

When preserving his pride becomes more important than embracing the truth, he rejects correction.

This is why many people repeatedly fall into the same mistakes.

It is not because no one warned them.

It is because they refused to listen.

👉 A proud heart rejects correction; a humble heart grows in wisdom.

This Happened to Jesus

This truth is clearly seen in the life of Jesus.

When He came to earth, He proclaimed God’s truth.

He showed love.

He healed the sick.

He taught the way of life.

Yet instead of receiving Him, many rejected Him.

Some mocked Him.

Some became angry.

Some even sought to have Him killed.

Not because His message was wrong,

but because they refused to accept the truth that exposed the condition of their hearts.

👉 Sometimes people reject the truth not because it is difficult to understand, but because it is difficult to accept.

This Also Happened to Paul

Paul experienced the same reality.

He faithfully preached the Good News.

He explained how God’s promises were fulfilled through Christ.

Some believed.

But others became angry.

Some opposed him.

Others persecuted him.

Instead of accepting the message, they rejected the messenger.

👉 When a heart is hardened, even the clearest truth can be rejected.

Not Every Correction Will Be Welcomed

Every believer needs to understand this.

When we speak the truth in love, not everyone will respond positively.

Some will be grateful.

Some will change.

But others may become offended.

We should not be surprised.

The prophets experienced this.

Jesus experienced this.

The apostles experienced this.

👉 Our responsibility is to be faithful to the truth; how others respond is their responsibility.

Of course, we must speak with humility, love, and the right motives.

But we should never alter the truth simply to avoid opposition.

Examine Your Own Heart

Before thinking about others, we should first examine ourselves.

When God corrects us through His Word, how do we respond?

Do we humble ourselves?

Or do we make excuses?

Do we listen?

Or do we look for reasons to reject His correction?

👉 Each of us has a choice: to be a mocker who rejects correction or a humble learner who embraces it.

Application: How Do I Respond to Truth?

Ask yourself:

  • How do I respond when someone corrects me?
  • Do I become defensive when my mistakes are pointed out?
  • Do I value truth more than my pride?
  • Am I willing to receive God’s correction through His Word?
  • Are there areas of my life that I resist surrendering to His guidance?

Wisdom begins with a heart that is willing to listen.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:7 reminds us that not everyone will receive the truth with humility.

A mocker rejects correction and often responds with anger, ridicule, or resistance. True wisdom, however, is seen in a heart that is willing to receive rebuke and learn from it.

When we value truth more than our pride, we allow God to shape our lives. The purpose of correction is not to shame us but to guide us back to the right path and draw us closer to Him.

There are two important lessons we can take from this verse.

First, as those who share God’s message, we should not be surprised when we face rejection, criticism, or opposition. This happened to the prophets. It happened to Jesus. It happened to the apostles. It happened to Paul and many other servants of God.

Not everyone will be ready to receive God’s truth when they first hear it.

Therefore, when people reject God’s message, we should not immediately become discouraged or think that we have failed. Our responsibility is to faithfully proclaim the truth with love, humility, and sincerity. God is the One who works in the hearts of people.

👉 The measure of success for a servant of God is not how many people accept the message, but how faithfully he proclaims it.

Second, when we ourselves are corrected, how do we respond?

Do we make excuses?

Do we become angry?

Do we take offense?

Or do we humbly receive what God is teaching us?

A person who grows in wisdom is not someone who never makes mistakes, but someone who is willing to acknowledge those mistakes and change through God’s help.

👉 Correction is not a sign that God has rejected us; often, it is a sign that He loves us and is shaping us.

May we guard ourselves from developing the heart of a mocker who rejects the truth. Instead, may we cultivate a humble heart—one that is willing to listen, willing to learn, and willing to change.

The Question:

When God reveals your mistakes through His Word or through other people, do you receive that correction with humility?

And when others reject God’s truth, do you remain faithful in sharing it with love, grace, and sincerity?
