Proverbss 9:10

NIV (New International Version)

The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

True Wisdom Begins with God

The Foundation of All Wisdom

This is perhaps one of the most important verses in the entire Book of Proverbs.

After describing the difference between the wise and the foolish, Solomon now reveals the very foundation of true wisdom.

He says:

👉 “The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.”

Many people search for wisdom in different places.

  • Some seek it through education.
  • Others seek it through experience.
  • Still others look for it in philosophy, science, or human opinion.

While these things may provide knowledge, the Bible clearly teaches that true wisdom has only one ultimate source:

It begins with God.

👉 Wisdom does not begin with human intelligence but with a right understanding of who God is.

What Does “The Fear of the Lord” Mean?

When some people hear the phrase “fear of the Lord,” they imagine being terrified of a harsh enemy.

But that is not what this verse means.

The fear of the Lord refers to:

  • deep reverence
  • holy awe
  • humility
  • trust
  • and willing submission to His will

It is recognizing that God is the Creator.

  • He is holy and all-powerful.
  • He has the rightful authority to rule over our lives.

👉 A person who fears God does not run away from Him but abides in His Word as an expression of genuine devotion, obedience, and submission to Him.

  • "This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth, but you shall meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do according to all that is written in it." — Joshua 1:8
  • "His delight is in the law of the Lord, and on His law he meditates day and night." — Psalm 1:2–3
  • "Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path." — Psalm 119:105
  • "Man does not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of the Lord." — Deuteronomy 8:3
  • "Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that proceeds out of the mouth of God." — Matthew 4:4
  • "If you continue in My word, then you are truly My disciples; and you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free." — John 8:31–32
  • "Let the word of Christ richly dwell within you." — Colossians 3:16

There Is No True Wisdom Apart from God

Many people may be considered wise by the standards of the world.

  • They may be highly educated.
  • They may be successful in business.
  • They may excel in many fields.

Yet if they do not acknowledge God, they still lack the most important kind of wisdom.

Why?

Because they do not understand the most important truths about life.

  • They do not know the Source of all things.
  • They do not understand why they were created.

👉 A person may know many things, but if they do not know God, they possess knowledge without true wisdom.

Knowing God Gives Us the Right Perspective

When we come to know God, our perspective on life changes.

We begin to understand that:

  • our lives have purpose
  • we are accountable to God
  • life on earth is temporary
  • and our choices have eternal consequences

As a result, we become more careful in how we live.

Our direction becomes clearer.

We begin to understand what truly matters.

👉 Knowing God gives us the right perspective on reality.

The Problem of a Person Who Does Not Fear God

In the previous verses, we saw the picture of a mocker.

  • He refuses correction.
  • He refuses to listen.
  • He refuses to change.

At the root of this problem is a lack of humility before God.

When a person does not acknowledge God's authority, pride easily takes control.

He begins to think:

  • I know best.
  • I am always right.
  • I should be the one in control.

👉 Pride is the natural result of a heart that refuses to submit to God.

But the person who fears the Lord is willing to receive correction because he understands that there is always more to learn.

The Fear of God Brings Blessing

Throughout the Old Testament, God repeatedly showed Israel that obedience to Him brings blessing.

  • When they walked according to His commands, they experienced His guidance and protection.
  • When they turned away from Him, they suffered the consequences of their disobedience.

This was not because God desired to harm them.

Rather, turning away from God means turning away from the very source of wisdom, truth, and life.

👉 Obedience to God is not a burden—it is His protection for our lives.

Christ Is the Ultimate Wisdom of God

In the New Testament, we see that true wisdom is fully revealed in Jesus Christ.

He did not merely teach wisdom.

  • He is the Wisdom of God made visible.
  • Through Him we come to know the Father.
  • Through Him we receive salvation.
  • Through Him we learn how to live according to God's will.

👉 Anyone who truly knows Christ has begun to walk in genuine wisdom.

It is not enough to simply have information about God.

We must have a personal relationship with Him through Jesus Christ.

Application: What Is the Foundation of Your Wisdom?

Examine yourself:

  • Are my decisions based on the Word of God?
  • Do I have a deep reverence and submission to the Lord?
  • When God corrects me, am I willing to obey?
  • Do I value the opinions of the world more than the truth of God?
  • Am I continually growing in my knowledge of Christ?

True wisdom begins with a heart that recognizes God as Lord.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 9:10 teaches a foundational truth:

👉 The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom.

  • It is not merely an addition to wisdom.
  • It is not just one of many ways to become wise.
  • It is the very foundation of all true wisdom.

When we acknowledge God, we begin to see life from His perspective.

When we submit to Him, we learn to walk in the right path.

And as we grow in our knowledge of Him, we also grow in understanding, discernment, and wisdom.

The Question:

Is the foundation of your life built upon reverence and obedience to God, or are you relying mainly on your own knowledge and abilities?
