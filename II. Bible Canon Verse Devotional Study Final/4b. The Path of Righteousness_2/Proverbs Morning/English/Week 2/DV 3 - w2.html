Proverbs 8:7

NIV (New International Version)

7My mouth speaks what is true, for my lips detest wickedness.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom Speaks the Truth

A Clear Declaration: Truth Alone

In this verse, wisdom makes a firm declaration:

“Truth alone… and a hatred for wickedness.”

There is no mixture of falsehood. No compromise. No “gray area.”

God’s wisdom is:

  • Truthful in what it says
  • Pure in its intent
  • Direct in its message

In the previous verse (Proverbs 8:6), wisdom’s words were described as noble and excellent. Here, it becomes even clearer—they are completely true.

Truth vs. Falsehood

Wisdom does not only speak truth—it also hates falsehood.

This means:

  • Truth and lies cannot coexist
  • Following wisdom requires turning away from deception

In the following verse (Proverbs 8:8), the opposite of wisdom is described as “crooked” speech.

👉 The world may tolerate “white lies” or half-truths—but God’s wisdom does not compromise.

Christ: The Living Truth

This truth is perfectly embodied in Christ:

  • “He committed no sin, and no deceit was found in His mouth” (1 Peter 2:22)
  • He Himself said, “I am the truth” (John 14:6)

His life and words are the perfect expression of wisdom—without any trace of falsehood.

The Call to Believers

Because wisdom speaks truth, we are also called to live it out:

  • Ephesians 4:15 – Speak the truth in love
  • Ephesians 4:25 – Put off falsehood and speak truthfully
  • Ephesians 4:29 – Speak only what builds others up
  • Colossians 3:8–9 – Put away slander and lies

And a strong reminder from James:

“Out of the same mouth come praise and cursing… this should not be.” (James 3:10)

👉 It is not enough to know the truth—it must be reflected in our speech.

The Problem of the Tongue

One of humanity’s greatest struggles is speech.

At times:

  • We know what is right, yet still lie
  • We praise God, yet speak harm against others
  • We speak kindly in front of people, but differently behind their backs

This is the human condition that wisdom seeks to correct.

Application: What Comes Out of Your Mouth?

If wisdom speaks only truth—

Ask yourself:

  • Are my words truthful?
  • Do they build others up?
  • Is there any deception in the way I speak?

True wisdom is seen not only in what we think—but in what we say.

Conclusion

Wisdom is clear: it speaks truth—and it hates falsehood.

The two cannot coexist.

If we walk in wisdom, we are called to live in truth—in mind, in heart, and in speech.

The question is: Do your words reflect truth—or falsehood?

Wisdom is speaking—the challenge is whether you will follow.
