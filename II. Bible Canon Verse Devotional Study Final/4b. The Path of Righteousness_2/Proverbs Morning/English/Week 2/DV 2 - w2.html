Proverbs 8:6

NIV (New International Version)

6Listen, for I have trustworthy things to say; I open my lips to speak what is right.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Truth and Value of the Words of Wisdom

Listen—This Is Important

Continuing the call of wisdom (Proverbs 8:1–4), this verse presents a clear command:

“Listen.”

This is not a simple suggestion—it is a call with weight and significance.

The words of wisdom are described as:

  • Noble – not ordinary or trivial
  • Excellent – pure, right, and beneficial

The idea behind this reflects a Hebrew term that conveys something clear, upright, and distinguished—like the speech of a ruler or a person with authority.

👉 This means: Wisdom does not speak meaningless words—its message carries weight, clarity, and authority.

Truth vs. Twisted Speech

In the following verse (Proverbs 8:8), a clear contrast is presented:

  • The words of wisdom → true and righteous
  • The opposite → crooked and deceptive

In today’s world, many voices compete for attention:

  • Human opinions
  • Cultural trends
  • Personal beliefs

But not all of them are true.

God’s wisdom is different—it is unchanging and without error.

The Word of God: The Only Infallible Truth

The Bible contains this kind of wisdom.

It does not merely provide knowledge—it reveals eternal truth about:

  • God
  • The world
  • Ourselves
  • Life and death
  • Heaven and hell
  • Righteous living

Science can help us understand many things—but the Word of God reveals the deepest truths of life.

👉 “All Scripture is God-breathed” (2 Timothy 3:16)

👉 “I am the way and the truth and the life” (John 14:6)

👉 “Your word is truth” (John 17:17)

Every word of God is trustworthy.

As was said to Joshua: not one of God’s promises failed—every one was fulfilled (Joshua 23:14).

Not Just Appealing—But True

It is important to note:

The words of wisdom are not merely “pleasant to hear”—they are right and true.

In our time, people often prefer:

  • What sounds good over what is right
  • What is easy to accept over what is true

But true wisdom:

  • May correct
  • May challenge
  • Yet always leads to what is right and good


Application: What Are You Listening To?

If wisdom speaks what is valuable and true—

The question is:

Are you listening?

Examine yourself:

  • Which voices do I listen to daily?
  • Does the Word of God have authority in my life?
  • Or do I give more weight to the opinions of the world?
  • Or do I spend most of my time on social media, games, and worldly distractions?

Listening to wisdom is not just hearing—it is obeying.


Conclusion

Wisdom speaks—and its words are noble, excellent, and true.

It is not like other voices in the world—it carries authority, truth, and life.

The question is not whether someone is speaking—but whether you are willing to listen.

Will you listen to the truth—or choose what is easier to hear but distorted and leading toward spiritual death?

The word of wisdom is clear—the response is yours.
