Proverbss 9:4a

CSB (Christian Standard Bible)

"Whoever is inexperienced, enter here!"

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom Calls Those Who Are Willing to Learn

The Invitation Is for Those Who Admit Their Need

After preparing her house and banquet, Wisdom proclaimed this invitation:

👉 "Whoever is inexperienced, enter here!"

Notice that she did not call those who believed they already knew everything.

Neither did she seek those who trusted in their own wisdom.

Her invitation was directed toward those who recognized that they still had much to learn.

This is the first step toward true wisdom—the humility to admit that we do not know everything.

👉 A person who is willing to learn is closer to wisdom than someone who thinks there is nothing left to learn.

God Welcomes Imperfect People

Sometimes people think they must first become good, wise, or worthy before they can come to God.

But Scripture teaches the opposite.

God does not call perfect people.

He calls those who recognize their need for Him.

Throughout the ministry of Jesus, many of those He called were ordinary people.

There were fishermen, tax collectors, and individuals looked down upon by society.

They were not chosen because they were the most capable.

They were chosen because they were willing to follow and learn.

👉 Our weaknesses are not barriers to God; our pride is.

True Wisdom Begins with God

Many people possess a high level of education yet lack true wisdom.

On the other hand, there are simple people who may not have many academic achievements but possess a deep understanding of God.

Why?

Because true wisdom does not begin with how much we know.

It begins with our relationship with God.

Worldly wisdom may provide information.

God’s wisdom provides direction for life.

👉 True wisdom is not merely knowing the truth—it is living according to it.

The Danger of Thinking We Already Know Everything

One of the greatest obstacles to growth is believing that we no longer need to learn.

When a person is full of his own opinions, it becomes difficult for him to accept God's correction and guidance.

But a humble person continues to grow.

He continues to learn.

He continues to be taught.

👉 A heart that teaches itself is easily led astray, but a heart that allows God to teach it walks in the right path.

That is why it is important to maintain a teachable spirit regardless of our age, experience, or level of knowledge.

God Continues to Call

Wisdom’s invitation was not only for people in the days of Proverbs.

It is also for us today.

Every time we open the Bible, hear a sermon, or sense God speaking to us, He repeats the same invitation:

👉 “Come.”

  • Come and learn.
  • Come and be corrected.
  • Come and grow.
  • Come and know Him more deeply.

It does not matter how many shortcomings we have.

What matters is that we are willing to come to God with a humble heart.

Application: Are You Willing to Learn?

Examine yourself:

  • Do I recognize that I still have much to learn from God?
  • Am I willing to receive correction and guidance?
  • Do I trust my own wisdom more than God's Word?
  • Do I approach the Lord with a humble and teachable heart?
  • Am I continually growing in my knowledge of Him?

Growth in wisdom begins with the humility to come before God.

CONCLUSION

Wisdom calls those who lack understanding.

This is not an insult—it is an invitation.

God invites those who are willing to admit that they need Him.

He is not looking for perfect people.

He is looking for people with hearts that are willing to learn and submit to His truth.

When we acknowledge our need and come to God, true wisdom begins.

For the person who relies solely on his own wisdom can easily lose his way, but the one who comes to God will find direction, truth, and life.

The question is:

Do you still have a heart that is willing to learn from God, or do you believe that your own wisdom is already enough?
