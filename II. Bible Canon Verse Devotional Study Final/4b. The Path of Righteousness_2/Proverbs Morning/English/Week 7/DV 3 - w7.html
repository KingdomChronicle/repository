Proverbss 9:3

NIV (New International Version)

She has sent out her servants, and she calls from the highest point of the city,

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom Actively Extends an Invitation

God Is Not Silent

After preparing her house and the abundant banquet, Wisdom did not keep everything for herself.

Instead, she sent out messengers to deliver the invitation.

The verse says:

👉 “She sent her servants into the midst of the city to proclaim this invitation to everyone.”

This shows that Wisdom does not simply sit and wait in silence.

She actively reaches out and calls people to come.

She desires for her invitation to reach as many people as possible.

This is also the heart of God.

He does not want people to remain lost or ignorant of the truth.

That is why He continually speaks, teaches, and calls through His Word and through the people He uses.

👉 God is a God who seeks and calls people to Himself.

The Invitation Is for Everyone

Notice that the servants were sent into the center of the city and to the high places.

These were locations where many people could see and hear them.

Wisdom’s invitation was not secret.

It was not reserved only for the wealthy, the educated, or the powerful.

It was for everyone.

👉 God’s invitation is open to every person who is willing to listen.

Many people think they are unworthy to come to God because of their past.

But the truth is that God calls all people.

No one is excluded from coming to Him if they are willing to respond to His invitation.

God Uses Messengers

Wisdom did not personally go to every home.

Instead, she sent servants to announce the invitation.

This is also how God works.

Throughout biblical history, God used people to deliver His message.

He used:

  • prophets
  • kings
  • apostles
  • pastors
  • teachers
  • and even ordinary believers

to share His truth.

👉 God chooses to use His children as messengers of His invitation to the world.

We are not only called to receive wisdom but also to share it with others.

The Great Commission of Jesus

This verse also reminds us of Jesus’ Great Commission to His followers.

Before He ascended into heaven, He commanded His disciples to go and make disciples of all nations.

The Good News was never meant to remain inside the walls of the church.

It is meant to be proclaimed to the people around us.

Like Wisdom’s servants, we have also been sent to deliver God’s invitation.

👉 We are messengers of God's love, grace, and truth.

We cannot change people’s hearts, but we can faithfully deliver the message that God may use to transform their lives.

Do Not Ignore the Call

When God calls, it is important that we respond.

Many people hear God's Word yet delay their response.

They assume they have plenty of time.

But none of us knows how long we will continue to hear His invitation.

👉 The best time to respond to God’s call is today.

Every opportunity to hear His Word is a gift that should never be taken for granted.

Application: Are You Responding and Sharing?

Examine yourself:

  • Am I responding to God's call in my life?
  • Do I listen when He speaks through His Word?
  • Are there times when I delay obeying Him?
  • Am I using the opportunities God gives me to share His truth with others?
  • Can people see the message of God’s wisdom through my life?

God’s invitation is not only meant to be received—it is also meant to be shared.

CONCLUSION

Wisdom sent her servants to proclaim her invitation throughout the city.

This reveals that God does not hide Himself from people.

He continues to call, invite, and seek those who are willing to come to Him.

He uses His Word, His servants, and even our own lives to make His truth known.

Yet every invitation requires a response.

It is not enough merely to hear God's call.

We must receive it, obey it, and pass it on to others.

The question is:

When you hear God's call, do you respond immediately—and are you willing to become a messenger of His invitation to others as well?
