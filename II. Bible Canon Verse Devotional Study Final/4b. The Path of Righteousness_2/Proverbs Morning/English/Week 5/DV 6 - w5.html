Proverbs 8:31

NIV (New International Version)

rejoicing in his whole world and delighting in mankind.

GABAY SA PAGNINILAY:

God’s Delight in Humanity

Humanity Is Precious in God’s Eyes

In this verse, we see a beautiful picture—wisdom rejoices because of humanity.

👉 Among all of God’s creation, human beings have a special place.

God created:

  • the heavens
  • the seas
  • the mountains
  • the animals
  • and everything in the world

But when He created humanity, He placed a unique purpose and value upon us.

We are not merely part of nature or simply another created thing.

👉 We were created in God’s image and for His purpose.

God Rejoiced in His Creation

After God created everything, Book of Genesis says:

“God saw all that He had made, and it was very good.”

All creation displays His wisdom and power.

And included in that joy was humanity.

This shows us:

👉 Your existence is not an accident.

You are not the result of chance. You are not a mistake. You are not without value.

God created you with purpose.

Sin Brought Separation

Although humanity was created for a beautiful relationship with God, sin entered the world.

Because of sin:

  • humanity’s relationship with God was broken
  • pain and suffering entered the world
  • and people moved away from His will

Gradually, the world became filled with evil.

But even when humanity sinned:

👉 God never stopped loving us.

The Greatest Proof of God’s Love

God demonstrated His love in the greatest way possible.

Book of Romans says:

“But God demonstrates His own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us.”

Instead of abandoning us, He made a way to restore our relationship with Him.

👉 This is how valuable humanity is to God.

He not only created us—He even gave His Son so that we could be saved.

Your Value Is Not Determined by the World

Sometimes the world measures value by:

  • success
  • money
  • abilities
  • position
  • or the opinions of others

When those things disappear, many people feel that they no longer have worth.

But God uses a different standard.

👉 Your value is not based on what the world says, but on the truth that you are loved by God.

Personal Application: Do You See Your Worth in God?

Examine yourself:

  • Have I ever felt that I have no value?
  • Do I find my identity in the world or in God?
  • Do I believe that God has a purpose for my life?
  • Do I remember that I am precious in His sight?

When we understand God’s love, the way we see ourselves begins to change.

Conclusion

God delighted in His creation, and humanity has a special place in His heart.

👉 Even though sin entered the world, God never stopped loving us.

You are so valuable to Him that He made a way for you to be restored to His presence.

The question:

Do you see yourself according to what the world says—or according to God’s love and purpose for your life?
