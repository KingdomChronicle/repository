Proverbs 8:21

NIV (New International Version)

bestowing a rich inheritance on those who love me and making their treasuries full.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The True Riches Given by Wisdom

The Blessing of Wisdom

In this verse, wisdom gives a promise:

👉 Those who love wisdom will receive riches and abundance.

But it is important to understand that this does not refer only to material wealth.

God’s wisdom provides:

  • right direction
  • wise decisions
  • godly living
  • and blessings with eternal value

A person with wisdom learns to:

  • live carefully
  • avoid wasting blessings
  • and use faithfully what God has entrusted to them

Wisdom Leads to Good Living

Often, a wise person becomes more responsible in:

  • handling money
  • making decisions
  • treating others
  • and daily living

This does not mean that every wise person will become materially wealthy.

However:

👉 Wisdom often produces better outcomes than reckless and foolish living.

A foolish person is quick to:

  • waste resources
  • make harmful decisions
  • and suffer because of selfish desires

But a wise person learns to live with discipline and responsibility.

The Greatest Inheritance

This verse also carries a deeper meaning for believers in Jesus Christ.

We have an inheritance from God that:

  • cannot perish
  • cannot be taken away
  • and will never fade

According to First Epistle of Peter:

“An inheritance that can never perish, spoil or fade.”

👉 The believer’s greatest treasure is not on earth, but in heaven.

We may lose:

  • money
  • businesses
  • possessions
  • or status

But the spiritual riches given through Christ will last forever.

The Treasure We Should Store Up

Jesus warned:

“Do not store up for yourselves treasures on earth…” (Matthew 6:19–20)

Instead, He taught us to store up treasures in heaven.

👉 True wisdom chooses eternal riches over temporary wealth.

Many people spend their lives trying to become rich on earth while neglecting their souls.

But a person with God’s wisdom:

  • puts the Lord first
  • values spiritual life
  • and prepares for eternity

God Fills the Heart

The verse also says:

“making their treasuries full.”

When a person walks in God’s wisdom, He fills their life with:

  • peace
  • hope
  • joy
  • and true satisfaction

👉 Only God can truly fill the emptiness of the human heart.

Many wealthy people still live without contentment, while even a simple person can have a satisfied heart when God is present.

Personal Application: What Fills Your Life?

Examine yourself:

  • What do I consider my true treasure?
  • Am I more determined to gain earthly wealth than to grow in God?
  • Am I using wisely the blessings God has given me?
  • Is my heart content in God, or always searching for more worldly things?

Whatever fills your heart will eventually shape the direction of your life.

Conclusion

God’s wisdom brings true riches and blessing.

👉 But the greatest treasure is not material wealth—it is a relationship with God and the eternal blessings He gives.

The riches of this world may disappear at any moment, but the treasures from God will remain forever.

The question is:

What fills your “treasury” today—temporary things, or the eternal riches that come from God?
