Proverbs 8:18

NIV (New International Version)

With me are riches and honor, enduring wealth and prosperity.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The True Riches That Come from Wisdom

Wisdom Brings True Blessing

In this verse, wisdom declares that with it come:

  • riches
  • honor
  • and prosperity

But this is not merely a promise of material wealth.

👉 The true riches that come from God are greater than money and possessions.

God’s wisdom provides:

  • right direction
  • godly living
  • strong character
  • and blessings with eternal value

A person with wisdom is far more secure than someone who has wealth but lacks understanding.

Riches That Never Fade

Many treasures in this world are temporary.

They can:

  • disappear in an instant
  • be stolen
  • run out
  • or be left behind at death

But the “enduring wealth” mentioned in this verse refers to blessings that:

  • cannot be destroyed
  • cannot be taken away
  • and carry eternal value

👉 God’s wisdom is a treasure that lasts forever.

This includes:

  • peace of heart
  • a relationship with God
  • righteous living
  • and eternal hope

A Warning About Wealth

There is nothing wrong with having wealth.

But wealth becomes dangerous when it becomes the center of a person’s life.

Jesus Christ warned about a man who stored up many riches yet was not rich toward God. (Luke 12:16–21)

That man believed his wealth was enough to make him secure and happy.

But God said to him:

“You fool!”

👉 Wealth without God offers no true security.

The Wisdom of Moses

Moses is an example of someone who chose God over the riches of the world.

According to Epistle to the Hebrews:

“He regarded disgrace for the sake of Christ as of greater value than the treasures of Egypt.”

Moses had the opportunity to remain in the luxury of Egypt.

But he chose obedience to God over the temporary riches of the world.

👉 A truly wise person chooses eternal value over temporary pleasure.

True Riches in God’s Eyes

In Book of Revelation, Jesus told the church in Laodicea that although they believed they were rich, they were actually spiritually poor.

Many people today are:

  • successful in the world
  • wealthy
  • influential
  • and well-known

Yet they have no true relationship with God.

👉 In God’s eyes, the greatest treasure is a heart filled with wisdom and faith.

Personal Application: What Do You Consider Treasure?

Examine yourself:

  • What do I value more—material things or God’s wisdom?
  • Am I more determined to gain wealth than to deepen my relationship with the Lord?
  • When God blesses me, do I use those blessings for His glory?
  • Is my heart attached to God or to worldly riches?

Where your heart is focused, your life will follow.

Conclusion

God’s wisdom brings true riches—not only in this life, but also in eternity.

👉 The riches that come from God are not measured by the amount of money we possess, but by the depth of our relationship with Him.

The world chases wealth that fades away, but the wise person seeks treasures that last forever.

The question is:

What matters more to you—temporary riches, or the eternal riches that come from God?
