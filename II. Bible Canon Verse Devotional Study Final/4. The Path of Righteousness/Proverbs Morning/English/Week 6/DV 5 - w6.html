Proverbs 6:16-19

ESV (English Standard Version)

16 There are six things that the Lord hates, seven that are an abomination to him: 17 haughty eyes, a lying tongue, and hands that shed innocent blood, 18 a heart that devises wicked plans, feet that make haste to run to evil, 19 a false witness who breathes out lies, and one who sows discord among brothers.


GUIDE REFLECTION:


Reflection Series of Proverbs 6:16-19 (4/9)

Hands That Shed Innocent Blood: The Tragedy of Violence

Violence and injustice have plagued humanity since the beginning of time. The shedding of innocent blood refers to the unjust taking of life, whether through murder, oppression, or cruelty.

Exodus 20:13 ESV
“You shall not murder.

In Exodus 20:13, God commands, "You shall not murder," emphasizing the sacredness of human life. Throughout the Bible, God condemns violence and calls His people to seek peace and protect the innocent. The tragedy of violence is not just in the loss of life, but in the destruction of communities, families, and moral foundations.

The First Murder: Cain and Abel

The earliest example of innocent blood being shed is found in Genesis 4:1-16, in the story of Cain and Abel. Cain and Abel were the sons of Adam and Eve, and both brought offerings to God. Abel, a shepherd, brought the best of his flock, while Cain, a farmer, brought an offering from his crops. God accepted Abel’s offering but rejected Cain’s, not because of the offering itself, but because of Cain’s heart.

Consumed by jealousy and anger, Cain lured Abel into a field and killed him.

Genesis 4:9 ESV
Then the Lord said to Cain, “Where is Abel your brother?” He said, “I do not know; am I my brother’s keeper?”

God punished Cain by cursing him to be a restless wanderer, marking him as a warning to others.

Cain’s story highlights how unchecked anger and jealousy can lead to violence. Instead of seeking God’s wisdom, Cain allowed his emotions to drive him to commit murder. His actions not only took Abel’s life but also separated him from God’s presence.

Violence in Society and God’s Justice

The Bible repeatedly warns against those who commit violence. Proverbs 6:17 lists "hands that shed innocent blood" as something that the Lord hates. In ancient Israel, the shedding of innocent blood brought severe consequences.

Deuteronomy 19:10 ESV
lest innocent blood be shed in your land that the Lord your God is giving you for an inheritance, and so the guilt of bloodshed be upon you.

Deuteronomy 19:10 warns that the land will be defiled if justice is not upheld.

Today, violence continues to plague the world—through wars, crime, oppression, and injustice. The innocent suffer because of greed, hatred, and corruption. However, God calls His people to stand against violence and defend the helpless.

Psalm 82:3-4 ESV
3 Give justice to the weak and the fatherless; maintain the right of the afflicted and the destitute. 4 Rescue the weak and the needy; deliver them from the hand of the wicked.”

The Betrayal and Death of Jesus: The Ultimate Innocent Bloodshed

The most tragic example of innocent blood being shed is the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. Jesus was sinless, yet He was falsely accused, brutally beaten, and nailed to a cross. Pontius Pilate, the Roman governor, recognized Jesus' innocence and publicly declared,

Matthew 27:24 ESV
So when Pilate saw that he was gaining nothing, but rather that a riot was beginning, he took water and washed his hands before the crowd, saying, “I am innocent of this man’s blood; see to it yourselves.”

However, he still allowed Jesus to be executed to satisfy the angry crowd.

Jesus’ death was the greatest injustice in history. Yet, His sacrifice brought redemption. His blood was shed so that sinners could be forgiven and reconciled to God. Instead of responding with violence, Jesus prayed for His executioners,

Luke 23:34a ESV
And Jesus said, “Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do.”

His example teaches that even in the face of injustice, love and forgiveness triumph over violence.

God’s Call to Peace and Justice

God commands His people to seek peace rather than violence. Romans 12:19 says,

Romans 12:19 ESV
Beloved, never avenge yourselves, but leave it to the wrath of God, for it is written, “Vengeance is mine, I will repay, says the Lord.”

Instead of taking revenge, believers are called to trust in God’s justice. Jesus taught in Matthew 5:9,

Matthew 5:9 ESV
“Blessed are the peacemakers, for they shall be called sons of God.

Christians are called to be advocates for peace, justice, and the protection of the innocent. Whether in personal relationships or society, God desires His people to promote reconciliation, not bloodshed.

Conclusion

The shedding of innocent blood is a tragedy that grieves God and corrupts society. From Cain’s murder of Abel to the crucifixion of Jesus, the Bible shows the devastating consequences of violence. However, God calls His people to choose peace, defend the innocent, and trust in His justice.

Instead of responding with hatred, believers are to follow Jesus' example of love and forgiveness. In a world filled with violence, the mission of God's people is clear—to stand for righteousness, protect the innocent, and spread the message of peace.
