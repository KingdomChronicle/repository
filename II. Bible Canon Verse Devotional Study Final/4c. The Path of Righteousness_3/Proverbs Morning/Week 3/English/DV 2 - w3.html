Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:14

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

Wise people store up knowledge, But the mouth of the foolish is near destruction.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Greatest Treasure Is Wisdom Stored Up

The Wise Never Stop Storing Up Wisdom

Solomon said:

"Wise people store up knowledge, but the mouth of the foolish is near destruction."

A wise person is never satisfied with knowing only a little.

He continues to learn.

He continues to listen.

He continues to grow in the knowledge of God.

The word "store up" paints the picture of someone carefully gathering a priceless treasure.

Just as a person stores food before a famine comes, the wise store up wisdom before the trials of life arrive.

👉 Wisdom is not something we seek only when problems arise; it is something we prepare before those problems come.

The Words of a Fool Lead to Destruction

In contrast, Solomon describes the fool as someone who does not take time to learn.

Instead of listening, he prefers to speak.

Instead of thinking carefully, he offers reckless opinions.

More often than not, his own words become the cause of his downfall.

Many relationships are damaged because of careless speech.

Many opportunities are lost because of a lack of wisdom.

Many problems become worse because of words spoken without careful thought.

👉 A mouth without wisdom can easily create problems that are difficult to fix.

Store Up God's Word in Your Heart

Information alone is not what we should be collecting.

Far more important is storing God's Word in our hearts.

When temptation comes, God's Word gives us the right direction.

When trials come, His promises give us hope.

When someone needs guidance, we have truth to share.

We cannot give wisdom if we have not first stored up wisdom ourselves.

👉 As we fill our hearts with God's Word, we become better prepared for every challenge in life.

Wisdom Is Not Gained Through Education Alone

Education is important.

But true wisdom is not measured by the number of diplomas or academic titles we have earned.

Many people are highly educated yet lack the wisdom of God.

Meanwhile, Jesus' disciples were ordinary people in the eyes of society.

Yet when the leaders of Israel saw their courage and wisdom, they were amazed.

They recognized that these men had been with Jesus.

👉 The greatest source of wisdom is not the classroom but a continual walk with Christ and His Word.

Christ Is Our Greatest Treasure

Jesus is the very Wisdom of God.

The more we know Him, the wiser we become in our decisions, our words, and our way of life.

He does not simply give us the right answers.

He transforms our hearts so that we may live according to God's will.

Therefore, storing up wisdom is more than simply gaining knowledge.

It is drawing near to Christ each day and living according to His Word.

👉 Those who have a deep relationship with Christ possess a treasure that can never be stolen and wisdom that will never fade.

Living It Out: What Am I Storing Up Every Day?

Examine yourself:

  • What fills my mind more often—the Word of God or the things of this world?
  • Do I set aside time each day to learn from the Scripture?
  • Before I speak, do I consider whether my words reflect wisdom?
  • Do my words provide direction or create confusion?
  • Can others see that my wisdom comes from my relationship with Christ?

Those who continually store up wisdom today will be prepared for the challenges of tomorrow.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:14 teaches that wise people never stop learning. They continually store up God's wisdom so that they are prepared for every situation in life and become a blessing to others.

In contrast, fools do not prepare. They often speak without thinking and rely on their own wisdom, causing their words to bring trouble and destruction.

As believers, we are called to store up treasures that are greater than material wealth. Our greatest treasure is the Word of God planted in our hearts. It provides right direction, strength during times of trial, and wisdom that we can share with others.

👉 Those who store up God's Word will never lack wisdom when the challenges of life come.

The question is:

What are you filling your mind with each day—the wisdom of God or things that contribute nothing to your spiritual growth?
