Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:18

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

Whoever hides hatred has lying lips, And whoever spreads slander is a fool.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Hatred and Slander Are Not Marks of Wisdom

Hatred Begins in the Heart

God looks not only at what we do but also at the condition of our hearts.

Proverbs says:

"Whoever hides hatred has lying lips, and whoever spreads slander is a fool."

This verse shows that hatred never remains hidden forever.

In time, it reveals itself through our words, actions, and the way we treat others.

When we allow anger and bitterness to remain in our hearts, we become easily tempted to lie, tear others down, or spread words that wound people.

👉 A heart filled with hatred is easily used by sin to harm others.

Slander Comes from an Unchanged Heart

Some people hide their hatred behind a mask of kindness.

They treat someone well face to face, but speak against that person behind his back.

Others openly slander, spread gossip, or use hurtful words to destroy another person's reputation.

According to Solomon, such behavior is not a mark of wisdom but of foolishness.

A wise person knows how to control his tongue.

He understands that every word he speaks has the power either to give life or to inflict wounds.

👉 Wisdom is not measured by eloquence but by the ability to control one's tongue.

Our Words Reveal What Is in Our Hearts

Jesus also taught that the mouth speaks from the overflow of the heart.

If our hearts are filled with God's love, peace, and grace, those same qualities will be reflected in our speech.

But if our hearts are filled with envy, anger, and bitterness, speaking words that honor God will be difficult.

It is not enough merely to change the way we speak.

God must first transform our hearts.

👉 When God changes the heart, our words gradually change as well.

Love Is the Cure for Hatred

Hatred seeks to hurt.

Love seeks to heal.

This does not mean that love ignores or approves of sin.

Rather, it chooses to forgive, extend grace, and seek the good of others.

This is exactly what Christ demonstrated.

Although He was mocked, falsely accused, and nailed to the cross, He did not retaliate.

Instead, He even prayed for those who were persecuting Him.

👉 True love does not repay hatred with hatred but overcomes it with goodness.

Believers Are Called to Give Life Through Their Words

In today's world, it is incredibly easy to tear others down.

With just a few clicks on social media, a person's reputation can be damaged.

Gossip, misinformation, and hurtful words spread quickly.

But as followers of Christ, we are called to something different.

We are called to speak words that bring hope, truth, encouragement, and grace.

We should never become instruments of division but of reconciliation and peace.

👉 Ask yourself: Do my words draw people closer to God or push them farther away from Him?

Living It Out: What Is Flowing Out of Your Heart?

Examine yourself:

  • Am I harboring anger or bitterness toward anyone?
  • Do I find it easy to speak negatively about others when they are not around?
  • Do I use my mouth to spread gossip or slander?
  • Do my words bring life or cause harm?
  • Am I allowing the Holy Spirit to transform my heart so that my speech becomes pleasing to God?

God desires not only clean actions but also pure hearts and wholesome words.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:18 reminds us that hatred and slander are not the fruit of wisdom but of a heart that refuses to submit to God. A person ruled by anger easily falls into lying and tearing others down, but a person shaped by God's wisdom learns to speak with truth, love, and self-control.

As followers of Christ, we are not called to spread hatred but peace. Instead of causing division, let us become God's instruments of forgiveness, reconciliation, and hope. As we allow the Lord to transform our hearts, our words will gradually become a fountain of life to those around us.

The Question:

When you speak about other people, do your words reflect the love of Christ, or do they reveal the hatred and bitterness that God desires to remove from your heart?
