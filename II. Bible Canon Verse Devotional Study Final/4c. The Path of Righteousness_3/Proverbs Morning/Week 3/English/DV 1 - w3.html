Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:13

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

Wisdom is found on the lips of him who has understanding, But a rod is for the back of him who is devoid of understanding.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wisdom Is Revealed in Our Words and Our Way of Life

The Lips Reflect the Heart

Solomon said:

"Wisdom is found on the lips of him who has understanding, but a rod is for the back of him who lacks understanding."

This verse teaches that wisdom is not measured merely by how much a person knows but by the way he speaks and lives.

A person with understanding speaks with care.

His words offer guidance, hope, and truth.

He does not speak recklessly.

Before he speaks, he considers whether his words will benefit others.

👉 The mouth of a wise person becomes a channel of blessing to others.

The Fool Refuses to Learn

In contrast, Solomon describes the fool as someone who refuses to accept wisdom.

He does not learn from counsel.

He refuses correction.

He repeatedly makes the same mistakes even though he already knows their consequences.

That is why Solomon says that "a rod is for his back."

This does not mean that violence is the solution.

Rather, it illustrates that some people refuse to learn through instruction and only learn after experiencing the painful consequences of their wrong choices.

👉 When we refuse to learn from God's wisdom, life itself becomes our teacher through the consequences of our choices.

Christ Is the True Wisdom

We see the perfect picture of wisdom in Jesus.

Throughout His earthly ministry, every word He spoke was full of truth and grace.

Many were amazed at His teaching because He spoke with authority, unlike the teachers of the Law.

His words did more than impart knowledge.

They gave life.

They brought hope.

They transformed lives.

When many of His followers left because His teaching was difficult to accept, Peter said:

"Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life."

👉 True wisdom is not found merely in good principles but in Christ Himself, who is the Word of God.

Our Words Reveal the Condition of Our Hearts

We often think that our words simply come out on their own.

But Jesus taught that the mouth speaks from what fills the heart.

If the heart is filled with anger, envy, and pride, those things will also be reflected in our speech.

But if the heart is ruled by God, our words will be filled with grace, truth, and love.

That is why lasting change in our speech begins with a transformed heart.

👉 It is not enough to change the way we speak; we must first allow Christ to transform our hearts.

Continue Listening to God's Wisdom

Today, we are surrounded by countless voices.

There is advice from social media.

There are opinions from all kinds of people.

There are philosophies that contradict God's Word.

If we are not careful, we can easily be led astray.

That is why we must nourish our minds daily with God's Word.

The more we come to know the Lord, the wiser our words and decisions become.

👉 Those who continually listen to God gradually learn to speak with wisdom.

Living It Out: What Do My Words Reveal?

Examine yourself:

  • Do my words bring life or cause pain?
  • Am I willing to receive counsel and correction?
  • Do I learn from God's Word, or only from the painful consequences of my mistakes?
  • Can people see the wisdom of Christ in the way I speak?
  • Do I allow the Holy Spirit to shape my heart each day?

A person who truly has understanding is not only skilled at speaking but is also willing to listen and submit to God's wisdom.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:13 teaches that wisdom is found on the lips of those who have understanding, while fools repeatedly suffer because they refuse to learn.

True wisdom is not measured by intelligence or the amount of knowledge we possess, but by a heart that is willing to receive the truth and by words that bring life to others.

As followers of Christ, we are called to let our speech reflect His character. As we fill our hearts with His Word, our lips will increasingly overflow with grace, truth, and wisdom.

We do not have to wait for painful experiences to teach us when we can learn by listening to and obeying God.

👉 Those who listen to God's wisdom become a blessing to others, but those who reject it often learn only through the bitter consequences of their own choices.

The question is:

When you speak, do people hear the wisdom of Christ from your lips, or do you still need to learn through the repeated consequences of your own wrong decisions?
