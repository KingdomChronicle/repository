Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:16

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The labor of the righteous leads to life, The wages of the wicked to sin.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

What Is the Fruit of Your Life?

Every Way of Life Bears Fruit

Solomon said:

"The labor of the righteous leads to life, but the wages of the wicked to sin."

The word "labor" in this verse does not refer only to money or income.

It points to the fruit or outcome of the way we live.

Every decision we make bears fruit.

Every choice has consequences.

The person who lives righteously reaps fruit that leads to life.

In contrast, the person who lives wickedly continues to produce more sin and destruction.

👉 What matters is not only what we do, but also the direction our actions are leading our lives.

The Righteous Live for Life

The righteous person lives according to the will of God.

He uses his time, abilities, and blessings for things that bring life.

When he works, he does so with integrity.

When he earns an income, he uses it to provide for his family, help others, and honor God.

The fruit of his life is peace, joy, and blessing to those around him.

This does not mean he is free from trials.

But even in the midst of hardship, his life has purpose because it is centered on God.

👉 A life centered on God continually produces fruit of eternal value.

The Wicked Are Never Truly Satisfied

On the other hand, Solomon describes the wicked as people who use what they receive to indulge in even more sin.

Their time.

Their talents.

Their influence.

Even their wealth.

All of these are used to satisfy their own desires rather than to glorify God.

They may accumulate much.

They may appear successful in the eyes of the world.

But if everything they have is used against God's purposes, it ultimately amounts to nothing.

Sin always promises satisfaction.

But it can never truly satisfy the human soul.

👉 A life separated from God may seem successful by the world's standards, yet the soul remains empty.

Christ Gives True Life

The ultimate fulfillment of this verse is found in Jesus Christ.

He said:

"I have come that they may have life, and that they may have it more abundantly."

The life Jesus gives is more than simply a long life.

It is a life with purpose.

A life filled with peace.

With hope.

With joy.

And above all, with eternal life.

When Christ is the center of our lives, everything we do gains true meaning.

Our work.

Our family.

Our service.

Even our trials are used by God to produce good.

👉 Christ alone can give a life that is not merely long but truly meaningful.

Use Your Life for God

God has entrusted us with more than money.

He has also entrusted us with our time.

Our talents.

Our strength.

Our opportunities.

And our influence.

The question is how we use them.

Are we using them to advance God's kingdom?

Or are we simply spending them on temporary things?

In the end, God will not measure us by how much we accumulated.

He will measure how faithfully we used what He entrusted to us.

👉 True success is measured not by how much we receive, but by the fruit of our faithfulness to God.

Living It Out: What Kind of Fruit Is Your Life Producing?

Examine yourself:

  • Does my life produce life and blessing for others?
  • How do I use my time, talents, and blessings?
  • Do I use my earnings to honor God?
  • Is Christ the center of my decisions and dreams?
  • If someone examined the direction of my life today, where would it be leading?

Every day God gives us is an opportunity to live for things of eternal value.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:16 teaches that every way of life bears fruit. The person who lives righteously reaps life, while the one who lives in wickedness sinks deeper into sin and destruction.

It is not only what we earn that matters, but how we use it. Everything we have—our time, talents, strength, and material blessings—has been entrusted to us by God to be used for His glory and for the good of others.

Through Jesus Christ, we have received the true life that the world can never give. When He is the center of our hearts, our lives become meaningful and bear fruit of eternal value.

👉 A life devoted to Christ is never wasted, because everything done for Him bears fruit both now and for eternity.

The Question:

Does the fruit of your life bring life and glory to God, or are your time, strength, and blessings simply being spent on things that have no eternal value?
