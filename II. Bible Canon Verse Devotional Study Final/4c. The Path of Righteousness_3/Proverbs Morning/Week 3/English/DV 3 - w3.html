Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:15

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The rich man’s wealth is his strong city; The destruction of the poor is their poverty.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The True Stronghold Is the Treasure That Comes from God

From Storing Up Wisdom to True Riches

In the previous verse (Proverbs 10:14), Solomon said that the wise store up wisdom. Their greatest treasure is not gold or silver, but the wisdom that comes from God. This is their true wealth because it equips them for every decision, every trial, and every circumstance of life.

On the other hand, the fool does not store up wisdom. What comes from his mouth is foolishness that leads to destruction. While the wise become rich in God's wisdom, the fool suffers because of his own words and sinful way of living.

Now, in Proverbs 10:15, Solomon introduces another kind of wealth—the wealth of this world.

"The rich man's wealth is his strong city; the destruction of the poor is their poverty."

Solomon is not teaching that God loves the rich more than the poor. He is simply describing the reality of life. Those who possess wealth often have the resources to meet many of life's needs, while poverty brings heavier burdens and greater hardships.

Notice, however, that these two verses are closely connected.

In verse 14, the wise store up wisdom.

In verse 15, the rich possess wealth that serves as a stronghold.

Through this comparison, Solomon shows that there are two kinds of riches to which people may devote their lives—the wealth of this world and the riches that come from God.

👉 The truly wise person treasures the riches that come from God above all else, for they alone have eternal value.

Earthly Wealth Has Its Limits

There is nothing wrong with having wealth. Money has many important uses.

It can buy food.

It can pay for medical care.

It can provide a comfortable life.

In many situations, it is a great help during times of need.

But that is as far as it can go.

It cannot stop aging.

It cannot prevent death.

It cannot remove sin.

It cannot buy true peace or eternal life.

This is also what Solomon declares in Proverbs 11:4:

"Riches do not profit in the day of wrath, but righteousness delivers from death."

The day will come when everything accumulated in this world will be left behind. In the face of death, the size of a bank account, possessions, businesses, or material wealth has no lasting value. No amount of wealth can save anyone from the judgment of God.

👉 Earthly wealth may provide temporary security, but it cannot rescue anyone from death or eternal destruction.

The True Treasure Is the Treasure That Comes from God

This is what Solomon has been teaching since the previous verse.

The wise do not spend their lives merely accumulating money.

Instead, they make it their priority to store up the wisdom of God.

As they grow deeper in their knowledge of the Lord, they become richer in things that money can never buy.

They possess peace.

They possess hope.

They possess godly direction.

They possess a faith that remains steadfast even if every material possession is lost.

This is the treasure that cannot be stolen, destroyed, or taken away by death.

👉 True wealth is not measured by how much we own but by the depth of our relationship with God and the wisdom He gives.

Christ Is Our Greatest Treasure

Ultimately, true treasure is not merely something—it is a Person.

Jesus Christ is the greatest treasure anyone can ever receive.

In Him we have found forgiveness of sins.

In Him we have received eternal life.

In Him we have come to know the wisdom of God.

Through Him, we possess a treasure that cannot be taken away by poverty, sickness, old age, or even death itself.

Therefore, even if a believer loses all earthly possessions, he is never truly poor, because he possesses eternal riches.

On the other hand, a person may own all the wealth in the world, yet without Christ, he remains impoverished of the greatest treasure of all.

👉 The truly wealthy person is the one who has a relationship with Christ, for in Him is a treasure that not even death can take away.

Living It Out: What Is Your Stronghold?

Examine yourself:

  • What do I devote more time to—storing up earthly wealth or storing up God's wisdom?
  • Where does my sense of security rest—in my savings or in God?
  • When I experience lack, do I continue to trust the Lord?
  • Do I use the blessings God has given me to serve Him and bless others?
  • Do I value my relationship with Christ more than any material possession?
  • If I were to lose everything I own, would my faith remain steadfast?

The one who stores up God's wisdom possesses a treasure that no earthly wealth can either give or take away.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:14 and 10:15 are closely connected. In the first verse, Solomon teaches that the wise store up true riches—the wisdom of God. In the next verse, he reminds us that material wealth may serve as a temporary stronghold, but it has its limits.

Wealth has value in this present life, but it cannot save anyone from sin, stop death, or give eternal life. God alone is our true and unshakable refuge.

Through Jesus Christ, we have received a treasure far greater than gold or silver—forgiveness of sins, reconciliation with God, peace, hope, and eternal life. This is the treasure that will never perish.

👉 A person who has God may lack material possessions, yet will never be poor in true riches. A person without God may possess great wealth, yet remain spiritually impoverished of the treasure that has eternal value.

The question:

What are you investing in each day—the temporary wealth that will one day be left behind, or the treasure that comes from God and will endure forever?
