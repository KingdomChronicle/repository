Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:17

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

He who keeps instruction is in the way of life, But he who refuses correction goes astray.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Receiving Correction Is the Path to Life

Wisdom Knows How to Listen

Solomon said:

"He who keeps instruction is in the way of life, but he who refuses correction goes astray."

One of the most important marks of a wise person is the willingness to receive counsel and correction.

He knows that he does not know everything.

He recognizes that there are still areas of his life that God desires to shape.

When he is corrected according to the truth, he does not see it as a personal attack but as an opportunity to grow.

👉 A person who knows how to listen continues to walk on the path of life.

Correction Is an Expression of God's Love

Many people are afraid of rebuke.

They think it is a sign of rejection or a lack of love.

But in reality, God's correction is the fruit of His love.

Just as a loving father disciplines his child to keep him from harm, God corrects us to turn us away from sin and lead us into His will.

Correction is not meant to shame us.

It is meant to set us on the right path.

👉 When we receive God's correction, we choose the path that leads to life.

Rejecting the Truth Leads Us Astray

On the other hand, Solomon describes the person who refuses correction.

He does not want anyone pointing out his faults.

He would rather follow his own thinking than the Word of God.

At first, his life may seem to be going well.

But little by little, he drifts away from the right path.

Going astray rarely happens all at once.

It begins with small acts of rejecting the truth.

One piece of advice ignored.

One warning dismissed.

Until eventually, he loses his way completely.

👉 A person who continually rejects correction gradually drifts away from God's path without even realizing it.

Christ Is the Way of Life

Jesus did not merely teach the right way.

He declared:

"I am the way, the truth, and the life."

Listening to His words is more than gaining knowledge.

It is choosing life.

Every command Christ gives is for our good.

Every correction He brings is meant to rescue us from the things that would destroy our lives.

When we follow Him, we do not lose our way because He Himself is our Shepherd who faithfully guides us.

👉 Listening to Christ is walking on the only path that leads to true life.

Be Careful of Voices That Lead You Astray

There are countless voices competing for our attention today.

Some people say that the Bible is no longer relevant.

Others teach that truth is nothing more than personal opinion.

Many philosophies elevate human desires above the will of God.

Many speak as though they are experts, yet their teachings lead people away from the truth of God's Word.

That is why we must be discerning.

Not everything popular is right.

Not every modern idea is from God.

The Word of God must remain our standard of truth.

👉 A person who is not rooted in God's Word can easily be swept away by the world's false teachings.

Living It Out: Am I Willing to Receive Correction?

Examine yourself:

  • How do I respond when God corrects me through His Word?
  • Am I willing to receive counsel from the people God has placed in my life?
  • Am I insisting on something even though I know it is contrary to God's will?
  • Do the voices I listen to draw me closer to Christ or pull me away from Him?
  • Am I walking on the path of life, or am I gradually drifting away from the right direction?

A heart that is willing to receive correction is a heart that is ready to grow in wisdom.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:17 teaches that listening to instruction and accepting correction are not signs of weakness but of wisdom. The person who humbly receives God's guidance remains on the path that leads to life.

On the other hand, the one who continually rejects the truth gradually loses his way. The consequences may not be immediately visible, but every refusal to listen takes him farther from God and His purpose.

As followers of Christ, it is not enough merely to hear God's Word. We must receive it, obey it, and allow it to transform our lives. Every correction from God is an expression of His love because He desires to keep us on the path that leads to true life.

👉 Every correction received with humility is a step closer to Christ. Every truth we reject is a step farther away from Him.

The Question:

When God corrects you through His Word or through the people He uses, do you humbly choose to obey, or do you insist on following your own path even though you know it is leading you away from true life?
