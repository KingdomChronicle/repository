Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:2

NIV (New International Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

Ill-gotten treasures have no lasting value, but righteousness delivers from death.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

True Wealth Is Not Measured by Money but by Righteousness

Not Every Kind of Wealth Is a Blessing

In our world today, success is often measured by the amount of money, possessions, and investments a person has accumulated.

When someone owns a large house, drives an expensive car, or has a successful business, we quickly assume that life is going well for them, often thinking, "I wish I had that too."

But Solomon reminds us that not every kind of wealth is truly a blessing.

The verse says:

👉 "Ill-gotten treasures have no lasting value, but righteousness delivers from death."

Some riches are accumulated through dishonesty.

Some businesses prosper through exploitation.

Some people become wealthy through greed, corruption, or taking advantage of others.

These may provide temporary comfort.

But they can never give genuine peace.

👉 Wealth gained through unrighteous means may bring temporary satisfaction, but it can never provide lasting security.

Money Has Its Limits

Many people assume that if they have enough money, they are secure.

But life proves that money cannot buy everything.

  • It cannot stop aging.
  • It cannot prevent death.
  • It cannot heal every disease.
  • Neither can it purchase God's forgiveness.

Many wealthy people have died, leaving behind everything they had accumulated.

Their possessions remained in this world.

But their souls still stood before God.

👉 When the end of our lives comes, no earthly wealth can be taken into eternity.

Righteousness Has Eternal Value

In contrast, Solomon speaks about righteousness.

Righteousness is not simply being a good person.

It is living according to the will of God.

It is a heart that trusts the Lord and walks faithfully before Him.

A person who lives righteously may never become the wealthiest person in the world.

Yet that person possesses something far more valuable.

  • Peace with God.
  • A clear conscience.
  • The hope of eternal life.

👉 True wealth is having a right relationship with God.

Righteousness Delivers from Death

Solomon says, "righteousness delivers from death."

This does not mean that righteous people will never die physically.

Everyone will face physical death.

But the person who lives in God's righteousness has a hope that extends beyond the grave.

In the light of the New Testament, we understand this truth even more clearly.

None of us possesses a righteousness of our own that can save us.

We are all sinners.

But God gave His son Jesus Christ so that whoever believes in Him may be declared righteous before God.

The righteousness that saves us is not the result of our good works.

It is God's gift through Christ.

👉 The greatest treasure a believer possesses is the righteousness received through Christ, because it leads us to eternal life.

What Are You Storing Up?

It is only natural to save for the future.

It is good to be diligent.

It is good to be responsible with our finances.

But there is a more important question.

What are we truly investing our lives in?

Many people spend their entire lives accumulating wealth but never take the time to know God.

They prioritize:

  • their business,
  • their career,
  • their possessions.

But they neglect their souls.

Jesus asked:

"What good will it be for someone to gain the whole world, yet forfeit their soul?"

👉 It is far better to be rich toward God than merely rich in this world.

Live with an Eternal Perspective

There is nothing wrong with possessing wealth.

In fact, God may entrust many blessings to His servants.

What matters is how we earn it and how we use it.

Our wealth should become a tool for glorifying God.

It should never become our god.

When our hearts are focused only on temporary things, it is easy to lose our direction.

But when Christ is our greatest treasure, we possess a hope that can never be taken away.

👉 The person who has God is never truly poor, and the person who is without God is never truly rich, no matter how great their possessions may be.

Living It Out: What Is Your True Treasure?

Examine yourself:

  • What do I desire more—to become wealthy or to be righteous before God?
  • Are there times when I sacrifice integrity just to gain an advantage?
  • Do I trust in my savings or in God, my true Provider?
  • Am I using the blessings I receive to serve and honor the Lord?
  • If my life ended today, what true treasure would I bring before God?

The greatest investment is not what is in our bank account but our relationship with Christ.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:2 teaches that not all wealth has lasting value.

Riches gained through wickedness may provide temporary pleasure, but they cannot save a person from death or give peace before God.

On the other hand, the righteousness that comes from God has eternal value. Through Jesus Christ, we have been declared righteous and given eternal life—a treasure that can never be stolen, destroyed, or lost.

Therefore, as we work diligently to provide for our needs in this world, let us never forget that our greatest treasure is not what we own, but our relationship with the Lord.

The question is:

What are you investing your life in more—the accumulation of temporary earthly treasures, or the pursuit of eternal treasure through a righteous life in Christ?
