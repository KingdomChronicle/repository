Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:6

MBBTAG (Magandang Balita Biblia)

The Proverbs of Solomon

The righteous receive blessings and goodness, but the mouth of the wicked conceals violence.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Heart Is the Source of Our Words and Blessings

The Life of the Righteous Produces Blessings

In this verse, Solomon once again presents the clear contrast between the righteous and the wicked.

He says:

👉 "The righteous receive blessings and goodness, but the mouth of the wicked conceals violence."

The righteous are described as receiving blessings.

In contrast, the wicked are described as using their mouths to bring violence.

This shows that the way we live has consequences.

A person who lives according to God's will becomes a channel of His blessings.

This does not mean that every trial and hardship will disappear from their life.

Yet even in the midst of suffering, they experience God's goodness, presence, and blessings.

👉 True blessing is not measured merely by how much we receive, but by God's presence and His favor upon our lives.

Words Reflect the Heart

On the other hand, Solomon says that the mouth of the wicked conceals violence.

This reminds us that words do not come out without a reason.

What a person says is the result of what fills their heart.

This is exactly what Jesus taught.

He said that the mouth speaks from the abundance of the heart.

If the heart is filled with anger, envy, deceit, and evil, those same things will be reflected in our speech.

But if God transforms our hearts, our words will be filled with:

  • truth
  • love
  • forgiveness
  • encouragement
  • and wisdom

👉 Before God changes our speech, He first changes our hearts.

Words Can Give Life or Cause Pain

Our words carry tremendous power.

With just one sentence, we can strengthen someone's heart.

But we can also wound them.

Many relationships are destroyed because of hurtful words.

Many friendships are broken because of slander.

Many families are torn apart because of anger that was left unchecked.

Therefore, a person who walks in wisdom learns to guard their mouth.

They do not speak merely according to their emotions.

Instead, they allow God to guide their words.

👉 A righteous person is known not only by what they do but also by the way they speak.

True Righteousness Is Found in Christ

All of us have sinned.

All of us have spoken words that have hurt others, caused damage, or displeased God.

But through Jesus Christ, there is forgiveness and transformation.

When we surrender our lives to Him, He not only forgives us.

He also gradually transforms our hearts.

And as He changes our hearts, He also changes the way we speak.

Little by little, we become people whose words bring hope instead of wounds, peace instead of conflict, and truth instead of deception.

👉 Only Christ can transform the heart, and a heart transformed by Him produces life-giving words.

Be a Channel of Blessing Through Your Words

As followers of Christ, we are called to be a blessing to others.

Not only through what we do.

But also through what we say.

In our homes.

At work.

In our churches.

And even on social media.

We can choose to become channels of:

  • hope instead of discouragement
  • truth instead of lies
  • forgiveness instead of revenge
  • love instead of hatred

👉 Every word that comes out of our mouths is an opportunity to display the character of Christ to others.

True Transformation Is First Seen at Home

We often strive to be kind, gentle, and respectful when we are at church, at work, at school, or with other people. Yet there is one place we often fail to examine—our own home. It is there that the true condition of our hearts is first revealed.

Isn't it true that some married couples who have been together for many years no longer realize that they have developed the habit of speaking to one another in a harsh, sharp, or irritated manner? Because they have been together for so long, short replies, an impatient tone, or words lacking respect and affection gradually become normal. Even if they never raise their voices, the way they speak slowly wounds each other's hearts. Over time, the respect and tenderness they once had are replaced by complacency and a lack of care in the words they speak.

Likewise, some children no longer realize that they have begun to lose respect in the way they speak to their parents. Answering back rudely, speaking with an irritated tone, or showing a lack of respect in their speech gradually becomes part of their everyday lives. Even some grandchildren become accustomed to speaking or behaving disrespectfully toward their grandparents, as though such behavior were perfectly normal.

Little by little, a beautiful value that was once highly treasured is fading away—that children, grandchildren, and younger people should show deep respect to their parents and elders. In many homes today, talking back, raising one's voice, or speaking to elders as though they were merely equals is no longer considered wrong but has become commonplace.

It is true that culture and society's standards change over time. Many expressions of respect that were once highly valued are gradually disappearing because of modern thinking and changing social trends. But as true followers of Christ, it should not be the culture of the world that shapes our character—it should be the Word of God. Respect for our spouse, our parents, our grandparents, and all our elders is not an outdated tradition but the fruit of a heart that is submitted to God.

Sometimes we are even more careful with our words toward people outside our home than toward our own family. We are more courteous to coworkers, friends, or even strangers, yet inside our homes we easily become impatient, irritable, and hurtful with our words. It is heartbreaking to realize that our kindest words are often reserved for others, while our own family is the one that most frequently experiences our frustration and painful speech.

As followers of Christ, it is not enough for our good character to be seen only by people outside our homes. God's true transforming work should first be evident to those who are closest to us. They are the first ones who can truly testify whether our hearts are genuinely being transformed by the Lord.

👉 Do not simply ask yourself whether you are kind to other people. A far more important question is:

  • "Does my spouse—especially after many years of marriage—experience the same respect, kindness, and love that I show to other people?"
  • "Do my children, parents, grandparents, and even my grandchildren see the transforming work of Christ in the way I speak to them and treat them every day?"

The home is the first place where the fruit of a heart transformed by God should be seen. For if the love of Christ truly reigns in our hearts, it will be evident not only in public but especially within our own homes. True holiness does not begin with how others see us, but with how we love, honor, and speak to our own family every single day.

👉 If Christ is truly transforming our hearts, it will be evident not only in the way we speak to other people but even more in the way we treat and speak to those within our own homes.

Respect should never disappear simply because we have been together for a long time, have become overly familiar with one another, or because times have changed. Instead, it should grow deeper as we continue to grow in Christ. For the home is the first place where the fruit of a heart that has truly been transformed by God should be seen.

Application: What Do Your Words Reveal?

Examine yourself:

  • Do my words bring life to others, or do they hurt them?
  • What usually comes out of my mouth when I am angry or hurting?
  • Can people see Christ's transforming work in the way I speak?
  • Do I use my words to encourage others and share the truth?
  • Am I willing to let God transform not only my mouth but also my heart?

Our words are a reflection of our hearts.

If we want our speech to change, we must first allow God to transform our hearts.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:6 teaches that the life of the righteous produces blessings, while the mouth of the wicked becomes an instrument of hurt and destruction.

This difference begins not with our lips but with our hearts.

When our hearts follow God, our words become channels of His grace.

But when our hearts are ruled by sin, even our speech becomes a source of wounds and conflict.

Therefore, more than simply being careful about what we say, God invites us to surrender our hearts to Him.

For a heart transformed by Christ produces a righteous life and words that give life.

The question is:

If your words were examined every day, would they reveal the heart of someone being transformed by Christ, or a heart still ruled by anger, pride, and selfish desires?
