Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:1

NIV (New International Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

A wise child brings joy to a father; a foolish child brings grief to a mother.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

A Child's Wisdom Brings Joy to the Family

Every Way of Living Affects Others

As the second major section of the book of Proverbs begins, Solomon introduces a series of short proverbs filled with practical wisdom for everyday life.

The very first proverb is about the home.

It is not about wealth.

It is not about success.

It is not even about fame.

Rather, it is about the kind of child who grows up within a family.

The verse says:

👉 "A wise child brings joy to a father, but a foolish child brings grief to a mother."

This reminds us that our decisions do not affect only ourselves.

They also affect the people who love us.

👉 The way we live can become either a source of joy or a source of sorrow for our family.

A Wise Child Brings Joy

A wise child is not merely someone who is academically intelligent.

In the book of Proverbs, true wisdom is living according to the will of God.

Such a person:

  • listens to correction,
  • walks in humility,
  • obeys God's Word,
  • and lives with reverence and fear of the Lord.

This kind of child becomes a source of joy to his or her parents.

Not because he or she is perfect,

but because they see their child walking in the right path.

No earthly wealth can compare to the joy a parent experiences when seeing a son or daughter love and follow God.

👉 The greatest gift a child can give to his or her parents is not success or wealth, but a life that is lived according to the wisdom of God.

A Foolish Child Brings Sorrow

On the other hand, Solomon describes the foolish child.

This does not mean someone who lacks intelligence.

In Proverbs, a "fool" is a person who rejects God's wisdom.

He chooses to follow his own desires.

He refuses correction.

He is unwilling to learn.

He disregards the will of the Lord.

As a result, he is not the only one who suffers.

His parents suffer as well.

Many fathers and mothers spend years caring for and praying for their children.

Therefore, when a child chooses to live in sin and stubbornness, it is not merely a personal failure.

It becomes a heavy burden upon the hearts of the entire family.

👉 Sin is never merely personal; it often brings pain even to those who genuinely love us.

Wealth Is Not the Measure of Success

Today, many parents dream of seeing their children become wealthy, successful, or well-known.

Yet Proverbs reminds us that something far more important exists.

A person may be successful in the eyes of the world while remaining far from God.

He may possess many riches yet have no peace.

He may hold a high position yet have poor character.

True success is not measured by the size of our income, but by the kind of life we live before God.

👉 To God, a heart that willingly obeys Him is far more valuable than a life filled with worldly success but lacking holiness.

A Challenge to Children

If we are children, this verse encourages us to examine how we treat our parents.

Are we bringing them peace?

Or are we the cause of their worry?

Not every parent is perfect.

Yet God still commands us to honor them.

One of the greatest ways we honor our parents is by living lives marked by wisdom and holiness.

👉 When we obey God, we not only honor Him—we also become a source of joy to our family.

A Challenge to Parents

This verse also serves as a reminder to parents.

The ultimate goal of raising children is not merely for them to obtain good careers or secure comfortable futures.

What matters even more is that they come to know God,

learn to love His Word,

and walk in His ways.

Education is important.

Preparing children for the future is important.

But above all, nurturing their faith and character carries eternal value.

👉 The greatest inheritance parents can leave their children is not material possessions, but introducing them to the Lord.

Our Heavenly Father Also Rejoices

Beyond our earthly parents, this verse also reminds us of the heart of our Heavenly Father.

When we walk in His wisdom, He delights in us.

We are not saved because of our good works.

We are saved by grace through faith in Christ.

Yet those who truly belong to Christ desire to live in a way that pleases their Father.

Just as a good child desires to bring joy to his or her parents, every believer should desire to live for the glory of God.

👉 True wisdom is seen in a life that daily brings pleasure to our Heavenly Father.

Living It Out: Am I Bringing Joy or Sorrow?

Examine yourself:

  • Does the way I live bring joy or concern to my parents?
  • Is obeying God more important to me than pursuing worldly success?
  • Do I willingly receive correction and counsel from those who love me?
  • As a parent, do I prioritize my children's spiritual growth over their material success?
  • Does my life bring pleasure to my Heavenly Father?

True wisdom is not merely demonstrated by what we say.

It is revealed in the way we live each day.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:1 reminds us that wisdom does not simply improve our own lives.

It also becomes a blessing to those who love us.

A wise child brings joy to his or her parents because that child's life reflects the wisdom of God.

Meanwhile, the person who rejects wisdom not only brings destruction upon himself but also becomes a source of grief to the family.

Ultimately, the highest calling of every believer is not merely to become successful in the eyes of the world, but to remain faithful to the Lord. When our lives are centered on Christ and His wisdom, we become a blessing to our family, to those around us, and above all, we bring glory to our Heavenly Father.

The question is:

Does the kind of life you are living today bring joy to your parents and delight to God, or has it become a source of sorrow to them?
