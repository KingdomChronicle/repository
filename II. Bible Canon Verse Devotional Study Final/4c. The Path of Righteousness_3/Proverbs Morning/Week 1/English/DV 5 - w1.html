Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:5

NIV (New International Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

A wise youth harvests in the summer, but one who sleeps during harvest is a disgrace.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Wise Make the Most of the Opportunities God Gives

Everything Has Its Proper Time

In nature, there is a time for planting and a time for harvesting.

Harvest cannot be postponed once the proper season has arrived.

If the harvest season passes, the opportunity to gather the crop is lost.

This is the picture Solomon uses in this verse.

He says:

👉 "A wise youth harvests in the summer, but one who sleeps during harvest is a disgrace."

A wise person acts at the right time.

He is not careless.

He does not wait until it is too late.

In contrast, the lazy person allows opportunities—many of which come only once—to pass by.

👉 Wisdom is not only knowing what to do, but also knowing when to do it.

Procrastination Can Lead to Regret

Many people have wonderful dreams.

Many also have excellent plans.

But not everyone is willing to take action.

We often hear statements like:

"I'll do it tomorrow."

"When I have more time."

"When life becomes easier."

Yet while we postpone what should be done, the opportunities God has placed before us may already be passing us by.

Some opportunities never return.

👉 Laziness and procrastination can rob us of the blessings God has prepared for us.

Be a Faithful Steward of Every Opportunity

God entrusts us with more than just money.

He also entrusts us with our:

  • time
  • abilities
  • health
  • relationships
  • and opportunities

Each of these has a purpose.

When we use them faithfully, we become good stewards of God's gifts.

But when we neglect them, we gradually waste the blessings He has given us.

👉 Wisdom is seen in those who know how to make the most of every opportunity God provides.

There Is Also a Season for Spiritual Harvest

Beyond material work, this verse also reminds us of a far greater harvest.

When Jesus spoke to His disciples, He said:

"Open your eyes and look at the fields! They are ripe for harvest."

He was referring to people who were ready to hear the Good News.

Many are searching for hope.

Many are seeking forgiveness.

Many are looking for the true meaning of life.

But if believers remain silent or become careless in sharing the gospel, the opportunities God places before us may pass by.

👉 Every day may be an opportunity for someone to come to know Christ.

Do Not Waste the Time God Has Given You

Life is filled with seasons that cannot be revisited.

There is a time to learn.

There is a time to serve.

There is a time to love.

There is a time to share the gospel.

There is a time to repent and return to God.

We do not know how long God will continue to give us these opportunities.

Therefore, while there is still time, let us use it for His glory.

👉 A wise person does not wait for a "better time"; he makes the most of the time he has today.

Living It Out: Am I Using the Opportunities God Gives Me?

Examine yourself:

  • Are there things I continue to put off even though I know God wants me to do them?
  • Am I making good use of the time and abilities God has entrusted to me?
  • Are there people God wants me to reach by sharing the gospel?
  • Do I prioritize temporary things over things of eternal value?
  • If I were to look back on my life, could I honestly say I was a faithful steward of the opportunities God gave me?

Every day is a gift from God.

The question is how we choose to use it.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:5 teaches that a wise person acts at the proper time, while a lazy person allows valuable opportunities to pass until only regret remains.

This is not merely about harvesting crops or working diligently.

It is also a reminder to make the most of every opportunity God gives us—in our families, in our service, in our spiritual growth, and especially in sharing the Good News.

Some opportunities are given by God only once.

Some hearts that are open today may not be open tomorrow.

Some acts of obedience that can be done today may not be possible later.

Therefore, while there is still time, let us be faithful stewards of every opportunity God graciously gives us.

The question is:

Are you making the most of the opportunities God is giving you today, or are you continually postponing things of eternal value until the season of harvest has already passed?
