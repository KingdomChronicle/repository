Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:8

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The wise in heart will receive commands, But a prating fool will fall.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Wise Know How to Listen

Wisdom Begins with a Humble Heart

In this verse, Solomon once again highlights the contrast between a wise person and a fool.

He says:

👉 "The wise in heart will receive commands, but a prating fool will fall."

A person with understanding is willing to listen.

He does not think that he already knows everything.

He welcomes wise instruction because he recognizes that there is still much for him to learn.

On the other hand, a fool would rather talk than listen.

He is full of his own opinions.

He refuses correction.

As a result, he often ends up making poor decisions that lead to ruin.

👉 True wisdom is not measured by how much we know, but by our willingness to keep learning.

Those Who Listen Learn More

There is a reason God gave us two ears but only one mouth.

We are meant to listen more than we speak.

Today, many people want to be heard.

Everyone has an opinion.

Everyone wants a voice.

But few people truly know how to listen.

If we fail to listen, we may miss:

  • the counsel of our parents
  • the encouragement and correction of fellow believers
  • the wisdom of those with experience
  • and above all, God's voice through His Word.

👉 A heart that knows how to listen is a heart that is willing to be shaped by God.

Pride Prevents Us from Learning

One of the greatest obstacles to wisdom is pride.

When we always think we are right, we stop asking questions.

We stop listening.

We stop accepting correction.

But a humble person is not afraid to admit when he is wrong.

He understands that correction is not an enemy but one of God's tools for helping him grow.

👉 A humble heart grows in wisdom, but a proud heart remains foolish.

Mary Chose to Listen to Jesus

We see a beautiful example of this in the life of Mary.

While Martha was busy with many tasks, Mary sat at Jesus' feet and listened to His teaching.

Martha's service was not wrong.

But Jesus pointed out that one thing was even more important.

Mary chose to listen to the Word of God.

That was the one thing that could never be taken away from her.

The same is true for us today.

We have many responsibilities.

We are busy with many activities.

Yet we must never neglect spending time listening to and studying God's Word.

Many people talk about God, but not everyone truly knows Him or has a genuine relationship with Him.

👉 Before we speak about God, we must first know Him through His Word and develop a genuine relationship with Him.

Listen Before You Speak

Another lesson we learn from this verse is the importance of controlling our speech.

James teaches us:

"Be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath."

Many problems can be avoided if we first learn to listen before we speak.

When we listen with humility, we become more careful with our words.

We understand situations more clearly.

And we become instruments of peace rather than conflict.

👉 Those who know how to listen usually know how to speak wisely as well.

Application: Am I a Good Listener?

Examine yourself:

  • Do I accept wise counsel even when it is difficult to hear?
  • Do I know how to listen before I speak?
  • How do I respond when someone corrects me?
  • Do I set aside time to listen to God through His Word?
  • Do I care more about expressing my own opinions than learning from others?

A wise person is never ashamed to learn.

Instead, he continually allows God to shape his life.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:8 teaches that a person with understanding welcomes wise instruction, while a fool often brings ruin upon himself because he prefers talking over listening.

In our generation, filled with noise, opinions, and endless conversations, we need hearts that are willing to become still and listen to God.

We do not grow in wisdom because we speak more.

We grow when we allow God to teach us through His Word, through the people He uses, and even through correction that is sometimes difficult to accept.

A person who knows how to listen is easier for God to guide.

And the one who is easily guided by God is far more likely to walk in the path of wisdom and life.

The question:

When God speaks through His Word, through life's circumstances, or through the people He uses, do you have a heart that is willing to listen and obey, or do you give more attention to your own voice than to His?
