Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:7

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The memory of the righteous is blessed, But the name of the wicked will rot.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

What Kind of Legacy Will You Leave Behind?

Everyone Leaves a Legacy

The day will come when each one of us will leave this world.

No one will remain here forever.

Yet even after our lives are over, one thing will remain.

It is the legacy or memory we leave in the lives of the people we have encountered.

Solomon says:

👉 "The memory of the righteous is blessed, but the name of the wicked will rot."

This verse does not say that the righteous will never die.

Both the righteous and the wicked will face death.

But the memories they leave behind are very different.

👉 We cannot choose when our lives will end, but we can choose the kind of legacy we will leave behind.

The Righteous Leave a Blessed Legacy

A person who lives according to God's will leaves an imprint that is not easily erased.

He may never have become famous.

He may never have become wealthy.

But he is remembered for his:

  • faithfulness
  • kindness
  • love
  • integrity
  • and faith in God

When his family, friends, or church remember him, their hearts are filled with gratitude to God.

Not because he was perfect,

but because he allowed God to use him as a blessing to others.

👉 The greatest legacy we can leave is not our possessions but our godly way of life.

The Wicked Leave Behind Shame

On the other hand, Solomon says that the name of the wicked will rot.

A person who lives in wickedness, deceit, and selfishness may become well known during his lifetime.

But as time passes, his name fades away or is remembered only because of his evil deeds.

Wealth can disappear.

Power can be lost.

Fame can be forgotten.

But a life of wickedness leaves wounds and shame that are difficult to erase.

👉 A reputation built on sin does not last; in the end, it leads to disgrace.

The True Measure of Success

Today, many people strive to become well known.

They want to gain more followers.

They want their names to be recognized.

They desire success and influence.

But the question is not merely how many people know us.

The more important question is:

What will they remember about us?

A simple life that became a blessing to many is far better than a famous life that brought only pain and destruction.

👉 A good name before God is far more valuable than a great name before the world.

Christ's Legacy

No one has ever left a greater legacy than Jesus Christ.

He did not leave behind wealth.

He did not build a palace.

He did not establish a powerful earthly kingdom.

Instead, He gave humanity the greatest gift of all—His own life.

Through His death and resurrection, millions of people from every generation have received forgiveness, hope, and eternal life.

As followers of Christ, we are also called to live in such a way that we leave behind a legacy of faith for the next generation.

👉 The greatest legacy we can leave is a life that teaches others to know and follow Christ.

Live for What Has Eternal Value

The day will come when it will no longer matter how much wealth we accumulated.

It will no longer matter how famous we became.

What will remain is the impact our lives had on God's kingdom.

Some people came to Christ because of our prayers.

Some children learned to follow God because of our example.

Some people grew stronger in their faith because of our words and our way of life.

These are the memories that carry eternal value.

👉 A life devoted to God continues to bear fruit even after our time on earth has ended.

Application: What Will You Leave for the Next Generation?

Examine yourself:

  • When my family remembers me, what will be the first thing they remember about me?
  • Does my way of life teach others to love and follow God?
  • What do I pursue more—being popular or being faithful?
  • If God were to call me home today, what spiritual legacy would I leave behind?
  • Have people drawn closer to Christ because of my life?

Every day is an opportunity to build a legacy that brings glory to God.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:7 teaches that the life of the righteous leaves behind a memory that inspires others, while the name of the wicked gradually fades away or is remembered only for evil.

God does not measure success by the amount of wealth we accumulate, the length of our fame, or the size of our influence.

He measures it by our faithfulness to Him and the impact our lives have on others.

The day will come when all our earthly possessions will pass away.

But the faith we lived out, the souls we led to Christ, the hearts we strengthened, and our faithfulness to God will remain of eternal value.

Therefore, let us live not merely for temporary success but for a legacy that brings glory to God and becomes a blessing to future generations.

The question:

When your family, friends, and those who have known you remember you many years from now, what kind of legacy will they recall—a life lived for yourself, or a life that faithfully served and glorified God?
