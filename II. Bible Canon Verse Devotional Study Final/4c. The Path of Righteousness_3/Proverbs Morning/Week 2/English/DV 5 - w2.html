Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:11

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The mouth of the righteous is a well of life, But violence covers the mouth of the wicked.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Power of Our Words

Every day, we use words.

We may not always realize it, but every word we speak has the power either to bring life or to inflict wounds.

Solomon says:

👉 "The mouth of the righteous is a well of life, but the mouth of the wicked conceals violence."

This verse shows that our speech reflects the condition of our hearts.

The righteous speak words that bring life.

In contrast, the wicked use their words to conceal evil and harm others.

👉 Our words reveal who truly reigns in our hearts.

The Mouth of the Righteous Is a Well of Life

Solomon describes the mouth of the righteous as a "well of life."

A well continually provides fresh and clean water.

It refreshes those who are thirsty.

In the same way, the words that come from a person who walks with God bring life to others.

His words:

  • give hope to the discouraged,
  • strengthen the weary,
  • teach the truth to those who have gone astray,
  • and bring peace to the troubled.

👉 A person filled with God's wisdom becomes a channel of blessing through his words.

Words Can Heal or Hurt

A single word can change the course of someone's life.

A word of forgiveness can heal a wound that has remained for years.

A word of encouragement can give strength to someone who is ready to give up.

Yet a word filled with anger, slander, or lies can also leave deep wounds that are difficult to heal.

That is why we must be careful with the words we speak.

👉 Before speaking, ask yourself: Will these words bring life or cause wounds?

The Wicked Use Words to Conceal Evil

On the other hand, Solomon says that the mouth of the wicked conceals violence.

This means they use their words to cover up their evil deeds.

They may lie to escape accountability.

They may deceive others to get what they want.

They may use flattering words to hide evil intentions.

But no deception can ever deceive God.

He sees not only our words but also the true motives of our hearts.

👉 People may be deceived by our words, but God never will.

Jesus Is the Well of Life

The greatest example of life-giving words is Jesus.

He said:

"The words that I have spoken to you are spirit, and they are life."

Every word of Christ brings truth, hope, forgiveness, and eternal life.

He never spoke to tear people down.

He never spoke to deceive others.

Instead, He used His words to bring people to the Father.

As followers of Christ, we are also called to become channels of His life through our words.

👉 When Christ reigns in our hearts, our mouths become instruments of His grace.

A Believer's Words Should Build Others Up

Many people are already weary from the hurtful words of this world.

Many experience judgment, ridicule, and hopelessness.

As believers, we have the opportunity to become channels of God's love.

Our words should:

  • give hope to those who have lost hope,
  • strengthen those who are weak,
  • speak the truth in love,
  • and make Christ known at every opportunity.

👉 Every word spoken by a believer should impart grace and point others to Christ.

Living It Out: What Comes Out of My Mouth?

Examine yourself:

  • Do my words bring life or cause pain?
  • Am I often complaining, slandering, or judging others?
  • Do I use my mouth to encourage others?
  • Do my words reflect the love and truth of Christ?
  • If others heard my words every day, would they be drawn closer to God?

A mouth transformed by God becomes a well of life for others.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:11 teaches us that our speech is never a small matter in God's sight.

The mouth of the righteous becomes a well of life because the heart has been transformed by God.

But the mouth of the wicked is used to conceal evil and bring destruction.

As followers of Christ, we are called to be careful with every word we speak.

May our lips become instruments of God to bring hope to the discouraged, truth to those who have gone astray, peace to the troubled, and blessing to everyone we encounter.

For in a world filled with destructive speech, words that flow from a heart transformed by Christ can become a well of life that draws others closer to Him.

The question:

If people listened to your words every day, would they find a well of life flowing from Christ, or would they hear only words that bring wounds and destruction?
