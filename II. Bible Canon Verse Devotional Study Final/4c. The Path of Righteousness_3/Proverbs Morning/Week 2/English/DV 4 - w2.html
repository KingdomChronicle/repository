Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:10

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

He who winks with the eye causes trouble, But a prating fool will fall.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Wickedness Begins in the Heart and Reveals Itself Through Actions

In this verse, Solomon describes two kinds of people who bring trouble.

The first is the person who "winks with the eye."

The second is the one with a "prating mouth."

Although they are described differently, they produce the same result.

Both ultimately lead to destruction.

👉 When the heart is filled with evil, it will be revealed through our actions and our words.

The Wink of the Eye Is a Picture of Hidden Evil

During Solomon's time, winking or signaling with the eyes was not merely a harmless gesture.

It was often used as a secret signal to carry out deception or evil schemes.

It portrays a person who secretly practices wickedness.

They may not say anything.

Yet their heart is filled with deceit.

They desire to manipulate others or stir up trouble.

Today, this may no longer be expressed through a wink.

Instead, it can be seen in secret plans, deception, fraud, and every attempt to mislead others.

👉 God not only sees what we do; He also sees the hidden motives of our hearts.

An Unrestrained Mouth Brings Destruction

Solomon also speaks of the person with a "prating mouth."

This refers to someone who speaks recklessly.

Someone who loves to argue.

Someone who spreads gossip.

Someone who is quick to judge.

And someone who never considers the impact of their words.

Many relationships have been ruined by a single careless statement.

Many friendships have been destroyed because of reckless speech.

Many families have been divided by words spoken in anger.

👉 A mouth that is not governed by wisdom can become the source of great destruction.

True Wisdom Is Seen in Self-Control

Wisdom is not measured by intelligence alone.

It also includes the ability to control our actions and our speech.

A wise person does not immediately act on impulse.

Neither do they speak whatever comes into their mind.

They understand that there is a proper time to speak.

And there is also a proper time to remain silent.

👉 A wise person not only knows how to speak—they also know how to exercise self-control.

Christ Transforms Our Hearts and Our Speech

Life transformation begins in the heart.

When Christ changes our hearts, our actions and our speech gradually change as well.

We no longer delight in deception.

We no longer enjoy tearing others down.

We no longer seek ways to hurt our neighbors.

Instead, we use our words to encourage others, give hope, and speak the truth in love.

👉 A mouth transformed by Christ becomes an instrument of blessing rather than destruction.

There Is Hope for Those Who Have Failed

The good news is that there is no one whom God cannot change.

The Apostle Paul was once a fierce persecutor of the church.

He spread fear and turmoil among believers.

But when he encountered Christ, his life was completely transformed.

The one who once tried to destroy the church became a faithful preacher of the gospel.

This reminds us that no heart is too hardened for God's grace to transform.

👉 Christ is able to transform even those who once caused trouble into instruments of His peace.

Living It Out: What Does My Life Reveal?

Examine yourself:

  • Are there secret things in my life that do not please God?
  • Do my words bring peace or conflict?
  • Do I often speak without considering the consequences?
  • Do I use my mouth to build others up or to wound them?
  • Can others see the transforming work of Christ in my heart through the way I live?

Wisdom is not only seen in what we know but also in the way we live each day.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:10 teaches that wickedness is revealed not only through obvious sins but also through hidden schemes and careless speech.

Those who rely on deception and destructive words will eventually reap destruction.

But God is able to transform even a heart once filled with evil.

When we allow Christ to reign in our lives, He changes not only our actions but also our thoughts, motives, and words.

May we become people who bring peace instead of conflict, using our lips to proclaim the truth, offer hope, and extend God's grace to others.

The question is:

When people hear your words and observe your actions, do they see a heart being transformed by Christ, or one that continues to bring trouble and destruction?
