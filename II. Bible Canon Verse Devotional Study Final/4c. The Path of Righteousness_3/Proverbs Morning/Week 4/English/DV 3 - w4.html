Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:21

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The lips of the righteous feed many, But fools die for lack of wisdom.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Life-Giving Words That Nourish Many

Solomon continues to reveal the tremendous power of our words. After comparing the tongue of the righteous to choice silver, he now shows the fruit of those words.

He says:

"The lips of the righteous feed many, but fools die for lack of wisdom."

It is not only the speaker who benefits from the wisdom within him.

His words also become a blessing to many people.

Just as food strengthens the body, words that come from God strengthen the soul.

👉 A person filled with wisdom not only grows personally but also becomes a blessing in the lives of others.

Good Words Are Spiritual Food

Food is necessary to sustain our physical bodies.

But there is another kind of food that is even more important—spiritual nourishment.

Jesus said:

"Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of God."

When a believer speaks the Word of God, it is as though he is serving food to hungry souls.

His words:

  • teach the truth,
  • strengthen the weary,
  • give hope to the hopeless,
  • correct those who have gone astray,
  • and lead people to Christ.

👉 The finest spiritual food we can offer others is the truth of God's Word.

The Word of God Gives Life

It not only strengthens believers.

It is also the means God uses to bring people into new life.

The apostle Peter wrote that the new birth comes through the living and enduring Word of God.

Therefore, whenever we share the gospel, we are not merely giving information.

We are sharing the message that has the power to save, transform hearts, and give eternal life.

This is why Peter said to Jesus:

"You have the words of eternal life."

👉 The most important words we can ever share are the message of Christ.

We Are Called to Feed Many

Not everyone is called to be a pastor or preacher.

But every believer is called to become a blessing through his or her words.

In our homes...

At our workplaces...

In our schools...

In our churches...

There are people who need:

  • a word of encouragement,
  • a reminder from God,
  • wise counsel,
  • or a simple proclamation of the gospel.

We may never realize it, but a single word spoken in love and truth can be used by God to change someone's life.

👉 We do not need to speak many words to become a blessing; we simply need to speak words that come from God.

The Fool Has No Life to Offer

On the other hand, Solomon says:

"But fools die for lack of wisdom."

A person who rejects God's wisdom not only brings ruin upon himself.

He also has nothing with which to nourish others.

His words may be filled with opinions, complaints, gossip, or deception, but they cannot impart true life.

He cannot give direction to others because he himself has gone astray.

This is the great difference between the person who lives according to God Word and the one who relies only on his own wisdom.

👉 We cannot provide spiritual nourishment to others if we ourselves are not feeding on the Word of God.

Living It Out: What Am I Feeding Others?

Examine yourself.

  • Do my words bring hope and strength to others?
  • Do I regularly share God's Word with my family and friends?
  • When I speak, do people hear more of my opinions or the truth of God?
  • Am I continually feeding on God's Word so that I will have something to share with others?
  • Is there someone God is using me to strengthen through my words?

A person who fills his heart daily with God's Word will never run out of good words to share with others.

CONCLUSION

King Solomon teaches us that the words of the righteous become a blessing to many people. Their words do not merely offer good advice; they also bring life, hope, and truth from God. In contrast, those who reject wisdom end in destruction and have no true life to offer others.

As followers of Christ, we are called to become channels of His Word. As we continue to grow in our relationship with the Lord and feed on His Word, He uses us to nourish souls that are hungry for truth and hope.

The question:

When people hear your words, are they drawn closer to Christ and strengthened in their faith, or do they receive no spiritual nourishment from what you say?
