Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:22

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The blessing of the Lord makes one rich, And He adds no sorrow with it.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

True Riches Come from God's Blessing

Today, many people believe that wealth is the key to security and happiness. As a result, some are willing to do anything to become rich—even through dishonest means or in ways that do not please God.

But Proverbs 10:22 reminds us:

"The blessing of the Lord makes one rich, and He adds no sorrow with it."

This verse does not teach that everyone who follows God will certainly become materially wealthy. It is true that God blessed some of His servants with great abundance, yet others lived in poverty. God's blessing is not measured by the amount of our possessions but by His good purpose and the peace that accompanies His provision.

👉 True riches are not merely measured by how much we possess, but by whether what we receive is the result of God's blessing and is used for His glory.

Wealth Without God Is Not True Security

A person may acquire great wealth through fraud, exploitation, or corruption.

But no matter how much he owns, money cannot buy genuine peace and security.

We can see this all around us. As people's wealth increases, they work even harder to protect it. They build high walls, install strong gates, hire security guards, and use various security systems to safeguard their possessions. There is nothing wrong with being careful, but this also shows that wealth alone cannot provide complete peace of mind.

There are also those who live in fear because the wealth they enjoy was gained through fraud, corruption, or other practices that are not pleasing to God. As a result, they constantly worry that the truth will be exposed, that they will lose their wealth, or that they will have to face the consequences of their actions.

On the other hand, a person who walks uprightly may never enjoy a luxurious lifestyle, yet he has peace because he knows that God is his Provider and that his life is in the Lord's hands.

This is also what Jesus taught. He told us not to store up treasures on earth where they can be destroyed or stolen, but to store up treasures in heaven. A person's heart will always follow what he considers to be his treasure. If our trust is in money, we will never find it sufficient. But if our trust is in God, we will find a peace that no one can take away.

👉 Not every wealthy person has peace, and not every poor person lives without anxiety. But true security does not come from the amount of our possessions; it comes from God, who is the source of every blessing.

God Is the True Source of Every Blessing

God warned Israel not to think that their success was simply the result of their own abilities.

When they became prosperous in the Promised Land, they could easily say, "My own strength has gained all of this for me."

But God reminded them that He is the One who gives the ability to prosper.

The same is true for us today.

It is easy to take pride in our hard work, intelligence, or business success.

Yet every opportunity, every measure of health, every talent, and every open door is still a gift from God.

👉 When we acknowledge that God is the source of our blessings, we remain humble and grateful.

God's Blessings Have a Purpose

Neither should we think that receiving material blessings from God is wrong. Throughout Scripture, we see that many of God's servants were truly blessed by Him.

God blessed Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Job, and Boaz with abundance and many possessions. Yet their wealth never replaced their faith. Instead, they used their blessings in ways that honored God and became a blessing to others.

This is also God's purpose for His people today. When He blesses His children, it is not merely for their personal comfort or something to boast about. He entrusts these blessings to us so that we may become faithful stewards of His gifts.

The blessings God gives can be used to support the work of the gospel, help those in need, strengthen the ministry of the church, and participate in the continued growth and expansion of His kingdom here on earth.

This shows that wealth itself is not the problem; the real issue is the condition of the human heart. When blessings become a reason for pride, self-reliance, or drifting away from God, they become dangerous. But when we recognize that everything comes from the Lord, what we receive becomes an instrument for glorifying Him.

👉 God's blessings are not merely privileges to be enjoyed, but responsibilities to be used for His glory and for the advancement of His kingdom.

The Greatest Blessing Is Christ

Far greater than any material wealth is the greatest blessing God has given—His Son, Jesus Christ.

Through Him we have received forgiveness of sins, reconciliation with God, eternal life, and a hope that will never fade.

Money may be lost.

A business may fail.

Possessions may disappear.

But the riches we have in Christ can never be taken away.

And if God chooses to bless us with material possessions, let us remember that we are only stewards of them. But the salvation we have in Christ is eternal and can never be taken from us.

👉 The richest person is not the one who owns the most possessions, but the one who has Christ as his greatest treasure.

Living It Out: What Do I Consider My Treasure?

Examine yourself:

  • What gives me a sense of security—God or my money?
  • Do I thank God for every blessing I receive?
  • If God blesses me, do I use my time, talents, and resources to help others and support the work of His kingdom?
  • Am I willing to remain faithful even if prosperity comes more slowly?
  • Do I value God's peace more than wealth gained through dishonest means?
  • Do I see Christ as my greatest treasure?

CONCLUSION

King Solomon teaches us that true riches are not measured by the amount of our possessions but by the blessing that comes from God.

God may choose to bless His children with material abundance, just as He did Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Job, Boaz, and many others. Yet these blessings were never an end in themselves. God gives them so that He may be honored, others may be blessed, and His kingdom may continue to grow and expand through His servants.

On the other hand, wealth gained through dishonest means may bring temporary prosperity, but it is often accompanied by fear, anxiety, and the absence of true peace. In contrast, God's blessing brings peace because we know that He is our Provider and that we are only stewards of what He has entrusted to us.

Above all, the greatest treasure God can give is not gold or silver, but the salvation we have in Jesus Christ. All the riches of this world will one day pass away, but eternal life and our relationship with the Lord will remain forever.

The question:

What do you value more—the blessings God has entrusted to you to be used for His glory and the eternal treasure you have in Christ, or the temporary riches of this world that can never provide true peace and security?
