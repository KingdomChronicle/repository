Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:20

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

The tongue of the righteous is choice silver; The heart of the wicked is worth little.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The True Value Lies Not Only in Our Words, but in the Heart They Come From

Solomon continues to emphasize the importance of our speech. This time, however, he shows that the true value of our words depends on the condition of our hearts.

He said:

"The tongue of the righteous is choice silver, but the heart of the wicked is worth little."

Silver was one of the most valuable metals in Solomon's day. It was treasured for its purity, durability, and great worth.

So it is with the speech of the righteous.

Their words are valuable.

They are meaningful.

They are beneficial.

They have the power to bring life and hope to others.

👉 A person who walks with God becomes a blessing not only through their actions but also through their words.

Words That Come from God Bring Life

The speech of the righteous is not merely pleasant to hear.

It:

  • teaches the truth,
  • offers wise counsel,
  • encourages the discouraged,
  • gives hope,
  • and leads people closer to God.

Like silver refined by fire, their words are careful, truthful, and trustworthy.

This is also why the Word of God is so precious in our lives.

The Lord's words are pure and true.

As we allow His Word to fill our hearts, our mouths also become channels of His truth.

👉 When the heart is filled with God's Word, the mouth becomes an instrument of blessing.

Our Words Reflect Our Hearts

On the other hand, Solomon said:

"But the heart of the wicked is worth little."

This is a sobering comparison.

He did not simply say that the wicked speak wrongly.

He pointed to the condition of their very hearts.

This is because our words are merely the fruit of what fills our hearts.

Jesus taught the same truth:

"For out of the abundance of the heart his mouth speaks." (Luke 6:45)

If our hearts are filled with:

  • anger,
  • envy,
  • pride,
  • deceit,
  • or bitterness,

those same things will come out in our speech.

We cannot hide the true condition of our hearts forever, because sooner or later it will be revealed by our words.

👉 Our speech cannot truly change until God first changes our hearts.

Our Words Can Build Up or Tear Down

Every day we speak thousands of words.

The question is:

What do those words leave behind in the lives of others?

Some people leave others encouraged after they speak.

Some bring hope.

Some communicate the truth in love.

But there are also those whose every word leaves wounds.

Slander.

Gossip.

Judgment.

Cursing.

Insults.

This is the difference between a heart being shaped by God and a heart ruled by sin.

👉 Every word that comes from our mouths can become either a seed of life or a seed of destruction.

Christ Is the Greatest Example

No one has ever spoken with greater wisdom than Jesus.

His words:

  • taught the truth,
  • gave hope,
  • brought healing to broken hearts,
  • led sinners to repentance,
  • and offered eternal life.

Even when He corrected people, He did so with both love and truth.

As followers of Christ, we are likewise called to speak in ways that reflect His grace.

👉 When Christ reigns in our hearts, He will also be seen in our words.

Living It Out: What Is the Value of My Words?

Examine yourself.

  • Do my words bring hope or cause pain?
  • When I speak, do people hear more of my anger or the grace of God?
  • Is my heart filled with God's Word so that it also flows from my mouth?
  • Do I use my speech to strengthen the faith of others?
  • If someone evaluated my words, would they see the transformation Christ has made in my heart?

Our speech is one of the clearest pieces of evidence of who truly reigns in our hearts.

CONCLUSION

Proverbs 10:20 teaches that the true value of our speech is measured not by how eloquently we speak, but by the source from which our words come. The tongue of the righteous is like choice silver because it is used to speak the truth, give hope, and impart wisdom. But a heart that is separated from God has little to offer except words that destroy and lack eternal value.

If we desire our words to become a blessing, it is not enough to simply change the way we speak. We must allow Christ to transform our hearts. As our hearts are filled with His truth, love, and grace, our mouths will also become channels of life to those around us.

The question is:

If people listened to your words every day, would they hear the wisdom and love of Christ, or would they discover areas of your heart that still need God's transforming work?
