Daily Devotional Verses



Proverbs 10:23

NKJV (New King James Version)

The Proverbs of Solomon:

To do evil is like sport to a fool, But a man of understanding has wisdom.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

What Truly Brings Joy to Your Heart?

Everyone has things that bring them pleasure. Some find joy in helping others, being honest, and doing what is good. But there are also those who delight in taking advantage of others, slander, immorality, and other actions that oppose the will of God. These are not merely differences in behavior—they reflect the condition of the heart.

In Proverbs 10:23, Solomon does not merely compare two kinds of actions but two different kinds of pleasure. The wicked delight in sin, while the person of understanding delights in living according to the wisdom of God.

Solomon said:

“To do evil is like sport to a fool, But a man of understanding has wisdom.”

The verse shows that a person's true character is revealed not only by what he does but also by the things that bring him pleasure. The heart always follows what it loves most.

👉 Our deepest pleasures reveal who truly reigns in our hearts.

When Sin Becomes Entertainment

One sign of a heart that is far from God is treating sin as something entertaining. Slander becomes a joke. Immorality becomes a source of laughter. Deceit is regarded as being “resourceful.” Lying becomes merely a convenient way to get what one wants.

We see this not only in society but also in many shows, movies, and social media content. Sin is often presented as normal, amusing, or attractive. When we repeatedly watch and laugh at it, our consciences can gradually become numb.

This does not mean that humor is wrong. The warning of Proverbs is against laughing at the things God declares to be evil. Sin that should lead us to repentance must not be turned into entertainment.

👉 When sin becomes a source of pleasure, our hearts also gradually become hardened against God.

A Heart of Understanding Delights in the Will of God

On the other hand, a person of understanding has a different kind of joy. He does not follow God merely out of fear of punishment or a sense of obligation. He learns to love what is right because he understands that it is the path of true life.

Doing what is right can sometimes be difficult. There are times when we must forgive even when it hurts, remain honest even when it results in loss, or speak the truth even when it is unpopular. Yet the person of understanding experiences a deeper joy because he knows that God is pleased with the way he lives.

True wisdom is not merely knowing the difference between right and wrong. It is a heart that delights in obeying the Lord even when no one is watching.

👉 A person who truly possesses wisdom finds joy in living in a way that pleases God.

Christ Is the Perfect Example of Joyful Obedience

This principle was perfectly displayed in the life of Jesus Christ. His delight was to do the will of the Father. He did not live to pursue His own desires but to fulfill God's purpose, even when it led Him to the cross.

We, however, often fall into the opposite pattern. Many times, we have chosen the temporary pleasure of sin over the joy that comes from obeying God. But through the grace of Christ, we are not only forgiven; we are also given new hearts that gradually learn to love the things God loves.

Through the Holy Spirit, He transforms our desires. The things that once brought us pleasure gradually lose their appeal, while the things that once seemed difficult to obey become a source of joy as our relationship with Christ grows deeper.

Jesus does not merely change what we do—He changes what we desire most.

👉 The grace of Christ not only forgives sin but also gives us new hearts that delight in obeying God.

Choose a Joy That Leaves No Regret

Every day, we choose what we will allow to shape our hearts. The things we watch, read, listen to, and enjoy have a powerful influence on our thinking and way of life.

It is good to ask ourselves: What do I often laugh at? What brings me pleasure? Do these things draw me closer to God, or are they gradually leading me away from Him?

As we fill our minds more with the Word of God and deepen our walk with Christ, we discover more and more that obeying Him is not a burden but a source of true joy.

👉 The most enduring joy does not come from sin but from living with God and for God.

Living It Out: What Gives Me True Pleasure?

Take time to examine yourself before God.

  • What things do I often enjoy, and what do they reveal about my heart?

  • Are there sins that I treat as jokes or as something normal even though they clearly displease God?

  • Do I delight in doing what is right even when no one sees or praises me?

  • How do the things I watch, listen to, and follow affect my view of sin?

  • Is my joy in Christ growing deeper than the temporary pleasures the world offers?

As we come to know God more deeply, we discover that no pleasure can compare with living according to His will.

CONCLUSION

Here King Solomon teaches that the clearest difference between the righteous and the wicked is seen in what they delight in. The wicked rejoice in sin, but the person of understanding finds pleasure in wisdom and obedience to God.

The verse reveals that our pleasures are not merely personal preferences. They show where our hearts are directed. Sin may offer temporary enjoyment, but it always leaves behind regret and destruction.

In Jesus Christ we find true transformation. He gives us new hearts that not only turn away from sin but also learn to love righteousness. As we walk with Him, our greatest delight becomes doing the will of God.

👉 A heart truly transformed by Christ gradually loses its appetite for sin and increasingly delights in living in a way that pleases God.

Question:

If God were to examine the things you truly delight in each day, would He see a heart that rejoices in His will, or a heart that continues to be entertained by the very things He calls sin?
