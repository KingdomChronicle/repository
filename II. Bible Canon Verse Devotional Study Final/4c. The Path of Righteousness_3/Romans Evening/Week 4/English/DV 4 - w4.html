Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:15-17

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

15 But the free gift is not like the offense. For if by the one man’s offense many died, much more the grace of God and the gift by the grace of the one Man, Jesus Christ, abounded to many. 16 And the gift is not like that which came through the one who sinned. For the judgment which came from one offense resulted in condemnation, but the free gift which came from many offenses resulted in justification. 17 For if by the one man’s offense death reigned through the one, much more those who receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness will reign in life through the One, Jesus Christ.)

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Gift That Brings Justification

In the previous part of the verse, Paul showed that Adam's one act of disobedience brought condemnation upon all humanity. But his comparison does not end there. In this portion, he directs our attention to God's marvelous response to the problem of sin—His free gift through Jesus Christ.

"But the free gift which came from many offenses resulted in justification."

How beautiful is the contrast that Paul presents. One act of disobedience resulted in condemnation, yet God gave a gift that brings justification even after humanity's countless transgressions. As the sins of mankind continued to increase, God's grace did not run out. Instead, He Himself provided the way for sinners to be forgiven and restored to a right relationship with Him.

Justification does not mean that God overlooked sin or simply treated the guilty as though they had never sinned. Rather, it is His righteous declaration that the one who believes in Jesus Christ is now righteous before Him. This righteousness is not the result of man's own goodness but of what Christ has accomplished for us.

The greatness of this grace becomes even more evident when we notice that Paul says this gift came after many offenses. God was not dealing with only one sin but with humanity's repeated rebellion against Him. Yet instead of allowing condemnation to prevail, He provided the way of justification through His Son.

This reveals the true condition of mankind. This is not a new truth in Paul's letter. From the earlier chapters of Romans, he has already made it clear that no one is righteous by his own ability, and no one is without sin. All have sinned and fallen short of the glory that God intended. Therefore, if salvation depended on our own goodness or on our ability to avoid sin, not one of us would have any hope of being justified.

The more we understand Paul's words, the more we recognize the depth of our need for God's grace. If Adam's one act of disobedience brought condemnation upon all humanity, how much more should we, who continually sin in thought, word, and deed, recognize our desperate need? If God were to judge us solely on the basis of our own works, not one of us could stand righteous before Him.

Yet this is where the grace of God shines even more brightly. Justification was not given because our sins were few, but in spite of our countless transgressions. Therefore, salvation is truly a gift of grace and not a reward for good works. If Adam's one sin brought condemnation, then our only hope in the face of our many sins is the abundant grace of God given through Jesus Christ.

Because this is a gift of His grace, there is hope for everyone who comes to Jesus Christ in faith. He does not wait for our goodness to become sufficient before He accepts us. Instead, He receives all who come to Him with repentance and faith in what Christ has done. When we truly understand this truth, both our pride and our despair disappear. We have no reason to boast because salvation is not something we have earned. Yet neither should we lose hope, for God's grace is far greater than the multitude of our transgressions.

CONCLUSION:

This verse shows that God's response to humanity's countless sins is His free gift that justifies those who believe in Jesus Christ. Our hope does not rest in our own righteousness but in His abundant grace.

Through Christ, those who were once under condemnation can now be accepted by God as righteous. This is not the result of our own goodness but of His grace freely given to all who come to Him in faith.

Therefore, let us not place our trust in our own ability or good works. Instead, let us trust in Jesus Christ, who has given the gift that brings justification to all who believe in Him.

In the end, as man's disobedience increases, the marvelous grace of God shines even more brightly. Through Jesus Christ, His free gift brings justification, so our hope rests not in our own righteousness but in His abundant grace.
