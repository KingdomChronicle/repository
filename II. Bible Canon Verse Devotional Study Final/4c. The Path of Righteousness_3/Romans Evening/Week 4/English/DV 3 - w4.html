Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:15-17

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

15 But the free gift is not like the offense. For if by the one man’s offense many died, much more the grace of God and the gift by the grace of the one Man, Jesus Christ, abounded to many. 16 And the gift is not like that which came through the one who sinned. For the judgment which came from one offense resulted in condemnation, but the free gift which came from many offenses resulted in justification. 17 For if by the one man’s offense death reigned through the one, much more those who receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness will reign in life through the One, Jesus Christ.)

GUIDE REFLECTION:

One Act of Disobedience Brought Condemnation

In the preceding part of the passage, Paul showed that God's grace is far greater than the damage caused by Adam's sin. Now, he explains why God's gift can never be compared with the result of Adam's disobedience. One brought condemnation, while the other serves an entirely different purpose in God's redemptive plan.

“This gift is not like the result of Adam's disobedience. For the judgment that followed one act of disobedience brought condemnation,”

Paul emphasizes that the outcomes of these two acts are completely different. Adam's disobedience opened the way for God's judgment. It was not merely the consequence of a poor decision, but the righteous response of a holy God against sin. God is perfectly holy and just; therefore, He cannot overlook disobedience.

It is also noteworthy that Paul says judgment came after one act of disobedience. This reveals how serious sin is in the sight of God. It does not take many sins to deserve judgment. A single act of disobedience against God's clear command was enough to bring condemnation.

This also demonstrates Adam's position as the representative of humanity. His disobedience affected not only himself but also opened the way for sin and condemnation to reach all mankind. As a result, the entire human race came to live under the penalty brought about by sin.

In our day, we often minimize sin. We tend to think that some sins are not that serious or can simply be overlooked. Yet this passage reminds us that every act of disobedience is committed against a holy God. The seriousness of sin is not measured by our own standards but by the holiness of the God whom we have offended.

At the same time, Paul is preparing his readers for an even more astonishing truth. If one act of disobedience brought condemnation, what then will God's gift through Jesus Christ accomplish? This is the question he answers in the next part of the passage.

CONCLUSION:

This passage shows that sin is never insignificant in God's eyes. Adam's single act of disobedience brought condemnation and judgment, revealing both God's holiness and His justice.

Therefore, we should never take our own sin lightly or imagine that we can cover it with our good works. Our condition reminds us of how desperately we need the grace of God.

So let us humble ourselves before God and acknowledge the seriousness of sin. A right understanding of our true condition prepares our hearts to more fully grasp the greatness of His gift.

In the end, this passage teaches that one act of disobedience was enough to bring condemnation because God is holy and just. Yet as we recognize the weight of sin, God is also preparing us to understand more deeply the far greater grace that He would reveal through Jesus Christ.
