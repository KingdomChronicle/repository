Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:15-17

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

15 But the free gift is not like the offense. For if by the one man’s offense many died, much more the grace of God and the gift by the grace of the one Man, Jesus Christ, abounded to many. 16 And the gift is not like that which came through the one who sinned. For the judgment which came from one offense resulted in condemnation, but the free gift which came from many offenses resulted in justification. 17 For if by the one man’s offense death reigned through the one, much more those who receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness will reign in life through the One, Jesus Christ.)

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Reigning in Life

In the previous part of the passage, Paul showed that because of Adam's disobedience, death reigned over all humanity. But that is not God's final word. In this concluding comparison between Adam and Jesus Christ, Paul reveals the ultimate triumph of God's grace. If death reigned through one man, far greater is what Christ has accomplished for those who receive His grace.

“But through the one Man, Jesus Christ, those who receive the abundance of grace and the gift of righteousness will reign in life.”

Paul did not merely say that those who believe in Christ will have life. He said that they will reign in life. What a remarkable difference! If death reigned over humanity through Adam, those who have received God's abundant grace are now portrayed as those who will reign. Those who were once under the dominion of death have not merely been set free; they have been raised to a new position through Jesus Christ.

Paul also emphasizes that this promise belongs to those who have received the abundance of grace and the gift of righteousness. This new standing is not the result of human goodness or personal effort. It is the result of God's overflowing grace and the justification He has graciously given through Christ. Therefore, all praise belongs to Him alone.

This reign begins even now in the new life received by every believer, yet it will be fully revealed at Christ's return. As children of God, we have become fellow heirs with Christ. Thus, the Scriptures declare, “For both He who sanctifies and those who are being sanctified are all of one, for which reason He is not ashamed to call them brethren.” (Hebrews 2:11). Those who were once sinners have been welcomed into God's family and given a standing they could never attain through their own efforts.

Yet the greatness of God's grace does not end there. The day is coming when our frail bodies will be transformed into glorified bodies like the glorious body of Christ. The creation that was once subjected to the curse because of sin will give way to a new heaven and a new earth where there will be no more sin, suffering, or death.

All of this demonstrates that what Christ accomplished is not merely a temporary remedy for the consequences of sin. It is the complete fulfillment of God's plan for humanity, a plan that began even after Adam's first fall.

As we learned in the previous reflection, humanity has never stopped searching for a cure for death. Despite the advances of medicine, science, and technology, mankind remains powerless to remove the dominion of death. Yet the marvelous truth is that God did not wait for humanity to discover the solution. From the very moment Adam and Eve fell, He had already revealed His plan of redemption. In Genesis 3:15, He promised the coming Savior, and in the fullness of time, that promise was perfectly fulfilled through the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

Therefore, while humanity continues searching for a way to conquer death, the Gospel declares that the true solution has long been provided by God. Victory over death is not the result of human progress but of the finished work of Jesus Christ. That is why Paul can say that those who receive His abundant grace have not merely escaped death, but will reign in life. What all humanity has long sought is found only in Christ.

Even God's original purpose for humanity will be completely fulfilled in Christ. Adam was placed to exercise dominion over God's creation, yet he failed because of his disobedience. Through Jesus Christ, however, the redeemed are not merely restored to what was lost. As fellow heirs with Christ, they will share in His eternal reign. Thus, God's grace does not simply remove the consequences of sin; it brings His people into a glory far greater than what was lost through Adam.

When we truly understand this glorious hope, our fear and despair begin to fade. We are no longer slaves to sin and death. Our future is secure because it rests on the finished work of Jesus Christ and the unchanging promises of God.

CONCLUSION:

This passage reveals that what Jesus Christ accomplished did more than save us from the reign of death. Through His abundant grace and the gift of justification, He has brought His people into a new standing where they will reign in life.

In Him, we have not only been forgiven and accepted by God. We have become His children, fellow heirs with Christ, and possess the assurance of an eternal and glorious future.

Therefore, let us live today with faith, hope, and thanksgiving. As we await the full revelation of Christ's kingdom, may we remain faithful to Him, for our victory rests not in ourselves but in His finished work.

Ultimately, this passage shows that the final word of history is not death but life. Through Jesus Christ, those who have received His abundant grace have not only been delivered from the reign of death but have also been called to share in His eternal reign. This is the supreme triumph of God's grace—a glory far greater than all that was lost through Adam's disobedience.
