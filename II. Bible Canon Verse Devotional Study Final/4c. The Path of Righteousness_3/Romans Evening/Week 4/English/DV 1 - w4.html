Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:15-17

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

15 But the free gift is not like the offense. For if by the one man’s offense many died, much more the grace of God and the gift by the grace of the one Man, Jesus Christ, abounded to many. 16 And the gift is not like that which came through the one who sinned. For the judgment which came from one offense resulted in condemnation, but the free gift which came from many offenses resulted in justification. 17 For if by the one man’s offense death reigned through the one, much more those who receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness will reign in life through the One, Jesus Christ.)

GUIDE REFLECTION:

One Man's Disobedience

In the previous verse, Paul said that Adam was a type or foreshadowing of the One who was to come. This does not mean that Adam was like Christ in his character or holiness. Their similarity lies in the far-reaching impact of what each one did. The action of one person can produce consequences that extend to many.

However, in this verse, Paul immediately made it clear that what they did is not equal.

“But the two are different, because the free gift of God is not like Adam’s sin.”

Adam’s sin was not merely a simple mistake or a momentary weakness. It was a deliberate act of disobedience against God’s clear command. Adam knew what God had forbidden, yet he still chose to act contrary to His word.

Through that act of disobedience, Adam rejected God's rightful authority to rule over his life. Instead of trusting in God's wisdom and goodness, he chose to follow his own desires.

Although Adam committed only one act, its consequences did not remain with him alone.

Paul said:

“Indeed, many died because of one man’s sin.”

Through Adam’s sin, sin entered the world, and with it came death. As the first man and the representative of humanity, the consequences of his disobedience extended to all his descendants. As a result, the entire human race experienced separation from God and the condemnation brought about by sin.

This reveals how serious sin truly is. It is not a minor failure that can simply be overlooked. Sin is rebellion against God, and its natural consequence is destruction and death.

Although Adam held the unique position of being the first man, this principle remains true: sin never remains without consequences. Every act of disobedience against God affects our own lives and often reaches into the lives of others as well.

Therefore, we should never take sin lightly or think that it is merely a personal matter. The sin we tolerate today may bring deeper destruction to our hearts, our families, and our relationship with God.

CONCLUSION:

This passage reminds us that sin should never be taken lightly. Adam’s disobedience brought death to many and revealed how severe the consequences of rebellion against God truly are.

Therefore, we must take our own sin seriously. We should neither conceal it nor ignore it, but acknowledge our need for God and come to Him in repentance.

The weight of this sin demonstrates why humanity needed a Savior who never sinned or disobeyed, but came to accomplish what Adam failed to do.

In the end, this passage shows that the sin of one man brought death to many. Therefore, we must never underestimate sin, but recognize the seriousness of its consequences and prepare our hearts for the magnificent grace that God has prepared.
