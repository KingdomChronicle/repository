Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:6-11

AMP (Amplified Bible)

Christ Died in Our Place

6 While we were still helpless [powerless to provide for our salvation], at the right time Christ died [as a substitute] for the ungodly. 7 Now it is an extraordinary thing for one to willingly give his life even for an upright man, though perhaps for a good man [one who is noble and selfless and worthy] someone might even dare to die. 8 But God clearly shows and proves His own love for us, by the fact that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. 9 Therefore, since we have now been justified [declared free of the guilt of sin] by His blood, [how much more certain is it that] we will be saved from the wrath of God through Him. 10 For if while we were enemies we were reconciled to God through the death of His Son, it is much more certain, having been reconciled, that we will be saved [from the consequences of sin] by His life [that is, we will be saved because Christ lives today]. 11 Not only that, but we also rejoice in God [rejoicing in His love and perfection] through our Lord Jesus Christ, through whom we have now received and enjoy our reconciliation [with God].

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this verse, Paul concludes his description of the blessings received by those who have been justified. In the previous verse, he showed that we have been reconciled to God through the death of Christ. Now, he explains why our salvation is absolutely certain.

“And because of this, we can be certain that we will be saved because Christ is alive.”

This part is incredibly important. Paul did not simply say that Christ died for our sins. He also declared that we can be certain of our salvation because Christ lives.

Why is His resurrection so important?

If Jesus had died but had not risen again, His death would have been no different from the death of any other person. We would have no assurance that He had truly conquered sin and death. Nor would we have any proof that God had fully accepted His sacrifice as the payment for our sins.

Paul says the same thing elsewhere in his letter:

if Christ has not been raised, our faith is futile, and we are still in our sins.

The resurrection proves that the cross was not a defeat but a complete victory.

But Christ did not remain in the tomb.

He rose again on the third day. Through His resurrection, God demonstrated that sin had been fully paid for and that Christ had triumphed over death. Death no longer has the final word. Life has prevailed.

But there is even more than that. Jesus did not rise again only to die someday once more. He lives forever.

This is the great difference.

Our Lord is alive today. He reigns at the right hand of the Father, continually intercedes for His children, and actively preserves all those whom He has saved. His work did not end at the cross; He continues it as our living Great High Priest and King.

This is why Paul says, "we shall certainly be saved." Our assurance is not based on our ability to remain faithful to God. It rests on the fact that our Savior is alive and will never die again. He is the One who continually holds us secure, so we can be confident that He will carry us safely to the very end.

“And not only that! We also rejoice in what God has done through our Lord Jesus Christ...”

Because of this, the believer's heart is filled with genuine joy.

We do not rejoice merely because we have been delivered from punishment. We rejoice because we have a living Lord. We are not following the memory of a great teacher or a hero from the past. We are following a living Savior who is with us every day, guiding us, strengthening us, and fulfilling all His promises.

Our joy is not simply the result of the blessings we have received. Above all, we rejoice in God Himself. Through Jesus Christ, we can now come before Him with confidence, worship Him with gladness, and live with a hope that will never disappoint.

CONCLUSION:

Paul beautifully concludes this section with an invitation to rejoice. Our faith does not rest on a Savior who remained in the tomb, but on a Lord who rose again and lives forever.

If Christ had not risen from the dead, we would have no assurance that He had conquered sin and death. We would have no living Mediator interceding for us, no King reigning over our lives, and no steadfast hope to sustain us until the end.

But because Christ lives, everything has changed. His resurrection proves that God has fully accepted His sacrifice. He now reigns, intercedes for His children, and will faithfully fulfill His promise to keep them secure until the end.

Therefore, our assurance does not rest on our own strength but on the power of the living Christ. Because of this, our hearts are filled with genuine joy. We rejoice not only because of the blessings we have received, but because our Lord is alive, is with us today, and will remain faithful forever.

In the end, the resurrection of Jesus Christ provides the unshakable foundation of our faith. Because our Savior lives, our salvation is certain, our hope is secure, and our joy in God will never fade. This is the glorious truth that gives strength to every believer until the day we are with Him forever.
