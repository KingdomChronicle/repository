Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:6-11

NLT (New Living Translation)

Christ Died in Our Place

6 For when we were still without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly. 7 For scarcely for a righteous man will one die; yet perhaps for a good man someone would even dare to die. 8 But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. 9 Much more then, having now been justified by His blood, we shall be saved from wrath through Him. 10 For if when we were enemies we were reconciled to God through the death of His Son, much more, having been reconciled, we shall be saved by His life. 11 And not only that, but we also rejoice in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, through whom we have now received the reconciliation.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this verse, Paul reveals one of the greatest blessings that resulted from the death of Jesus Christ—our reconciliation with God.

“Once, we were enemies of God...”

This was the true condition of every person before placing their faith in Christ. Because of sin, we were separated from God and opposed to His holy will. We were not merely people who had made mistakes; we were people who lived in rebellion against Him. Because God is holy and righteous, sin became the barrier that separated us from Him.

This does not mean that God failed to love us. Rather, it was our sin that placed us in a state of separation from Him and made us deserving of His righteous judgment. Nor did we have any ability to remove this barrier through our own goodness, religion, or good works.

But that was not the end of our story.

“...but He has now received us as His friends through the death of His Son.”

Here we see the amazing grace of God. While we were still far from Him, He Himself made a way to restore our relationship with Him. We were not the first to come to God seeking reconciliation; God took the first step because of His great love.

The word "received" refers to reconciliation. The relationship that sin had broken was restored through the death of Jesus Christ on the cross. Through His sacrifice, the barrier between us and God was removed. God's justice was fully satisfied, making it possible for Him to receive those who place their faith in Christ without compromising His holiness and righteousness.

It is important to understand that this reconciliation did not begin with our desire to come to God. It began in the heart of God. He gave His Son to accomplish what we could never do for ourselves. God Himself provided the way for those who were once His enemies to become His friends.

This is the heart of the gospel. God not only forgave our sins, but He also restored us to a close and peaceful relationship with Himself. The purpose of salvation is not merely to rescue us from the punishment of sin, but to restore us to fellowship with God, our Creator.

CONCLUSION:

The transformation God accomplished through Jesus Christ is immeasurable. Once, we were separated from Him because of our sin and had no ability to repair our broken relationship with Him. But through the death of His Son, God made a way for us to be reconciled to Himself.

This reconciliation is not the result of our own goodness or efforts. It is solely the result of God's grace and Christ's sacrifice on the cross. Because of this, we can now come before God with confidence—not as enemies, but as those whom He has graciously received.

Whenever we consider the greatness of our sin, let us remember that God's love is even greater. He loved us first, He took the first step, and He provided the way to restore our relationship with Him.

In the end, reconciliation with God is one of the greatest blessings of the gospel. It is far more than the forgiveness of sins; God has restored us to His presence so that we may know Him, walk with Him, and experience a relationship that will never be broken again because of the finished work of our Lord Jesus Christ.
