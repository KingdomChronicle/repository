Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:6-11

NLT (New Living Translation)

Christ Died in Our Place

6 For when we were still without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly. 7 For scarcely for a righteous man will one die; yet perhaps for a good man someone would even dare to die. 8 But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. 9 Much more then, having now been justified by His blood, we shall be saved from wrath through Him. 10 For if when we were enemies we were reconciled to God through the death of His Son, much more, having been reconciled, we shall be saved by His life. 11 And not only that, but we also rejoice in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, through whom we have now received the reconciliation.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

After showing that the believer's hope will never disappoint because of God's love, Paul now presents the greatest evidence of that love.

“For when we were still without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly.”

Notice the condition of humanity before God's salvation came.

“When we were still without strength...”

The word “without strength” does not merely refer to physical weakness. It describes our spiritual condition. We were completely unable to save ourselves. We could not remove our sin, change our hearts, or become righteous before God through our own efforts.

Simply put, we were utterly in need of a Savior.

Yet what is remarkable is that God did not wait for us to become good before He loved us.

“...Christ died... for the ungodly.”

Jesus did not die for those who deserved it. He died for sinners. While we were separated from God, unable to come to Him, and possessing nothing worthy of praise, God Himself took the initiative to save us.

This is what sets God's grace apart from the ways of mankind. People usually reward those who deserve it. But God gave salvation to those who did not deserve it.

Paul also points out that all of this happened “in due time.”

Christ's death was not an accident or something that happened by chance. It was part of God's perfect plan. Jesus came at the exact time appointed by the Father to fully accomplish His plan of salvation.

Here we see two attributes of God—His love and His wisdom. He knew the perfect time and the perfect way to save humanity.

Therefore, our salvation is not the result of our seeking God. It is the result of God seeking us first.

CONCLUSION:

This passage reminds us that God's love is not based on our worthiness. He loved us while we were weak, sinful, and completely unable to save ourselves.

This is the beauty of the gospel. God did not say, "Change first, and then I will love you." Instead, He demonstrated His love by sending Jesus Christ to die for us while we were still in our sinful condition.

Whenever we think we must first become good enough before God can accept us, let us remember the cross. The cross proves that God's love came before our transformation. Because He loved us, He gave us the opportunity to be transformed.

Therefore, let us not place our confidence in our own strength or goodness. Our hope rests entirely on what Christ has done for us. And if God did not abandon us when we were at our weakest, we can be even more certain that He will never forsake us now that we are His children.

In the end, the cross of Christ is the greatest proof that God's love is not merely spoken—it is demonstrated. And the God who gave His Son for us while we were still sinners will remain faithful to love us, guide us, and keep us until the very end.
