Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:6-11

NLT (New Living Translation)

Christ Died in Our Place

6 For when we were still without strength, in due time Christ died for the ungodly. 7 For scarcely for a righteous man will one die; yet perhaps for a good man someone would even dare to die. 8 But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us. 9 Much more then, having now been justified by His blood, we shall be saved from wrath through Him. 10 For if when we were enemies we were reconciled to God through the death of His Son, much more, having been reconciled, we shall be saved by His life. 11 And not only that, but we also rejoice in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, through whom we have now received the reconciliation.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

In this verse, Paul continues to affirm the certainty of the salvation that belongs to those who believe in Jesus Christ.

“Much more then, having now been justified by His blood...”

Our justification is not the result of our own goodness or obedience to the Law. It is the result of Christ's blood, which was shed on the cross. His death became the payment for our sins so that we might be declared righteous before God.

The word “now” is especially significant.

It shows that justification is not something we are still waiting to receive. It is a present reality already granted to every true believer in Christ.

We do not gradually become acceptable to God. The moment a person genuinely repents of his sin and places his faith in Jesus Christ, God declares that person righteous because of Christ's finished work on the cross.

It is important to understand that Paul is not referring to a faith that exists only in words. Many claim to believe in Christ, yet they continue living as they always have, with no repentance and no evidence of transformation. Such faith is not saving faith.

True faith is more than intellectual agreement or verbally confessing that Jesus is Lord. It is a heart that fully trusts in Christ, repents of sin, and is willing to follow Him. This does not mean that a believer instantly becomes perfect, but it does mean that a new direction begins in that person's life. He gradually turns away from his former way of living and grows in obedience to God through the power of the Holy Spirit.

Therefore, when we say that a person has been "justified now," we are referring to someone who possesses genuine faith—a faith that bears fruit. The fruits of transformation are not the reason that person is saved; they are the evidence that his faith is living and genuine. Justification remains God's gracious gift received through faith alone in Christ, yet that faith never remains without producing an effect in the life of the one who has truly received Jesus.

“...we shall be saved from wrath through Him.”

The wrath of God refers to His holy and righteous judgment against sin. Because God is holy, He cannot simply overlook sin. It must be dealt with.

But the good news is this: Jesus Christ has already borne that judgment in our place.

That is why Paul says that if God has already accomplished the greater work—giving His Son so that we might be justified—we can be even more certain that He will save us from His wrath.

This does not imply that there is still uncertainty about our salvation. Rather, Paul is strengthening our assurance. If the greatest aspect of our salvation has already been accomplished through the blood of Christ, then we can be certain that God will fulfill every one of His promises to the very end.

Our assurance does not rest on the strength of our faith but on the sufficiency of Christ's sacrifice. The blood of Jesus is completely sufficient to pay for our sin, and therefore His salvation is completely trustworthy.

CONCLUSION:

This passage brings great comfort to everyone who has truly believed. We do not live in fear that one day God might condemn us again because of our sins. The judgment that we deserved has already been borne by Christ on the cross.

Whenever we struggle with doubts about our salvation, we should not look to our own ability or performance. Instead, we should look to the blood of Jesus Christ. Our hope does not rest on our faithfulness but on His finished work.

If God gave His own Son to justify us while we were still sinners, how much more can we be certain that He will keep those whom He has already redeemed? God never begins a work that He does not finish.

In the end, the blood of Christ has not only secured our forgiveness; it has also given us assurance. Therefore, we can live with peace and confidence, knowing that the God who has justified us is the same God who will save us to the very end.
