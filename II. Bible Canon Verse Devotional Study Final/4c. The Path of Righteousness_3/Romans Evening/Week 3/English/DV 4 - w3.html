Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:12-14

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

12 Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned— 13 (For until the law sin was in the world, but sin is not imputed when there is no law. 14 Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Sin Existed Before the Law

After showing that sin entered the world through Adam and spread to all people, Paul goes on to clarify an important truth:

"For until the law sin was in the world..."

Some might ask, "If the Law of Moses had not yet been given, how could people be considered sinners?"

Paul's answer is clear.

Sin already existed before God gave the Law at Mount Sinai. From the time Adam and Eve sinned, sin continued to exist throughout humanity. Even before Moses came, people lied, murdered, deceived others, worshiped idols, and committed every kind of evil.

In other words, sin did not begin when the Law was given. It was already present.

But Paul also says:

"...but sin is not imputed when there is no law."

This does not mean that people were without sin before the Law was given. Rather, Paul means that there was not yet a written law that clearly set forth God's commands and identified specific violations against them.

When God gave the Law through Moses, His standard of righteousness became clearly revealed. The Law identified sin by name and showed what it truly means to disobey God's holy will.

Simply put, the Law did not create sin. It merely exposed the sin that had already existed.

The Law is like a mirror. It does not cleanse a person who is dirty, but it reveals where the dirt is. In the same way, the Law did not make people sinners; it simply revealed their true condition before God.

Paul is showing that humanity's problem did not begin with the coming of the Law. Even before it was given, sin already existed and was actively at work in human life. The Law came not to create sin but to reveal more clearly the depth of the problem that had long existed within humanity.

CONCLUSION:

This verse reminds us that humanity's problem is not a lack of rules. The real problem is the sin that dwells in the human heart.

We may know the difference between right and wrong, but knowledge alone is not enough to change our sinful nature. God's commands are important because they reveal His holy standard and expose the areas of our lives that need His transforming work.

As we read God's Word, we not only learn what is right and wrong, but we also come to see our true condition before Him. This should lead us to humility, repentance, and a deeper desire to live according to His will.

Ultimately, this verse reminds us that sin existed even before the Law was given. The Law did not create sin; it simply exposed humanity's true condition. Therefore, rather than trusting in our own ability to obey God's commands, we should humble ourselves before Him and continually seek His mercy, wisdom, and guidance in our daily walk.
