Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:12-14

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

12 Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned— 13 (For until the law sin was in the world, but sin is not imputed when there is no law. 14 Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Death Entered Through Sin

After explaining that sin entered the world through one man, Paul now reveals its consequence.

"...and death through sin."

When God created Adam and Eve, death was not part of His original creation. Humanity was created with life and in close fellowship with God, who is the source of life. God gave them one command along with a clear warning:

"For in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die." (Genesis 2:17)

But when Adam and Eve disobeyed God, it was not only sin that entered the human experience. Death entered as well.

This death has several aspects.

First, there is spiritual death. The very moment they sinned, their close fellowship with God was broken. They hid from His presence, felt shame and fear, and were driven out of the Garden of Eden. This demonstrated that sin separates people from God.

Second, there is physical death. Although Adam and Eve did not die physically on the very day they sinned, they became mortal. From that time on, every human being has experienced aging, sickness, and eventually death.

Paul shows that death was not part of God's original design for humanity. It is the result of sin. Because mankind became separated from God, the source of life, death became part of the experience of the entire human race.

Therefore, sin is not merely a mistake with temporary consequences. It carries a devastating result. Every act of disobedience distances people from God, and this separation is the root of death.

CONCLUSION:

This verse reminds us that sin is never a trivial matter. It carries real and serious consequences. Death—both spiritual and physical—entered the human experience because of disobedience to God.

When we understand this truth, we become more careful in the way we live. Sin may appear attractive at first, but it always leads to the destruction of our relationship with God and ultimately brings ruin.

It also reminds us to remain humble. Death is a constant reminder that every person is affected by the consequences of sin and that no one can truthfully say they do not need God.

Ultimately, the truth that death entered through sin reminds us how seriously God views sin. The more we understand the weight of its consequences, the more we will learn to turn away from sin and live in reverent fear of God, who alone is the true source of life.
