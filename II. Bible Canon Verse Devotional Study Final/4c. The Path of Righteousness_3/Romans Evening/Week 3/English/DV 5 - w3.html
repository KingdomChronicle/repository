Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:12-14

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

12 Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned— 13 (For until the law sin was in the world, but sin is not imputed when there is no law. 14 Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Death Reigned from Adam to Moses

In this verse, Paul continues explaining the far-reaching effects of Adam's sin.

“Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses...”

In the previous verse, Paul explained that sin was already in the world before the Law was given. Now he presents the evidence for that truth.

If there had been no sin before the Law, why did the people who lived from Adam to Moses die?

The answer is clear. Even though the written Law had not yet been given, death still reigned.

The word “reigned” paints the picture of a king exercising authority and dominion. For many generations, from Adam to Moses, death seemed to rule over humanity. No one escaped its power.

Why?

Because the root of humanity's problem was not merely the violation of a written law. The entire human race was affected by Adam's sin. As a result, every person inherited a sinful nature and commits sin.

That is why Paul says:

“...even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam.”

Adam's sin was unique. He received a direct command from God and deliberately disobeyed it. In contrast, those who lived before the Law was given through Moses did not have the same written command to violate.

Yet even though they did not sin in the same manner as Adam, they still died.

This is clear evidence that sin is more than simply the wrong things we do. It is a condition that has affected the entire human race ever since Adam sinned.

We can see this throughout the biblical record. Even before the Law was given to Moses, sin had already spread through the lives of Cain, Lamech, the people in Noah's day, and those who built the Tower of Babel. Sin continued to rule over humanity, and with it came death.

This shows that the Law was not the cause of death. The Law merely exposed sin. Death is the result of the sin that entered humanity through Adam.

CONCLUSION:

The reign of death from Adam to Moses proves that humanity's greatest problem is not the absence of laws but the sin that dwells within our nature.

No one can overcome the consequences of sin by their own strength. Every person, no matter how good, intelligent, or powerful, lives under the power of death because all have been affected by the sin that entered the human race.

This truth should teach us humility. No one can claim to be righteous enough or capable enough to escape the consequences of sin by their own efforts. Instead, we should live in reverent fear of God and continually acknowledge our need for His mercy and grace.

Ultimately, the reign of death from Adam to Moses is clear evidence that sin existed long before the Law was given. It shows that humanity's problem is not merely the breaking of God's commands but the sinful condition that has affected the entire human race. As we recognize this truth, we become more humble before God and more deeply aware of our need for His mercy and grace.
