Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:12-14

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

12 Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned— 13 (For until the law sin was in the world, but sin is not imputed when there is no law. 14 Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Death Spread to All Humanity

In the final part of this verse, Paul reveals the consequence of Adam's sin for the entire human race.

“Thus death spread to all people, because all sinned.”

Adam's sin did not affect him alone. As the representative of humanity, his disobedience brought a sinful nature upon all his descendants. From that moment on, every person born into this world has been born with an inherent inclination toward sin.

This is why death has become the experience of every human being.

Death does not discriminate between the rich and the poor, the young and the old, the powerful and the ordinary. No one escapes it. This is clear evidence that the entire human race has been affected by sin.

Paul says:

“...because all sinned.”

This does not refer only to the fact that every person personally commits sin. It also teaches that the whole human race was affected by Adam's sin, so that everyone possesses a sinful nature. As a result, every person sins and experiences death.

This is also why Paul declared in the earlier chapters that "There is none righteous, no, not one," and "all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." (Romans 3:10, 23)

In light of this truth, there is no room for human pride. None of us is better than another when it comes to our standing before God. We all began in the same condition—sinners in need of His mercy and grace.

Paul's purpose is to show that the problem of sin is not merely the problem of a few individuals but of the entire human race. Therefore, no one can save themselves or boast of their own righteousness before God.

CONCLUSION:

The truth that “all have sinned” reminds us that no one can save themselves. We all stand equally before God as sinners in need of His mercy and grace.

When we understand this truth, our pride is stripped away, and we learn to walk in humility. We cannot claim to be more righteous or more deserving than anyone else, because we have all been affected by sin and are all in need of God.

It also reminds us not to trust in our own ability or goodness. Our greatest need is not merely a change in behavior but the transforming work of God in our lives. Therefore, we should come before Him each day with repentant hearts, ready to obey Him, and fully dependent on His grace.

Ultimately, the truth that death spread to all humanity because all sinned reminds us that no one can claim to be righteous by their own merit. We all stand equally before God as sinners in need of His mercy. When we recognize our true condition, we learn to live with humility, trust in God, and gratitude for His everlasting grace.
