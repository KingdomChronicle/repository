Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:12-14

NKJV (New King James Version)

Death in Adam, Life in Christ

12 Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned— 13 (For until the law sin was in the world, but sin is not imputed when there is no law. 14 Nevertheless death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

Sin Entered the World

In this passage, Paul begins a new comparison that becomes the central theme of the verses that follow. In the previous chapters, he explained how we are justified through faith in Christ. Now he explains why humanity needs a Savior.

He begins with this important truth:

“Sin entered the world through one man.”

The man Paul is referring to is Adam, the first man created by God. God created him holy, without sin, and in close fellowship with his Creator. He placed Adam in the Garden of Eden and gave him the freedom to enjoy everything there except for one command—not to eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

But Adam, together with Eve, chose to disobey God.

At that very moment, sin entered the human experience.

This does not mean that sin came into existence only then. Even before Adam and Eve sinned, Satan had already rebelled against God. What Paul is referring to here is the entrance of sin into humanity. Through Adam's disobedience, sin became part of the condition of every human being.

This is why no one has to teach people how to sin. From childhood, selfishness, lying, anger, and other expressions of our sinful nature naturally appear. Sin is not merely something people do—it has become part of our very nature as descendants of Adam.

This is what Paul wants to emphasize:

Humanity's problem goes deeper than sinful actions. The root of the problem is the sinful nature inherited by the entire human race.

And because sin entered through one man, all humanity has been affected by it.

But the story does not end there. This is why, in the following verses, Paul introduces the Second Adam—Jesus Christ—who came to accomplish what Adam failed to do. If Adam brought sin into humanity, Christ brought grace, righteousness, and life to all who believe in Him.

CONCLUSION:

Sin is not merely a mistake or a bad habit. It is a deep-rooted problem that began with Adam's disobedience and affected the entire human race. Therefore, humanity's greatest problem is not environment, education, or circumstances in life, but the sin that dwells within the human heart.

When we understand the depth of our condition, we also understand why our own goodness is never enough to make us righteous before God. No one can remove the sin rooted in their nature through personal effort or good works alone.

This truth also reminds us to remain humble. We all began in the same condition—sinners in need of God's mercy and grace. Rather than trusting in our own righteousness, we should come before God with repentant hearts, willing to follow and obey Him.

Ultimately, the truth that sin entered the world through one man reminds us that no one can claim to be unaffected by sin. We all need God. When we recognize our true condition, we become more humble before Him and learn to depend not on our own ability but on His mercy and grace.
