Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:1-5

NLT (New Living Translation)

Faith Brings Joy

5Therefore, since we have been made right in God’s sight by faith, we have peace with God because of what Jesus Christ our Lord has done for us. 2 Because of our faith, Christ has brought us into this place of undeserved privilege where we now stand, and we confidently and joyfully look forward to sharing God’s glory. 3 We can rejoice, too, when we run into problems and trials, for we know that they help us develop endurance. 4 And endurance develops strength of character, and character strengthens our confident hope of salvation. 5 And this hope will not lead to disappointment. For we know how dearly God loves us, because he has given us the Holy Spirit to fill our hearts with his love.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Blessings of Justification: New Life Through Christ

Peace and Hope Through Justification

After Paul presented the peace, grace, and hope enjoyed by those who have been justified, he made a surprising statement:

“Not only that, but we also rejoice in the sufferings we endure…”

At first glance, this may seem difficult to understand. How can suffering be a reason for joy? Paul is not saying that suffering itself is pleasant or something we should seek. Neither is he teaching that a believer will no longer experience pain or sorrow when facing trials.

Instead, he teaches that the suffering of a believer has a purpose.

Before we placed our faith in Christ, our hardships often seemed directionless and uncertain. But now, as children of God, no suffering is ever meaningless. God uses every trial to accomplish good in our lives.

“...because we know that suffering produces endurance.”

Endurance is not merely the ability to tolerate pain or hardship. It is continuing to trust God even when the waiting is long and the journey is difficult. It is remaining steadfast in faith even before we see God's answer.

Just as an athlete grows stronger through rigorous training, so the faith of a child of God is strengthened. Our trust in God does not deepen only during seasons of comfort. More often, it is shaped in the midst of tears, waiting, and trials.

Therefore, we do not rejoice because of the pain itself, but because we know that God is at work even in the midst of our suffering. No trial is ever wasted in God's hands. He uses each one to shape our character and deepen our faith.

James also declared this truth:

"Dear brothers and sisters, consider it pure joy whenever you face trials of many kinds, because you know that the testing of your faith produces endurance." (James 1:2–3)

CONCLUSION:

One of the greatest blessings of justification is the assurance that our sufferings are never meaningless. As believers, we know that God has a purpose for every trial He allows in our lives.

We may not immediately understand why there are seasons of pain, disappointment, or severe testing. Yet we can trust that God is not concerned only with our temporary comfort. Above all, He desires to shape our character so that we become more like Christ.

So instead of losing hope when trials come, let us learn to view them from God's perspective. Every hardship can become an opportunity to know His faithfulness more deeply, experience His grace, and grow stronger in our faith.

In the end, trials are not a sign that God has abandoned us. More often, they are one of the ways He prepares us for a greater purpose. And as we continue to trust in Him, our sufferings will produce endurance—a steadfast faith, not because life is easy, but because the God in whom we place our trust is great and faithful.
