Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:1-5

NLT (New Living Translation)

Faith Brings Joy

5Therefore, since we have been made right in God’s sight by faith, we have peace with God because of what Jesus Christ our Lord has done for us. 2 Because of our faith, Christ has brought us into this place of undeserved privilege where we now stand, and we confidently and joyfully look forward to sharing God’s glory. 3 We can rejoice, too, when we run into problems and trials, for we know that they help us develop endurance. 4 And endurance develops strength of character, and character strengthens our confident hope of salvation. 5 And this hope will not lead to disappointment. For we know how dearly God loves us, because he has given us the Holy Spirit to fill our hearts with his love.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

New Life Through Christ

Peace and Hope Through Justification

In the first four chapters of Romans, Paul clearly demonstrated that no one can be made righteous before God through personal effort or by keeping the Law. All have sinned and are in need of God's grace. But as Romans 5 begins, the focus of his message shifts. From explaining how we are justified, he now presents the blessings that result from being justified.

“Therefore, since we have been justified through faith...”

Notice the phrase “have been justified.” This shows that justification is not a process that is gradually achieved. It is a completed work of God for those who have placed their faith in Jesus Christ. The moment we believed in Christ, we were declared righteous before Him—not because of our own goodness, but because of the finished work of Jesus on the cross.

The first blessing of justification is this:

“...we have peace with God...”

The peace Paul speaks of is not merely a feeling of calmness in the heart. It is much more than that. It means that the relationship once broken by sin has now been restored. We were once separated from God because of our sins, but now, through Jesus Christ, we have been accepted as His children.

This peace does not depend on our emotions. There may be days when we feel fear, anxiety, or worry. Yet even when our emotions change, the reality that we have peace with God remains unchanged, because this peace is founded on what Christ has done, not on how we feel.

It is also important to understand that this promise is only for those who truly believe in Christ. Paul is not saying that the whole world automatically has peace with God. This peace is the result of genuine faith in Jesus as Lord and Savior.

Neither is it for those who are merely satisfied with outward or superficial religious observance while their hearts remain far from God. True faith is not found merely in words or profession, but is evidenced by a life that follows Christ.

We are not saved because of our obedience, but genuine saving faith always produces sincere obedience. Therefore, those who truly have peace with God are those who have genuinely believed, and whose faith is demonstrated by a life that continually submits to and follows the Lord.

And because God Himself established this peace through Christ, it is a permanent peace. No one can undo what God has accomplished for those who truly believe in Him.

CONCLUSION:

Romans 5 begins with a wonderful reminder that salvation is not merely deliverance from the penalty of sin. It also brings us into a new and peaceful relationship with God.

Many people seek peace through wealth, success, or favorable circumstances. But true and lasting peace begins only when our relationship with God has been restored through Jesus Christ.

Therefore, whenever we face trials, we do not have to question whether God still accepts us. Our justification does not depend on our performance but on the finished work of Christ. Because of this, we can approach God with confidence—not as enemies, but as His children.

In the end, the greatest blessing of justification is not merely the assurance of heaven, but the assurance that we already have peace with God now and forever.

And this peace will remain because it was established not by man, but by our Lord Jesus Christ.
