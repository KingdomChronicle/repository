Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:1-5

NLT (New Living Translation)

Faith Brings Joy

5Therefore, since we have been made right in God’s sight by faith, we have peace with God because of what Jesus Christ our Lord has done for us. 2 Because of our faith, Christ has brought us into this place of undeserved privilege where we now stand, and we confidently and joyfully look forward to sharing God’s glory. 3 We can rejoice, too, when we run into problems and trials, for we know that they help us develop endurance. 4 And endurance develops strength of character, and character strengthens our confident hope of salvation. 5 And this hope will not lead to disappointment. For we know how dearly God loves us, because he has given us the Holy Spirit to fill our hearts with his love.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Blessings of New Life Through Christ

Peace and Hope Through Justification

After Paul declared that we now have peace with God, he goes on to reveal another great blessing of justification:

“Through faith in Jesus Christ, we have gained access to God's grace.”

The word “grace” refers to God's unmerited favor—a blessing that we could never earn or pay for. It is God's favor freely given to those who do not deserve it.

If justice alone were served, what we deserved because of our sins was condemnation. But because of Jesus Christ, what we have received is not judgment but grace. It was not God's wrath that welcomed us, but His gracious favor.

Notice also that Paul says we “have gained access” to God's grace. This implies that God's grace is not merely something we received at the moment we first believed. It is the very position in which every believer now stands. We live every day under the grace of God.

This is a profoundly important truth. As believers, we do not live continually trying to earn God's acceptance. Rather, we live from the acceptance He has already given us through Christ.

This does not mean that we no longer have shortcomings. We still fail and continue to struggle against sin. But our relationship with God is not founded on our perfect performance; it is founded on the grace He has given us through Jesus.

Faith is what opened the way for us to enter into this grace. Not our good works, not our religious efforts, but our trust in Christ.

Because of this, we can come before God with confidence—not as people who fear that He might reject us, but as children who have already been accepted because of His everlasting grace.

CONCLUSION:

One of the greatest blessings of justification is the assurance that we now live under the grace of God. We no longer have to strive to earn His love, because He has already freely given it to us through Jesus Christ. Our acceptance before God does not depend on our own goodness but on the finished work of Christ on the cross.

However, it is vitally important to understand what God's grace truly means. Grace is not permission to continue living in sin. It is not a license to say, "I can keep on sinning because God will forgive me anyway." Such thinking does not reflect genuine faith, but a misunderstanding of God's grace.

True grace does not merely forgive the sinner; it also transforms the sinner's heart. The same grace that saved us also teaches us to turn away from ungodliness and to live holy lives. When we truly experience God's goodness and love, we are not motivated to abuse His grace. Instead, we are moved all the more to love Him, obey Him, and turn away from anything that grieves His heart.

This does not mean that we will become perfect in this life. We will still have weaknesses and moments when we stumble. But there is a great difference between a believer who stumbles while fighting against sin and repents, and a person who deliberately lives in sin while using grace as an excuse to continue doing so. The former comes to God for forgiveness and transformation; the latter uses God to justify disobedience and sinful living.

Therefore, let us not waste this great privilege. Let us live with humility, gratitude, and confidence, knowing that the grace that accepted us is the same grace that will strengthen us, transform us, and keep us until the very end.

In the end, the Christian life is not a life of striving to obtain God's grace, but a life of continually walking in the grace we have already received through faith in Jesus Christ. And as we continue to walk in this grace, God gradually transforms us to become more like His Son. This is the true fruit of grace—not the freedom to sin, but the freedom to live for Him.
