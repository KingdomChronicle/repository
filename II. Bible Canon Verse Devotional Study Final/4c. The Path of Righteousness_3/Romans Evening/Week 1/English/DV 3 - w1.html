Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:1-5

NLT (New Living Translation)

Faith Brings Joy

5Therefore, since we have been made right in God’s sight by faith, we have peace with God because of what Jesus Christ our Lord has done for us. 2 Because of our faith, Christ has brought us into this place of undeserved privilege where we now stand, and we confidently and joyfully look forward to sharing God’s glory. 3 We can rejoice, too, when we run into problems and trials, for we know that they help us develop endurance. 4 And endurance develops strength of character, and character strengthens our confident hope of salvation. 5 And this hope will not lead to disappointment. For we know how dearly God loves us, because he has given us the Holy Spirit to fill our hearts with his love.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Blessings of Justification: New Life Through Christ

Peace and Hope Through Justification

After Paul declared that we have peace with God and stand in His grace, he then presents another great blessing of justification:

“…and we rejoice in the hope of sharing the glory of God.”

The word “hope” used by Paul does not refer to something that may or may not happen. In Scripture, hope is a confident expectation. It is trusting in a promise that God will certainly fulfill, even though we have not yet seen it.

Therefore, the joy Paul speaks of is not based on our present circumstances. It is the result of the certainty that every believer's future is secure in the hands of God.

What is it that we are looking forward to?

“...that we will share in His glory.”

Earlier, Paul declared that all people have “fallen short of the glory of God” (Romans 3:23). Because of sin, we lost the privilege of sharing in His glory. But now, because we have been justified in Christ, what was once lost has been promised to us again.

The glory of God refers to His presence, holiness, majesty, and everything He has prepared for His children. The day will come when everyone who believes in Christ will be with Him forever and will share in His eternal glory.

This is why a Christian rejoices. Not because life is free from problems, but because he knows that his future is secure in Christ.

This joy is not founded on success, wealth, health, or comfort. These things may pass away. But God's promise will never fail.

Therefore, even as we go through trials, our hope remains steadfast, because our future does not depend on changing circumstances but on the faithfulness of the God who made the promise.

CONCLUSION:

One of the greatest blessings of justification is the possession of a certain and confident hope. As believers, we do not live filled with fear or uncertainty about our future. We live with joy because we know that the very best is yet to come.

In this world, we may experience suffering, disappointments, and many unexpected circumstances. Yet none of these can take away the hope God has given us. Our joy is not rooted in the temporary things of this world, but in the eternal promise that we will share in the glory of God.

Therefore, let us not allow our problems to steal our joy. Instead, let us continue to fix our eyes on God's promises and live with confidence and eager anticipation for the day when we will fully experience His presence.

In the end, the Christian's hope is not merely a wish that something good might happen. It is the unwavering assurance that God will fulfill what He has promised. Because of this, we can rejoice even now, for our eternal future in Christ is certain and will never disappoint.
