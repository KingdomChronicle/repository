Daily Devotional Verses



Romans 5:1-5

NLT (New Living Translation)

Faith Brings Joy

5Therefore, since we have been made right in God’s sight by faith, we have peace with God because of what Jesus Christ our Lord has done for us. 2 Because of our faith, Christ has brought us into this place of undeserved privilege where we now stand, and we confidently and joyfully look forward to sharing God’s glory. 3 We can rejoice, too, when we run into problems and trials, for we know that they help us develop endurance. 4 And endurance develops strength of character, and character strengthens our confident hope of salvation. 5 And this hope will not lead to disappointment. For we know how dearly God loves us, because he has given us the Holy Spirit to fill our hearts with his love.

GUIDE REFLECTION:

The Blessings of Justification: New Life Through Christ

Peace and Hope Through Justification

In this passage, Paul concludes his beautiful description of the blessings that flow from justification. From peace with God, acceptance through His grace, joy in hope, and growth through trials, he now reveals the reason why our hope will never disappoint.

“And this hope will not lead to disappointment...”

The hope that God gives is not like human hope, which is often filled with uncertainty and doubt. We frequently say, “I hope it happens,” or “I hope everything turns out well.” But the hope Paul speaks of is a firm assurance. It is a hope founded on the God who never lies and never fails to fulfill His promises.

Why can we be certain that we will never be disappointed?

“...because God has poured out His love into our hearts...”

God has not merely demonstrated His love through words. We have already experienced His love in our lives. God Himself has placed the assurance of His love within our hearts.

The word “poured out” speaks of abundance. God's love is neither scarce nor temporary. It has been lavishly given to every believer. This love gives us the strength to continue even when we are walking through difficult times.

“And this is through the Holy Spirit, who has been given to us.”

What a wonderful truth this is. God has not left us to journey through life alone. He has given the Holy Spirit to dwell in every person who believes in Christ.

The Holy Spirit continually reminds us of God's love, gives us peace in the midst of turmoil, strengthens us when we are weak, and reinforces our hope as we go through trials.

Because of this, our hope is not based on our own strength, emotions, or circumstances. It rests on the God who loves us and continues to work in our lives through His Spirit.

CONCLUSION:

Paul beautifully concludes this section with a profound assurance:

the hope of a believer will never end in disappointment.

Not because our faith is strong, but because the God in whom we place our trust is faithful.

In life, we may be disappointed by people, by our plans, or even by our own abilities. But no one who places his hope in God will ever be disappointed. His promises are certain because they flow from His everlasting love.

And the greatest assurance is this: God did not simply love us from a distance. He gave us the Holy Spirit to dwell within us and to let us experience His presence, His love, and His guidance every day. Therefore, in every trial there is strength; in every tear there is comfort; and in every season of waiting there is a hope that will never fade.

In the end, the life of a believer is not lived in fear but in assurance. For the God who saved us is the same God who continues to love us, guide us, and fulfill everything He has promised. Therefore, our hope in Christ is not only for today, but a certainty that extends into eternity.
