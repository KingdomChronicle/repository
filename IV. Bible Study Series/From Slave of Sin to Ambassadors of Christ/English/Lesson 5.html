“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Series Title:
From Slave of Sin to Ambassadors of Christ


Lesson 5: The Honor of Being God’s Co-Worker


Introduction

From slavery to sin, God has called us not only to be saved but also to take part in His divine work here on earth. He has entrusted us with the mission of spreading the Good News and building His Kingdom. Thus, we are not merely followers, but partners with Him in bringing salvation to many. To be a co-worker with God is a great privilege and a sign of the Father’s trust in His children.

Teaching Points

  1. The Meaning of “God’s Co-Worker”

    • The Apostle Paul said:
      “I planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the growth. So neither he who plants nor he who waters is anything, but only God who gives the growth. The one who plants and the one who waters have one purpose, and they will each be rewarded according to their own labor. For we are God’s fellow workers; you are God’s field, God’s building.” (1 Corinthians 3:6–9)


  2. Characteristics of a Co-Worker of God

    • Humble – Recognizes that the results come from God, not from human effort.

    • Faithful and Obedient – Works not for self-glory, but for the glory of God.

    • Diligent and Patient – Does not give up in hardship, knowing that labor in the Lord is never in vain.


  3. Privileges of Being God’s Co-Worker

    • Trusted by God – He entrusts us with a share in His plan of salvation.

    • Partnership with God – Our labor is not done alone; it comes with His presence and strength.

    • Sharing in the Reward – As Paul declared:

      “Therefore, my beloved brothers, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that your labor in the Lord is not in vain.” (1 Corinthians 15:58)


  4. Responsibilities of God’s Co-Worker

    • To faithfully plant and water, even when the results are not immediately visible.

    • To work in unity with fellow laborers, for the mission is one.

    • To remain faithful until the end, for every good work will be rewarded.


Scriptural Basis

  • 1 Corinthians 15:58 - “Therefore, my beloved brothers, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that your labor in the Lord is not in vain.”

  • John 15:16 - “You did not choose me, but I chose you and appointed you so that you might go and bear fruit—fruit that will last…”

  • 1 Corinthians 3:6–9 - 6 “I planted the seed, Apollos watered it, but God has been making it grow. 7 So neither the one who plants nor the one who waters is anything, but only God, who makes things grow. 8 The one who plants and the one who waters have one purpose, and they will each be rewarded according to their own labor. 9 For we are co-workers in God’s service; you are God’s field, God’s building.”

Discussion Questions

  1. What did Paul mean when he said, “I planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the growth”?

  2. How can you show your faithfulness as a co-worker in your church or community?

  3. What is the privilege of working with God in His mission?

  4. Why is it important to remain diligent in the Lord’s work even during trials?

Application / Challenge

  1. Personal Reflection:Identify the areas of your life where God is calling you to be more active as His co-worker. Are you planting, watering, or doing both?

  2. Faith Response:Pray daily for a humble and faithful heart, one that relies not on your own strength but on God who brings growth.

  3. Action Step:Take one concrete step this week as a co-worker of God—whether sharing the Good News, teaching or encouraging fellow believers, or faithfully serving in your church or community.

Conclusion

Being a co-worker of God is a mark of the Father’s great trust. Each of us has a role—some plant, others water—but it is only God who brings the growth. Therefore, let us not grow weary in our labor; instead, let us continue with faithfulness, humility, and zeal, knowing that our work is never in vain in the Lord.

A true friend of God is not only a listener but also a bearer of fruit—faithful as a co-worker in His Kingdom.
