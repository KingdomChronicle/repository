“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Series Title:
From Slave of Sin to Ambassadors of Christ


Lesson 4: The Honor of Being a Friend of God


Introduction

Many know God as the Almighty, the Creator, and the King of all. Yet, a greater truth is this: God desires a personal relationship with us. From being servants and children, He invites us into a higher level of intimacy—to become His friends. Not every child experiences this depth of closeness or open communication. But God calls us to elevate our walk with Him, moving from being children to becoming true friends—a relationship marked by trust, obedience, and constant communication.

Teaching Points

  1. Examples of God’s Friends

    • Abraham – Called God’s friend because of his faith and obedience (James 2:23).

    • Moses – Spoke with God face-to-face, as a friend speaks with a friend (Exodus 33:11).

    • The Disciples of Jesus – Raised from being servants to friends (John 15:15).


  2. Qualities of a Friend of God

    • Trust and faith in Him.

    • Constant communication—an intimate relationship through prayer and listening.

    • Obedience: “You are My friends if you do what I command you” (John 15:14).

    • Entrusted with God’s secrets and plans.


  3. Privileges of a Friend of God

    • Closeness – The privilege of approaching God without barriers.

    • Favor and protection – As shown in the lives of Abraham and Moses.

    • Partnership – Included in His mission and purpose.
    • Unfailing love – A love the world cannot break.


  4. Responsibilities of a Friend of God

    • Maintain faith and trust even in trials.

    • Live in obedience and holiness.

    • Bear witness to His love faithfully to others.


Scriptural Basis

  • John 15:14–15 – “You are My friends if you do what I command… I have called you friends.”

  • James 2:23 – “And he was called a friend of God.”

  • Exodus 33:11 – “The Lord spoke to Moses face to face, as one speaks to a friend.”

  • 1 John 3:1 – “See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God.”

Discussion Questions

  1. What is the difference between being a child of God and being a friend of God?

  2. How did Abraham, Moses, and the disciples become friends of God?

  3. What privileges do friends of God enjoy?

  4. How can you live out your friendship with God through constant communication?

Application / Challenge

  1. Personal Reflection:Elevate your relationship with God from simply being a child to becoming His friend.

  2. Faith Response:Practice constant communication with Him through daily prayer and listening.

  3. Action Step:Show faithful obedience to God’s commands as the mark of true friendship.

Conclusion

Being called a friend of God is one of the greatest honors. We are not only His children but are also given the privilege of friendship—a relationship built on trust, obedience, and constant communication. Like Abraham, Moses, and the disciples, we too are invited to walk in intimacy with God. May we rise from being children to becoming His true friends, living out this friendship in our daily walk of obedience and communion with Him.
