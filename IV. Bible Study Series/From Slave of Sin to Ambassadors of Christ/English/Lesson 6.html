“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Series Title:
From Slave of Sin to Ambassadors of Christ


Lesson 6: The Highest Trust – Becoming an Ambassador of God


Series Recap

In our journey from Lesson 1 to Lesson 5, we have seen the progressive growth of a person who belongs to the Kingdom of God. Each stage is both a privilege and an honor given by God, and with each privilege comes responsibility.

  • Lesson 1: Saved by God
    From slavery to sin, we were redeemed and set free by the blood of Christ. The first step in our journey into the Kingdom is salvation.

  • Lesson 2: Becoming a Servant of God
    From freedom, we are called to become servants of righteousness—a mark of complete surrender to the will of God.

  • Lesson 3: Becoming a Child of God
    Faithful servants are lifted up and called children, made heirs of the Kingdom.

  • Lesson 4: Becoming a Friend of God
    Not all children become friends. Friends are those who draw near to God, converse with Him, and understand His will.

  • Lesson 5: Becoming a Co-Worker of God
    A true friend shares in God’s work. In the vineyard of the Lord, some plant, some water, but it is God who gives the growth. The reward is promised to every faithful worker.

Now, in Lesson 6, we arrive at the highest level of trust—becoming an Ambassador of God.

This is the fullness of our calling: that in our lives we truly represent God and His Kingdom before all people.

This is not a role granted by men, but a divine appointment from God Himself.

And it is the greatest honor that our Father can entrust to us as His children.

Introduction

From slavery to sin, we were set free to become servants of God, called as children, counted as friends, and made co-workers in His vineyard. Yet the calling does not end there. The highest level of God’s trust is to become His ambassador on earth.

Being an ambassador of God is not attained through education, skill, or social position. It is a direct appointment from God Himself. It is a sign of His complete trust in a person whose whole life reflects the Kingdom of Heaven, so that through them the world may know who God truly is.

Teaching Points

  1. What is an Ambassador of God?

    • A representative of the Kingdom of God here on earth (2 Corinthians 5:20).

    • A bearer of God’s message of reconciliation to humanity.

    • A life that becomes a living image of the God he represents.


  2. Being an Ambassador: The Highest Trust

    • Not every child of God automatically becomes an ambassador.

    • This honor is the fruit of a life lived in humility, faithfulness, holiness, and steadfast service as a co-worker.

    • Our representation is tested in every area of life: in words, actions, and character, people must see God in us.


  3. The Responsibility of an Ambassador

    • To live with integrity and holiness, leaving no room for reproach against the God we represent.

    • To carry the message of salvation and proclaim the Good News.

    • To bring God’s will on earth as it is in heaven (Matthew 6:10).


  4. Contrast: The Failure of the World

    • As the line in the Father’s Love Letter says: “I was being misrepresented by those people who don’t know me.”

    • Misrepresentation of God causes others to stumble.

    • That is why it is crucial for ambassadors to live rightly, so that the true nature of God—holy, loving, and faithful—may be revealed.


Scriptural Basis

  • 2 Corinthians 5:20 - “We are therefore Christ’s ambassadors, as though God were making His appeal through us. We implore you on Christ’s behalf: Be reconciled to God.”

  • Matthew 5:16 - “In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven.”

Discussion Questions

  1. How is being an ambassador of God different from simply being His child or servant?

  2. How can we ensure that we do not misrepresent God in our daily lives?

  3. What is the greatest challenge for you as God’s representative in your family, workplace, or community?

Application / Challenge

  1. Personal Reflection:In what areas are you being a faithful representative of God, and where do you need to change?

  2. Faith Response:Begin living each day with the awareness that every word, action, and decision represents God.

  3. Action Step:Set a goal this week to share a personal testimony with someone about God’s goodness.

Conclusion

Becoming an ambassador of God is the highest level of trust that He can place upon a person. It is not a title given by men, but a divine appointment from Heaven.

In the life of an ambassador, nothing is seen but the God they represent—a God who is holy, loving, and righteous.

Let us, therefore, embrace this great calling. Let us not allow our weaknesses to misrepresent Him, but instead let our lives be mirrors of the Kingdom of Heaven here on earth, until the day of His return.
