“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Series Title:
From Slave of Sin to Ambassadors of Christ


Lesson 1: A Slave of Sin and the Salvation God Offers


Introduction

All people are born into sin. Because of Adam and Eve’s disobedience, the whole of humanity fell under the bondage of sin and death. No one can save themselves, no matter how hard they try. But the good news is this: God has provided salvation through Jesus Christ. He alone can set us free from sin and bring us true freedom.

Teaching Points

  1. The Bondage of Sin

    • Humanity is enslaved by sin (John 8:34; Romans 3:23).

    • The result: shameful deeds, slavery to evil desires, and death as its wage (Romans 6:21, 23a).

    • This slavery to sin is not only seen in actions but is rooted in human nature and thought (Ephesians 2:1–3).

  2. The Salvation God Offers

    • Through Jesus Christ, the penalty of sin was paid by His death and resurrection (Romans 5:8).

    • Salvation is a gift, not a result of human effort (Ephesians 2:8–9).

    • The blood of Christ cleanses and redeems (Ephesians 1:7).

  3. Freedom Through Christ

    • If Christ sets you free, you are truly free (John 8:36).

    • Those who receive Christ are given a new life and nature (2 Corinthians 5:17).

    • This freedom is not just escape from sin but a life lived in righteousness and holiness (Romans 6:22).

Scriptural Basis

  • Romans 3:23 – “For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God.”

  • Romans 6:23 – “For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.”

  • John 8:34, 36 – “Everyone who sins is a slave to sin… So if the Son sets you free, you will be free indeed.”

  • Ephesians 2:8–9 – “For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith—and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God—not by works, so that no one can boast.”

Discussion Questions

  1. What does it mean to be a slave of sin? How do we see this reality in society today?

  2. Why is human effort not enough to save us from sin?

  3. What is the most important thing Jesus did so we could receive salvation?

  4. In your personal experience, how have you seen the freedom that Christ brings?

Application / Challenge

  1. Personal Reflection: Identify areas in your life where you still feel the chains of sin.

  2. Faith Response: Remember that salvation is not by works but by faith in Christ.

  3. Action Step: Live out your freedom by turning away from sin and choosing to obey God this week.

Conclusion

Sin is a chain that leads to death, but God did not leave us in that condition. Through Jesus Christ, He offers salvation—a gift of grace that brings forgiveness, freedom, and eternal life. The first step toward becoming a child, a friend, and ultimately an ambassador of God is to receive Christ as Savior.
