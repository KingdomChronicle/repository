“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Series Title:
From Slave of Sin to Ambassadors of Christ (Bonus and Culminating Lesson)


Lesson 7: A Royal Priesthood – God’s Holy Nation


Introduction

This entire series has taken us on a journey: from being slaves of sin to becoming children, friends, co-workers, and ambassadors of God. Yet all of these stages lead to one ultimate destination—our true identity as a royal priesthood. This is the fulfillment of God’s purpose: that His people would not only be saved, but also reign and serve alongside Him as His Kingdom here on earth.

Teaching Points

  1. Chosen and Appointed by God

    • “But you are a chosen people, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, God’s special possession, that you may declare the praises of him who called you out of darkness into his wonderful light.” — 1 Peter 2:9

    • Our calling is not accidental; we were chosen even before the foundation of the world.

    • To be a royal priesthood means we are both kings and priests—vested with authority and entrusted with holy service.


  2. Kings: Reigning with Christ

    • “You have made them to be a kingdom and priests to serve our God, and they will reign on the earth.” — Revelation 5:10

    • As kings, Christ has given us authority over sin and the power of the enemy.

    • We are called to govern—not by human strength, but by the righteousness of God’s Kingdom.


  3. Priests: Mediating and Serving

    • “You will be for me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation.” — Exodus 19:6

    • The role of a priest is to stand between God and man—to offer, to pray, and to intercede.

    • Under the New Covenant, we no longer offer the blood of animals, but present ourselves as living sacrifices.

      • Romans 12:1 esv - I appeal to you therefore, brothers,[a] by the mercies of God, to present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to God, which is your spiritual worship.[b]


  4. United in the Kingdom of God

    • Each level we have studied (servant → child → friend → co-worker → ambassador) joins together to form the living picture of God’s Kingdom on earth.

    • Being a royal priesthood is not just an individual identity, but a collective calling as the people of God.

    • Through this, we see that God’s plan is not merely human salvation, but the establishment of His Kingdom on earth.


Purpose: Declaring God’s Glory

  • The royal priesthood’s mission is to proclaim the mighty works of God.

  • The Kingdom becomes visible on earth when God’s people live in righteousness, holiness, and faithful witness to Christ.

Discussion Questions

  1. What does it mean to be part of a royal priesthood?

  2. How do kings and priests demonstrate their roles in the Kingdom of God today?

  3. Why is it important for all levels of spiritual growth (servant → child → friend → co-worker → ambassador) to culminate in a royal priesthood?

  4. How can your life actively declare God’s glory this week?

Application / Challenge

  1. Personal Reflection:Embrace your true identity: you are not only saved, not only a child or friend—you are part of a royal priesthood.

  2. Faith Response:Live with authority as a king and with the heart of service as a priest.

  3. Action Step:Let your life demonstrate the Kingdom of God here on earth, until the return of Christ.

Conclusion

Everything we have studied points to one great truth:

God’s plan is not merely individual salvation, but the raising of a holy people—a kingdom of kings and priests.

Together, as faithful servants, children, friends, co-workers, and ambassadors, we embody the Kingdom of God on earth.

“Your kingdom come, your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.” — Matthew 6:10
