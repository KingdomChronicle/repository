“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Ten Characteristics of the Sealed


  1. Loving Obedience to God’s Commandments

    The sealed obey not out of fear, but out of love for God. True obedience flows from a transformed heart.

    John 14:15 (KJV) – “If ye love me, keep my commandments.”

  2. Deep Prayer Life of Submission

    The sealed live in constant communion with God, yielding their will fully to Him.

    1 Thessalonians 5:17 (KJV) – “Pray without ceasing.”

  3. Transformative Bible Study (Not Just Intellectual)

    They study the Word not to gain arguments, but to be changed into Christ’s likeness.

    John 17:17 (KJV) – “Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth.

  4. Natural, Consistent Witness for Christ

    The sealed cannot remain silent; their life and words consistently testify of Christ.

    Matthew 5:16 (KJV) – “Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven.”

  5. Reform of Health and Temperance

    They treat their body as God’s temple, practicing self-control in all things.

    1 Corinthians 6:19–20 (KJV) – “Your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost… therefore glorify God in your body.”

  6. Victory Over Known Sins Through Christ’s Power

    They do not continue in known sin, but live in the overcoming power of Christ.

    Romans 6:14 (KJV) – “For sin shall not have dominion over you.”

  7. Genuine Love for All People

    They love even their enemies, reflecting the love of Christ.

    John 13:35 (KJV) – “By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another.”

  8. Perseverance Under Persecution

    They remain faithful even when suffering, trials, or threats arise.

    Matthew 24:13 (KJV) – “But he that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved.”

  9. Daily Seeking the Holy Spirit’s Presence

    The sealed depend on the Spirit for guidance, strength, and holiness every day.

    Luke 11:13 (KJV) – “How much more shall your heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to them that ask him?”

  10. Hopeful Readiness for Christ’s Return

    The sealed live with constant expectation of Jesus’ coming, keeping their lives pure and focused on eternity.

    1 John 3:2–3 (KJV) – “When he shall appear, we shall be like him… And every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure.”


Discussion / Questions


  1. Loving Obedience :
    How is loving obedience different from legalistic obedience, and why is love the true foundation of keeping God’s commandments?

  2. Deep Prayer & Bible Study
    In what ways can a life of prayer and transformative Bible study change our character, rather than just giving us more knowledge?

  3. Witness & Health Reform
    How do our daily habits (speech, actions, health choices) serve as a consistent witness for Christ to those around us?

  4. Victory, Love, and Perseverance
    What practical steps help us overcome sin through Christ’s power, love our enemies, and remain steadfast under trials?

  5. Spirit-filled Readiness
    Why is daily dependence on the Holy Spirit essential for preparing to meet Christ at His return?
