“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus.

Revelation 22:11 (KJV)

He that is unjust, let him be unjust still… and he that is righteous, let him be righteous still…

And grieve not the Holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.


Explanation / Expansion


  • The sealing of God is one of the most decisive events in the final chapters of earth’s history.

  • Scripture reveals that before the Second Coming of the Lord, God marks His faithful people with His seal, showing their loyalty and obedience.

  • The sealing is directly tied to the last great test of worship.

  • A counterfeit system will enforce its mark upon humanity, requiring obedience to human authority in opposition to God’s law. In this crisis, every person will be compelled to choose: follow God or follow man.

  • God’s true people will be sealed in their foreheads, symbolizing a settled conviction in the mind and heart to obey Him fully.

  • From that point forward, destinies will be fixed: the righteous sealed for redemption, the unrighteous sealed for destruction.

  • The urgency is clear: now is the time to prepare. Today’s choices are shaping character, loyalty, and destiny.

  • The sealing will not be a sudden surprise, but the culmination of daily decisions of faithfulness or compromise.

Key Points


  1. Final Test of Worship – The sealing is linked to the end-time crisis when enforced worship challenges loyalty to God.

  2. True Faithfulness – God’s seal is placed on those who obey Him with conviction and endurance.

  3. Counterfeit Mark – The mark of the beast represents loyalty to human authority above God’s law.

  4. Close of Probation – Once the sealing is finished, destinies are fixed forever.

  5. Urgency of Preparation – Every daily decision now is preparing us to be either sealed by God or marked by the enemy.

Discussion / Questions


  1. What does Revelation 14:12 teach us about the qualities of those who will be sealed by God in the final crisis?

  2. Why is the sealing described as a “settled conviction in the mind and heart”? How does this differ from outward religion only?

  3. According to Ephesians 4:30, how is the Holy Spirit connected to the sealing of God’s people?

  4. Revelation 22:11 speaks of a time when destinies are fixed. What does this reveal about the urgency of preparing before probation closes?

  5. In light of the “final test of worship,” what daily choices can we make now to ensure that our loyalty is firmly on God’s side and not with the counterfeit system?
