“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

The Sealing is a Definite Event


Bible Verse(Foundation)

Revelation 7:2–3 (KJV)

And I saw another angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God: and he cried with a loud voice to the four angels, to whom it was given to hurt the earth and the sea, Saying, Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads.

Revelation 13:16–17 (KJV)

“And he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads: And that no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name.”


Explanation / Expansion


  • The sealing is not a slow, symbolic process unfolding over long church history. While God continually works in preparing His people, the final sealing is a definite, decisive event that takes place just before the close of probation and before God’s judgments are released upon the earth.

  • Revelation 7 shows an angel restraining the winds of destruction until the servants of God are sealed. This demonstrates God’s mercy and urgency—He ensures His people are marked with His seal of protection before the great crisis breaks forth.

  • At that critical moment, all humanity will be divided into two distinct groups:
    • Those who receive the Seal of the Living God—showing God’s ownership, loyalty, and protection.
    • Those who receive the Mark of the Beast—showing allegiance to human authority in opposition to God’s law.

  • This sealing represents a settling into the truth—both intellectually and spiritually—so that God’s people cannot be moved, even in the face of persecution.

  • It is the final preparation before Christ’s coming.

Key Points


  1. The Sealing is Urgent – It must happen before God’s judgments are released.

  2. Clear Division – Every person will either receive God’s seal or the mark of the beast.

  3. Not Symbolic Growth – The sealing is a final, end-time act, not a gradual church process.

  4. Protection & Ownership – God marks His true servants as His own.

  5. Character Stability – The sealing is about being firmly grounded in God’s truth, unmoved by deception.

Discussion / Questions


  1. According to Revelation 7:2–3, why must God’s people be sealed before the winds are released?

  2. What does the “seal of the living God” represent in a believer’s life?

  3. How is the Mark of the Beast described in Revelation 13:16–17, and what does it signify?

  4. Why is the sealing described as a decisive event rather than a slow, historical process?

  5. How can we prepare ourselves today to be settled into God’s truth and ready for the sealing?
