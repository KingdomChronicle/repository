“Good [morning/afternoon/evening], everyone! Welcome to our weekly Bible Study. This is a time we set apart each week to strengthen the foundation of our faith in the Word of God.

The Bible reminds us in 2 Timothy 3:16–17 that ‘All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness; that the man of God may be perfect, thoroughly furnished unto all good works.’

Our goal in this Bible Study is not only to gain knowledge, but to allow God’s Word to transform our lives, shape our character, and draw us closer to Christ.

As we meet each week, we will grow together in understanding, encourage one another in faith, and prepare ourselves to stand firm in the truth of God’s Kingdom.

Let us approach this time with open hearts, ready to listen, to learn, and to apply His Word in our daily walk.”

Bible Study Facilitator’s Instructions

More...
  1. Opening Prayer
    • Begin the session by leading in prayer.
    • Ask for the Holy Spirit’s guidance, wisdom, and open hearts as you study God’s Word.
  2. Fellowship Check-in
    • Greet the group warmly.
    • Ask each participant how they are and briefly recall what they learned from the previous lesson.
    • Encourage them to share short reflections or personal applications.
  3. Reading of the Foundation Verses
    • Assign participants to read the Bible passages aloud, one by one.
    • Example: “Let’s have [Name] read Matthew 24:14, then [Name] read Mark 13:10…”
    • Make sure everyone follows along in their Bibles.
  4. Expansion and Explanation of the Verses
    • After the verses are read, explain the meaning of the passage.
    • Highlight the context, the message, and how it connects to God’s plan and the end times.
    • Allow space for participants to share their initial thoughts or insights.
  5. Explaining the Key Points
    • Present the prepared key points one by one.
    • Relate each point back to the verses that were read.
    • Emphasize the lessons that are practical and applicable to daily Christian living.
  6. Discussion/ Questions
    • Read each question slowly and clearly.
    • Allow participants to respond freely—listen actively and encourage open sharing.
    • Guide the discussion so it remains rooted in the Word of God.
    • Example: “According to Matthew 24:14, what must happen before the end will come?”
  7. Closing Prayer
    • Summarize the lesson briefly, reminding participants of the main message.
    • Invite prayer requests from the group.
    • Close with a prayer, asking God to help everyone apply the message in their lives and to be faithful in sharing the Gospel.

Total Consecration of Life, Time, and Resources to God


Bible Verse(Foundation)

Matthew 6:33 (KJV)

But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you.

1 Corinthians 6:19–20 (KJV)

What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God’s.

Romans 12:1 (KJV)

Present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service.


Explanation / Expansion


  • Total consecration means surrendering everything—our bodies, time, talents, possessions, ambitions, and plans—to God as an act of worship.

  • The apostle Paul calls this a “living sacrifice”: not a one-time ritual, but a daily, ongoing offering of ourselves so that God’s will shapes our decisions and priorities.

  • Consecration transforms how we:
    • Use time → prioritizing prayer, Scripture, service, and fellowship.
    • Serve → offering gifts and talents to build up others instead of seeking self-glory.
    • Serve → offering gifts and talents to build up others instead of seeking self-glory.
    • Pursue goals → seeking God’s Kingdom first, above personal gain or comfort.

  • A consecrated life is marked by practical choices: saying “no” to compromises, conveniences, or pleasures that dishonor God, and saying “yes” to costly obedience, service, and generosity.

  • True consecration is not legalism—it is the overflow of love and gratitude to Christ.

  • We consecrate because we were bought with a price.

  • This response to grace yields transformed character, fruitful service, and readiness for the coming Kingdom.

Key Points


  1. Whole-Life Worship — Consecration is not limited to church activity; it is offering every area of life to God.

  2. Daily, Not Occasional — A “living sacrifice” is an ongoing surrender, renewed each day.

  3. Time as Treasure — Our priorities and use of time reveal the state of our heart.

  4. Stewardship of Resources — Money, possessions, and opportunities are entrusted to us for God’s work.

  5. Gifts for Service — Skills and abilities are meant to build others and glorify God.

  6. Costly Obedience — Real consecration often requires sacrifice, courage, and choices that align with God’s will.

  7. Fruit of the Spirit — Consecration produces Christlike character, practical love, and Kingdom fruitfulness.

Discussion / Questions


  1. What does it mean practically for you to “present your body a living sacrifice” this week? Name one concrete change you can make.

  2. How do you currently spend most of your time, and how does that reflect your consecration to God?

  3. In what areas of life (money, relationships, career, leisure) do you find it hardest to surrender fully to God? Why?

  4. How can our group encourage one another toward greater consecration of time, talents, and resources?

  5. Share an example (from Scripture or personal experience) where total consecration led to visible blessing or transformation.
