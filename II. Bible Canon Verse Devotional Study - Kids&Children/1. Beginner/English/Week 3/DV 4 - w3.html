Instruction on how to successfully kickstart your Daily Devotional Lifestyle with the Lord.

  • Start your Daily Devotion by praying sincerely to God, asking Him for understanding and guidance.
  • If you have your own Bible, open it to the verse; otherwise, read the verse from this page.
  • Next, read the devotional verse aloud three times so the enemy can hear God's Word from your lips.
  • Then, read the reflection section multiple times if needed, until you truly understand its message.
  • Then, end your devotion with prayer having a thankful heart.

Bible Verse of the Day

Acts 3:19

ESV (English Standard Version)

19 Repent therefore, and turn back, that your sins may be blotted out,


GUIDE REFLECTION:


The Story of Salvation (God’s Redemptive Plan)

Repentance and Faith: Turning to God with All Your Heart

Have you ever made a mistake or done something you knew was wrong? Maybe you told a lie, disobeyed your parents, or hurt someone’s feelings. We all mess up sometimes. But the good news is that God gives us a way to start fresh. That’s what repentance and faith are all about!

In Acts 3:19, the Bible says, “Repent, then, and turn to God, so that your sins may be wiped out, that times of refreshing may come from the Lord.”

Repentance means turning away from sin and choosing to follow God instead. Imagine you're walking the wrong way down a path, and someone says, “Hey! Turn around! That’s not the right direction!” Repentance is like turning around and heading the right way—with Jesus leading the way.

When we repent, we tell God we’re sorry, and we truly mean it. We don’t just say sorry—we change our hearts and actions.

But that’s only part of it. The second part is faith. Faith means trusting in Jesus and believing He is the Son of God who died for your sins and rose again. When you have faith, you believe that Jesus can forgive you and help you live a brand-new life with Him.

Repentance and faith go hand in hand. When we turn away from sin (repentance) and turn toward Jesus (faith), something amazing happens—God forgives us completely, and He gives us peace, joy, and a fresh start.

So if you’ve ever felt guilty, ashamed, or far from God, remember this: Jesus is always ready to forgive you. All you have to do is turn to Him with repentance and faith.
