The Story of Salvation (God’s Redemptive Plan)

The Holy Spirit’s Role: Growing Good Fruit in Us

Have you ever seen a tree full of delicious fruit like apples, oranges, or mangoes? Fruit is the result of a healthy tree that’s been watered, cared for, and given sunlight. In the Bible, God tells us that our lives can grow fruit too—not the kind you eat, but special “fruit” that shows how we are becoming more like Jesus. This is called the fruit of the Spirit!

In Galatians 5:22-23, the Apostle Paul tells us what this fruit looks like: “But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control.”

These aren’t things we can grow on our own. They come from the Holy Spirit, who lives in us when we believe in Jesus. The Holy Spirit is like the sunshine and water that help us grow strong in our faith. He helps us love others, stay joyful, be patient, and do what’s right—even when it’s hard.

Let’s break them down a bit:

  • Love: Caring for others, even when it’s not easy.

  • Joy: Being happy in God, even when things aren’t perfect.

  • Peace: Trusting God and staying calm instead of worrying.

  • Patience: Waiting without getting angry or upset.

  • Kindness: Being nice and helping others.

  • Goodness: Doing what’s right and making good choices.

  • Faithfulness: Being loyal and keeping promises.

  • Gentleness: Being careful with our words and actions.

  • Self-control: Saying “no” to things we know are wrong.

Each day, the Holy Spirit helps us grow in these ways. We may not be perfect, but just like fruit takes time to grow, so do we. All we need to do is stay close to God—reading His Word, praying, and listening to the Holy Spirit.

So next time you eat a piece of fruit, remember the fruit of the Spirit. Ask God to help you grow more loving, joyful, patient, and kind. With the Holy Spirit’s help, your life can be a beautiful tree full of godly fruit that blesses everyone around you!
