Instruction on how to successfully kickstart your Daily Devotional Lifestyle with the Lord.

  • Start your Daily Devotion by praying sincerely to God, asking Him for understanding and guidance.
  • If you have your own Bible, open it to the verse; otherwise, read the verse from this page.
  • Next, read the devotional verse aloud three times so the enemy can hear God's Word from your lips.
  • Then, read the reflection section multiple times if needed, until you truly understand its message.
  • Then, end your devotion with prayer having a thankful heart.

GUIDE REFLECTION:


Living for God (Christian Discipleship)

Tell the World About Jesus!

Have you ever had something so exciting happen that you just had to tell someone? Maybe you got a new pet, won a prize, or learned something cool. That’s how it feels when we know Jesus—we want to share the good news with others!

In Matthew 28:19–20, Jesus said,
"Go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age.”

This is called The Great Commission. It’s Jesus’ special mission for all of us: to tell others about Him!

What is the Gospel?

The Gospel is the good news that Jesus came to save us from sin, that He died for us, rose again, and gives us eternal life when we believe in Him. That’s the best news ever—and it’s meant to be shared!

How can kids and young people share the Gospel?

  • Be a good example – Let others see Jesus in how you act and treat people. Be kind, honest, and forgiving.

  • Talk about Jesus – Tell your friends why you believe in Jesus, or invite them to church or Sunday school.

  • Pray for others – Ask God to help your family and friends know Jesus too.

  • Use your talents – Sing songs about Jesus, draw pictures with Bible verses, or write encouraging notes with God’s love.

You don’t have to be a grown-up or a pastor to share the Gospel. Jesus uses kids and teens too! And the best part? Jesus promised in Matthew 28:20 that He is always with us—even when we’re scared or unsure.

So don’t be afraid to share your faith. When you tell others about Jesus, you’re being a light in the world, helping people find hope, love, and eternal life.

Let’s go and tell the world—Jesus loves them!
