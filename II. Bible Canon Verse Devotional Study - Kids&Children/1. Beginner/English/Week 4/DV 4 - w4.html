Instruction on how to successfully kickstart your Daily Devotional Lifestyle with the Lord.

  • Start your Daily Devotion by praying sincerely to God, asking Him for understanding and guidance.
  • If you have your own Bible, open it to the verse; otherwise, read the verse from this page.

  • Then, read the reflection section multiple times if needed, until you truly understand its message.
  • Then, end your devotion with prayer having a thankful heart.

Bible Verse of the Day

1 Corinthians 10:13

ESV (English Standard Version)

13 No temptation has overtaken you that is not common to man. God is faithful, and he will not let you be tempted beyond your ability, but with the temptation he will also provide the way of escape, that you may be able to endure it.


GUIDE REFLECTION:


Living for God (Christian Discipleship)

You Can Say No to Temptation!

Have you ever been tempted to do something you knew was wrong? Maybe you wanted to lie to stay out of trouble, copy someone’s homework, or be mean to someone who upset you. That feeling—that pull to do something wrong—is called temptation. Everyone faces it—even kids and grown-ups!

But here’s some good news from the Bible. 1 Corinthians 10:13 says, “God is faithful; He will not let you be tempted beyond what you can bear. But when you are tempted, He will also provide a way out so that you can endure it.”

This verse tells us three important things:

  1. Everyone faces temptation – You are not alone. Even Jesus was tempted when He was on earth!

  2. God knows your limits – He promises not to let temptation be too strong for you to handle.

  3. God gives us a way out – That means there’s always a way to make the right choice, even if it’s hard.

So how can you overcome temptation?

  • Pray and ask God for help – He loves you and wants to help you be strong.

  • Know God’s Word – When Jesus was tempted, He used Scripture to fight back. You can too!

  • Walk away – Sometimes the best thing to do is leave the situation.

  • Talk to someone you trust – A parent, teacher, or friend can help you do the right thing.

Remember, you are never alone in your struggles. God is with you, and He believes in you. With His help, you can be brave, strong, and full of faith—even when temptation comes.

So next time you feel tempted, remember this: You can say no—with God’s help!
